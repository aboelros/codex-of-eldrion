 76. The Ashen Tundra

Description: A vast, frozen wasteland of pulverized rock and "Memory-Ash" located on the northern pole of the 10th Dimension, where the temperature is permanently low enough to freeze time-itself.

---

 Overview
The Ashen Tundra is the ultimate outcome of the 10th Dimension’s entropy. While the City of Dust is actively crumbling, the Tundra is where the dust has settled and been "Frozen" into a state of permanent, icy stagnation. The "Snow" here is not made of water, but of finely ground grey ash the remains of the dimension’s original civilization. The cold is not just physical, but "Temporal" a chill that saps the kinetic energy from everything it touches, slowing movement and thought to a crawl.

The environment in the Tundra is one of absolute, white-and-grey blindness. The wind is a constant, low-pitched moan that carries the abrasive ash across the flat, featureless plains. The sun is visible only as a dim, frozen orb through the thick layers of suspended dust. It is a place of total sensory deprivation and physical endurance, where "Survival" is measured in the minutes between each temporal-chill.

 Geography & Architecture
The Tundra is a landscape of Frozen Plains and Ash-Glaciers.

1. The Bone-Barrens: Flat expanses of grey ash that have been compacted into a surface as hard as concrete. These barrens are littered with the frozen, stone-like remains of the dimension’s original wildlife.
2. The Ash-Glaciers: Gargantuan masses of frozen silt and "Time-Ice" that move across the plains with agonizing slowness, crushing anything in their path.
3. The Frost-Fissures: Deep rifts in the ash-crust that reveal the "Black-Ice" bedrock of the dimension, where the temperature is at its most lethal.

There is no permanent architecture. The Ash-Walkers live in Igloos of Fused-Ash structures made by melting the surface ash into a solid, insulating shell using primitive thermal-kits.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Breath, the Tundra was a series of vibrant, high-altitude steppes. During the Cooling of the 10th, a period triggered by the Warden to "Stabilize the Entropy" of the City of Dust, the dimension’s heat was siphoned away to power the Prismatic Spire. The resulting drop in temperature turned the steppes into the frozen wasteland seen today.

History records the Long March of the Frozen, a story about a group of ten thousand refugees who attempted to cross the tundra to reach the 11th Dimension. Legend says they were caught in a "Sudden-Freeze," and their bodies are still perfectly preserved in the ash, appearing as a "Statatue-Forest" of silent, grey figures.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Ash-Walkers (The Frost-Touched) are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a tough, stoic group of nomads who wear multi-layered furs made from the skin of the dimension’s "Stone-Lizards." They are experts in "Temporal-Insulation" and possess a unique ability to "Sleep while Walking," allowing them to survive the long, freezing nights of the tundra.

The Ice-Golems are the dominant non-humanoid lifeforms. These are automated guards made of fused ash and black-ice, created by the Warden to patrol the northern rifts. They move with a slow, grinding inevitability and are programmed to "Freeze" anything that produces a heat-signature.

The Order of the Frozen Sun is a resistance faction that believes the Tundra contains an "Ancient-Aetheric-Reactor." They spend their lives searching the frost-fissures, hoping to find the "Primal-Flame" that they believe can be used to "Defrost the World" and return the 10th Dimension to its original, warm state.

 Dangers & Hazards
Temporal-Chill is the most constant threat. Periodically, the temperature will drop to levels that physically "Freeze" the movement of atoms. Without "Thermal-Sync" armor, a person will be turned into a solid block of ash-ice in a heartbeat.

Ash-Blindness is a chronic hazard. The abrasive dust in the wind can sear the retinas and permanentely damage the lungs. Travelers must wear "Lead-Goggles" and "Sift-Masks" at all times.

The White-Out is a sensory hazard. The lack of any visual landmarks, combined with the permanent grey haze, can cause a person to lose their sense of direction entirely, eventually wandering in a circle until they die of exhaustion.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the tundra is the Frozen Sun. It is rumored that deep beneath the center of the Bone-Barrens, there is a hidden, obsidian chamber containing a massive, glowing orb of "Captivated Fire." Legend says that this is the Original Sun of the 13th Dimension, and that it was stolen by Gabriel to power the 10th Dimension’s stasis-fields.

Another rumor suggests that the Ashen Tundra is not a wasteland. Some claim it is a "Planetary-Scale Cryo-Vault," and that Gabriel is using the cold to store the "Physical-Bodies" of the entire Eldrion aristocracy, allowing them to remain perfectly preserved while they wait for the "Final Purification" of the universe.
