 77. The Rust Wind Valleys

Description: A series of deep, red-stained canyons where the atmosphere is hyper-saturated with corrosive iron-oxide, causing the rapid disintegration of both biological and metallic matter.

---

 Overview
The Rust Wind Valleys are the physical manifestation of the 10th Dimension’s "Corrosive-Entropy." While the City of Dust is crumbling and the Tundra is frozen, the Valleys are actively "Burning" through oxidation. The air here is a thick, orange-tinted haze of airborne rust particles, and a permanent, high-velocity "Rust-Wind" blows through the canyons, polishing the rock into jagged, red-slicked sculptures.

The environment in the valleys is one of permanent, abrasive motion. The sound is a constant, dry "Sandpaper-Hiss" as the rust-particles collide with the canyon walls. The light of the Apex is filtered through the orange haze, creating a world of bruised copper and blood-red shadows. It is a place of absolute material decay, where even the most advanced alloys from Dimension 2 will begin to pit and flake within minutes of exposure.

 Geography & Architecture
The Valleys are a landscape of Oxidized Gorges and Iron-Dunes.

1. The Crimson-Chasms: Deep, narrow valleys where the rust-wind is most concentrated. The walls are composed of "Iron-Stone," a mineral that grows new layers of rust as quickly as the wind can strip them away.
2. The Rust-Slicks: Expanses of fine, red powder that behave like quicksand. Without specialized "Traction-Boots," a traveler will sink into the rust and be "Asphyxiated" by the fine particles.
3. The Metal-Glades: Forests of "Iron-Wait-Trees" prehistoric metallic structures that have been rusted into fantastic, skeletal shapes.

Architecture is "Organic-Plastic" dwellings made of non-reactive ceramics and "Synthetic-Leather" that are resistant to oxidation, often built into the leeward sides of the canyons to avoid the direct force of the rust-wind.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Breath, the valleys were the dimension’s primary iron-mines. During the Oxidation of History, a period triggered by the Warden to "Destroy the Records of the Past," a massive cloud of corrosive gas was released into the atmosphere. The gas reacted with the dimension’s iron-rich crust, creating the permanent "Rust-Cycle" seen today.

History records the Siege of the Iron-Crown, a story about the last king of the 10th Dimension who barricaded himself in his palace made of pure steel. The Warden redirected the rust-winds onto the palace for three days. By the fourth morning, the palace and the king had been turned into a single, massive pile of red dust.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Rust-Nomads (The Red-Skinned) are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They wear multi-layered suits of treated leather and ceramic plating. Their skin has been stained a permanent, deep orange by the rust, and they possess a unique ability to "Breathe the Dust," their lungs having adapted to filter out the most corrosive particles.

The Rust-Eaters are the dominant wildlife. These are small, multi-legged crustaceans that feed on the iron-oxide. They move in massive swarms, appearing like a "Living-Wave" of red armor that can strip a metallic vehicle to its frame in minutes.

The Order of the Heart-of-Iron is a resistance faction that believes the Valleys contains a "Non-Oxidizing Core." They spend their lives searching the deepest chasms for a legendary, pure-iron pillar that they believe is the "Soul of the 10th Dimension." Legend says that anyone who touches the core will be granted "Immortality of Form."

 Dangers & Hazards
Rust-Lung is the primary medical threat. Inhaling the iron-oxide causes the lungs to "Solidify," leading to a permanent, wracking cough and eventually total respiratory failure. Most nomads use "Ceramic-Filters" that must be cleaned every hour.

Rapid-Equipment-Failure is a constant physical hazard. Even the most hardened ceramics will eventually "Pit" under the constant bombardment of the rust-wind. A traveler whose vehicle fails in the middle of a valley is effectively dead, as the walk to the nearest settlement is almost always too long for their filters to last.

The Orange-Out is a sensory hazard. The intensity of the rust-storms can create a total loss of visibility. The "Magnetic-Static" generated by the rust-particles also interferes with scanners and compasses, making navigation a matter of memory and "Wind-Sense."

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the valleys is the Heart of Iron. It is rumored that deep beneath the center of the Crimson-Chasms, there is a hidden, obsidian vault containing a single, massive pillar of "Divine-Platinum." Legend says that this pillar is the "Gravity-Anchor" of the 10th Dimension, and that if it ever rusts, the entire dimension will disintegrate into the Astral Sea.

Another rumor suggests that the Rust Wind Valleys are not a natural formation. Some claim they are a "Planetary-Scale Grinder," and that Gabriel is using the rust-winds to "Pulverize the Sins" of the 9th Dimension, turning the "Imperfect-Memories" into the red dust that eventually settles in the City of Dust.
