 78. The Erosion_Cliffs

Description: The precarious, terminal boundary of the 10th Dimension, where the realm’s stable crust gives way to a miles-high precipice that is slowly but perpetually crumbling into the absolute void of the Astral Sea.

---

 Overview
The Erosion Cliffs are the literal end of the world for the 10th Dimension. Situated on the absolute edge of the dimension’s "Gravitational-Plate," these cliffs represent the physical limit of Gabriel’s orderly creation. The "Rock" here is a fragile, multi-layered suspension of shale, obsidian, and "Time-Silt" that is under constant structural stress from the dimension’s rotational forces. Every hour, massive sections of the cliff-face sometimes the size of entire cities "Slough-Off" and fall into the shimmering, purple abyss of the void below.

The environment at the cliffs is one of dizzying perspective and permanent instability. The sound is a constant, low-pitched rumble of falling stone and the high-pitched "Whistling" of the Astral Sea’s winds. The light of the Apex is distorted by the spatial rifts at the edge, creating a world of "Stretching-Shadows" and prismatic horizons. It is a place of absolute vertigo, where the only constant is the knowledge that the ground beneath your feet is slowly disappearing.

 Geography & Architecture
The Cliffs are a landscape of Failing Precipices and Void-Vents.

1. The Terminal-Ledge: The absolute edge of the dimension, where the rock is thinnest and the gravity is weakest. It is a world of jagged, overhanging cliffs that "Groan" with every shift of the dimension.
2. The Falling-Stacks: Massive pillars of rock that have been partially separated from the main cliff but have not yet fallen, appearing as "Floating-Islands" for those brave enough to reach them.
3. The Rift-Canyons: Deep fissures that cut back into the dimension’s interior, acting as "Drainage-Basins" for the dust and debris that isn't immediately claimed by the void.

Architecture is "Extreme-Hanging" dwellings carved into the cliff-face and reinforced with massive iron bolts. The inhabitants use "Vine-Ladders" and "Cable-Slings" to move between the unstable tiers. Every building is equipped with a "Quick-Release" mechanism in case the section of cliff it is attached to begins to collapse.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Breath, the cliffs were the dimension’s primary "Starlight-Gardens." During the Boundary Failure, a period caused by the Warden’s over-extraction of time-energy, the dimension’s gravitational anchors began to fail, leading to the permanent erosion seen today.

History records the Massacre of the Last Step, a story about a group of fifty thousand people who attempted to "Hold the World Together" by building a massive stone buttress at the edge of the cliffs. The Warden, Jael, refused to provide the necessary aetheric-iron, and the entire structure collapsed into the void, taking the builders with it. Legend says that the "Light of their Souls" can still be seen as a faint, white glow at the base of the cliffs during the "New-Moon."

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Cliff-Dwellers (The Void-Watchers) are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a group of the dispossessed who have chosen to live on the edge rather than in the entropy of the city. They are master "Climbers" and possess a unique "Sense of Fall" a preternatural ability to know exactly when a section of rock is about to collapse.

The Void-Harpies are the dominant non-humanoid lifeforms. These are semi-organic entities made of leather and dark-glass. They haunt the rifts of the cliffs and feed on the "Cohesion" of physical objects, pushing travelers and debris off the edge to "Join the Void."

The Order of the World-Edge is a resistance faction that believes the Cliffs are a "Natural-Exit." They spend their lives at the absolute edge, building specialized "Gliders" and "Drogue-Chutes," hoping to find a specific "Air-Current" that will carry them through the void and safely into the 13th Dimension (The Primordial Wilds).

 Dangers & Hazards
Structural-Collapse is the most constant threat. The rock is fundamentally unstable. A single vibration from a thunder-cycle or a minor time-shift can cause a catastrophic "Slumping" of the cliff-face, killing everyone in the district.

The Abyssal-Pull is a physical hazard. Near the edge, the gravity of the 10th Dimension interacts with the "Non-Gravity" of the void, creating unpredictable "Pull-Zones." A person can be suddenly snatched from a ledge and "Sucked" into the abyss if they are not tethered.

Vertigo-Madness is a psychological hazard. The infinite downward perspective, combined with the constant sound of falling stone, can cause a state of "Terminal-Apathy," where a person simply gives up on the struggle to stay on the rock and steps off the edge.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the cliffs is the World-Edge. It is rumored that at a specific, hidden section of the precipice, the "Mist" is thin enough that one can look down and see the Physical Foundations of the 1st through 9th Dimensions. Legend says that Eldrion is built like a "Tower of Glass," and that the 10th Dimension is merely the topmost, and most fragile, layer.

Another rumor suggests that the Erosion Cliffs are not natural. Some claim they are a "Planetary-Scale Disposal-Chant," and that Gabriel is using the erosion to physically "Delete" the parts of Eldrion that are no longer useful to him, with the "Void" being a massive, multi-dimensional trash-can.
