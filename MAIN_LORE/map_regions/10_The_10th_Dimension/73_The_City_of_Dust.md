 73. The City of Dust

Description: A sprawling, skeletal ruin of a once-great metropolis that is in a state of permanent, accelerated entropy, where gravity and time combine to turn every structure into a fine, grey powder.

---

 Overview
The City of Dust (formerly known as "Eldrior") is the 10th Dimension’s graveyard of aspiration. While the Petrified Forest preserves the world in stone, the City is a place of absolute disintegration. Located in a "Time-Vortex" where the entropy is ten times the dimension’s norm, the city is slowly but surely being ground into "Memory-Silt" a fine, grey dust that carries the faint, psychic echoes of the people and things it once was.

The environment in the city is one of permanent, grey twilight and choking haze. The sound of the world is a constant, dry "Hissing" as walls crumble and dunes of dust shift in the hot, thermal winds. Visibility is rarely more than a few dozen feet, and the sky is obscured by a permanent cloud of pulverized concrete and iron. It is a place of profound loss, where "Permanence" is a foreign concept and where every step you take is a contribution to the world's destruction.

 Geography & Architecture
The City is a landscape of Crumbling Verticals and Silt-Canals.

1. The Bone-Towers: The remaining internal skeletons of the city’s skyscrapers, appearing like jagged black teeth reaching through the dust-haze.
2. The Ash-Rivers: Wide, slow-moving currents of dust that flow through the city’s original streets, behaving more like water than sand.
3. The Sifter-Flats: Large, open areas where the dust has settled into perfectly flat plains, concealing the remains of the city’s lower districts.

Architecture is "Entropy-Industrial" structures built from "Self-Healing Slag" (a material from Dimension 2) that can temporarily resist the erosion, often anchored deep into the dimension’s bedrock to prevent them from "Drifting" away in the dust-storms.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Breath, Eldrior was the administrative capital of the 10th Dimension. During the Erosion of Memory, a catastrophic failure in the dimension’s "Chrono-Stabilizers" caused a localized collapse of the time-stream. Instead of stopping, time began to "Fray" at the edges, turning the city into the entropic nightmare it is today.

History records the Massacre of the Last Archive, a period when the city’s scholars attempted to "Freeze" their books in stasis-fields. The Warden, Jael, ordered the fields to be "Inverted," causing the books and the scholars to instantly disintegrate into the very first dunes of the Dust-Bowl. Legend says that the "Voice of the City" can still be heard during the strongest storms, screaming from the dust.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Sifters are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a group of the dispossessed who have built their lives around "Dust-Mining" finding valuable artifacts and machine-parts in the silt-canals. They wear "Sift-Suits" made of multi-layered silk and lead-wire to protect themselves from the abrasive dust.

The Dust-Wraiths (The Half-Fallen) are the dominant non-humanoid lifeforms. These are individuals who have been "Eroded" by the time-vortex to the point where they are semi-transparent and made mostly of ash. They are invisible in the haze and feed on the "Cohesion" of physical objects, causing buildings to collapse with a single touch.

The Order of the White Shell is a resistance faction that believes the city is "The True Body of Eldrion." They spend their lives collecting specific "Psycho-Reactive" dust, hoping to find a way to "Re-Compile" the city’s original state using the concentrated memories of the silt.

 Dangers & Hazards
Ash-Suffocation is the most constant threat. The dust is so fine that it bypasses standard filters. A person who loses their "Seal-Mask" will have their lungs coated in a layer of dry ash within minutes, leading to a slow and agonizing death.

Structural-Dissolution is a physical hazard. The ground in the city is extremely unstable. A single vibration or thermal shift can cause a "Silt-Avalanche" that can bury an entire nomadic camp under hundreds of tons of dust.

Chrono-Drift is a psychological hazard. The "Memory-Silt" carries the psychic echoes of the past. Travelers often report experiencing vivid, terrifying hallucinations of the city’s original inhabitants, eventually losing their own sense of self as they "Drown" in the memories of others.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the city is the Library of Whispers. It is rumored that deep beneath the center of the Sifter-Flats, there is a vault where the resistance has built a "Pressure-Chamber" made of obsidian. Legend says that this chamber contains the "Physical-Soul of Eldrior," and that as long as the soul is protected, the city can never truly disappear.

Another rumor suggests that the City of Dust is not an accident. Some claim it is a "Planetary-Scale Hard-Drive," and that Gabriel is using the "Entropy" of the 10th Dimension to store the "Discarded-Data" of the entire Eldrion population, with each grain of dust representing a single, deleted memory.
