 19. Oakhaven Singularity

Description: A metaphysical anomaly where a fragment of the 1st Dimension’s ancient forest is being slowly consumed by a gravitational rift.

---

 Overview
Oakhaven Singularity is one of the most haunting and scientifically significant locations in the 2nd Dimension. It is a "Reality-Sore" a point where the boundary between the physical realm and the void has been worn thin by the weight of the industrial processing above. At the center of a mile-wide crater lies a swirling, vertical vortex of gravitational energy. Suspended within this vortex are the remains of Oakhaven, a legendary grove of trees from the pre-Apex era.

The Singularity is a place of suspended catastrophe. Trees, rocks, and ancient wooden structures are caught in an eternal spiral, frozen in the act of being pulled into a black, central point of infinite density. It is silent, save for the low, rhythmic thrumming of the rift and the occasional "cracking" sound of reality adjusting itself to the distortion.

 Geography & Architecture
The geography is entirely non-Euclidean. Up and down have no meaning within a half-mile of the rift.

1. The Event Horizon: A shimmering, translucent sphere that surrounds the vortex. Crossing this line means entering a zone where time moves at a fraction of its normal speed.
2. The Swirling Grove: The primary visual feature a forest of massive, petrified oaks that rotate slowly around the central rift. The roots of these trees are tangled with "Void-Tendrils," appearing like black, liquid smoke.
3. The Floating Archives: The remains of an ancient druidic library, its stone walls shattered and its scrolls eternally floating in the air.

There is no modern architecture here, as any iron or steel brought near the singularity is immediately torn apart by the tidal forces. The temporary encampments of researchers are built on the very edge of the crater, anchored to the bedrock with mile-long chains.

 History & Lore
Oakhaven was once the "Seed-Bank" of the 1st Dimension, a place where the druids of old kept the genetic memories of the world. During the Era of the First Warden, an attempt was made to "Sanitize" the grove using a Prototype Aetheric-Bomb. The bomb did not explode in the traditional sense; instead, it created a "Negative Energy Spike" that folded space inward.

The Singularity became a focal point for the Resistance. Legends say that because time moves so slowly within the rift, the druids who were caught in the initial blast are still alive, perpetually reaching for the "Master Seed" that is frozen at the very center of the vortex. If that seed were ever to be retrieved, it could theoretically re-grow the entire 1st Dimension from scratch.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Singularity-Monks are the primary inhabitants. They are a sect of the 11th Dimension’s exile-priests who believe that the rift is a "Doorway to the True God." They live in caves around the crater’s rim, spending their lives staring into the vortex and attempting to decipher the patterns of the swirling debris.

Void-Walkers are highly specialized scouts who use "Phase-Shifting" gear to temporarily ignore the gravitational pull. They are hired by the Warden to retrieve artifacts from the floating ruins, though fewer than half ever return.

There is also a rumor of the Time-Lost, individuals who have crossed the event horizon and are now seen as static, glowing figures within the spiral. They are not dead, but they are "Finished," their timelines having become infinite and stationary.

 Dangers & Hazards
Gravitational Shear is the most immediate physical threat. Within the Singularity, the pull on one’s head may be different from the pull on one’s feet. Without specialized "Anchor-Suits," a human body will be literally pulled apart (a process the monks call "Spaghettification").

Time Dilation is a psychological hazard. A person might spend five minutes attempting to retrieve a scroll and emerge to find that thirty years have passed in the dimension outside. This makes any mission into the rift a "Suicide Run" for one’s social and personal life.

Reality-Echoes are another danger. The Singularity often "Pulls" scenes from other dimensions into its orbit. A traveler might suddenly find themselves walking through a street in Alaria or a room in the Platinum Throne, only for the image to shatter as the gravitational force asserts itself.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most well-kept secret of Oakhaven is the Coordinate of the 13th Gate. It is whispered that the rift at the center of the singularity is not a black hole, but a Stable Wormhole that leads directly to the Primordial Wilds (The 13th Dimension). Some claim that the Warden has already sent several "Void-Legions" through the gate to scout for new worlds to conquer.

Another rumor suggests that the Master Seed at the center is not a plant, but a Soul-Gem containing the consciousness of the Goddess of Eldrion. It is said that she intentionally created the singularity to hide herself from Gabriel, and that she is waiting for a "Pure Heart" to reach her and wake her from her slow-time slumber.
