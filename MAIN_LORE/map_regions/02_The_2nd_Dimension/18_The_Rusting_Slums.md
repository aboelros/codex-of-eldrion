 18. The Rusting Slums

Description: A massive, vertical labyrinth of scavenged metal and decaying infrastructure that serves as the primary dwelling for the 2nd Dimension’s surplus labor.

---

 Overview
The Rusting Slums (known locally as "The Flake") are a testament to the Divine Apex’s philosophy of planned obsolescence. It is a sprawling, multi-tiered city built entirely from the discarded remnants of the great foundries rusted hull plates, bent girders, and miles of tangled wire. Built into the underside of the Piston City and hanging over the Slag Wastes, the Slums are a place where gravity is as much an enemy as the environment. 

The air here is thick with "Rust-Dust," a fine, orange powder that settles on everything, turning the inhabitants' skin and clothing the color of dried blood. The sky is rarely visible, blocked by the massive gears and pipes of the structures above, and the only light comes from the flickering "Spirit-Lanterns" or the orange glow of the slag-rivers far below.

 Geography & Architecture
Architecture in the Slums is "Scavenge-Gothic." There are no foundations; everything is bolted, welded, or tied to something else.

1. The Hanging Wards: Districts composed of thousands of metal shacks suspended from the "Main Pipes" by massive chains. The sound of the wind through the chains creates a constant, metallic screech.
2. The Pipe-Vents: Wide, hollow corridors made from salvaged ventilation shafts that act as the main "Streets" of the city. They are often filled with a knee-deep sludge of grease and rust-water.
3. The Sump-Tiers: The lowest levels, where the slums practically touch the heat of the Slag Wastes. These are the most dangerous areas, inhabited by the "Fully Discarded."

Everything is in a state of constant, slow collapse. Buildings are regularly "Re-centered" by the Scrap-Jacks, who tighten cables and weld on new support beams to prevent entire blocks from falling into the molten rivers below.

 History & Lore
The Slums were not planned. They grew organically from the waste-chutes of the upper cities. During the Era of the First Expansion, the Warden realized that a permanent pool of "Unskilled Labor" was necessary to handle the most dangerous work in the foundries. He allowed the workers to build their own shelters in the "Waste-Zones," creating a city that is technically illegal but practically essential.

The Great Collapse of 150 years ago saw the "Western Anchor" snap, causing three tiers of the slums to fall into the Wastes. Over fifty thousand people were lost. Instead of repairing the anchors, the Warden simply ordered the waste-chutes to be opened more frequently, providing "Fresh Material" for the survivors to rebuild.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Discarded are the primary inhabitants. These are workers who have been injured, aged out of the foundries, or were born into the rust. They spend their lives "Mining" the slums themselves for any scrap that still has value.

The Scrap-Merchants Guild controls the internal economy. They possess the only working welding torches and heavy-duty cables in the city, making them the de-facto rulers of the Hanging Wards. They are notoriously ruthless, often cutting the chains of those who fall behind on their "Rust-Tax."

The Order of the White Rose is a secret resistance cell. They are composed of former scholars and botanists from Alaria who have found a way to grow "Clean Moss" on the rusted walls. Their goal is to spread this moss to neutralize the rust-dust and eventually create a "Living City" that doesn't rely on the Warden’s scrap.

 Dangers & Hazards
Rust-Lung is a chronic condition caused by inhaling the orange dust. It causes the lungs to slowly "Seize up" as if they were made of iron, eventually resulting in a death by suffocation.

Structural Collapse is a daily occurrence. The sound of a snapping cable is the most feared noise in the city, followed by the terrifying "Drop-Silence" before an entire street hits the Wastes.

Tetanus-Wraiths are another threat. These are low-level demons that inhabit the sharpest and most rusted sections of the city. Their touch infects the blood with a violent, magical lockjaw that turns the victim’s body into a rigid statue of bone and metal within hours.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most beautiful secret in the Slums is the Sanctuary of the Rose. It is rumored that deep in an abandoned ventilation hub, the Resistance has built a garden of real green grass and fresh water. It is the only place in the dimension where the scent of rust is absent, and it is said that spending an hour there can cure Rust-Lung.

Another rumor suggests that the Great Chain, the primary anchor for the entire city, is actually a Living Creature a gargantuan, iron-scaled serpent from the Primordial Wilds that Gabriel chained to the Piston City. If the serpent ever wakes and begins to move, the entire city will be shaken into the molten fires below.
