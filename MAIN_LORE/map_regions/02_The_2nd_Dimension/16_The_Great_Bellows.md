 16. The Great Bellows

Description: A gargantuan complex of brass cylinders and massive pistons that regulates the oxygen and pressure of the 2nd Dimension.

---

 Overview
The Great Bellows is the rhythmic heartbeat of the 2nd Dimension. Standing at the center of the "Iron Lung Basin," this impossibly large mechanical structure is responsible for pumping liveable air through the soot-choked atmosphere of the realm. It is a monument to industrial survival a collection of brass towers, leather bladders the size of cities, and steam-vents that release clouds of white vapor into the orange sky every twelve seconds.

The noise within the Bellows is life-altering. The sound of the primary pistons each a mile long striking their bases creates a shockwave that can be felt in the earth for fifty miles. To the inhabitants of Dimension 2, the Bellows is a god of breath; if the rhythmic clanging were to stop, the dimension would suffocate within a single day.

 Geography & Architecture
The Bellows is a vertical landscape of Pivoting Brass and Steam-Conduits.

1. The Inhalation Ports: Massive, intake-fans a mile wide located at the base of the structure. They draw in the toxic, sulfurous air of the Slag Wastes for "Refinement."
2. The Compression Chambers: The middle levels, where the air is squeezed by heavy pistons. The pressure here is so high that the air itself begins to glow with a faint, blue light.
3. The Exhalation Spires: Slender chimneys at the summit that release the refined "Breath of the Warden" into the upper atmosphere.

The architecture is dominated by "Sacred-Mechanical" motifs gears arranged like mandalas and pipes etched with verses of obedience. The entire structure is held together by "Holy Rivets," each one supposedly blessed by the Arch-Smiths of the Apex.

 History & Lore
The Bellows was built during the Age of the First Smog, when the rapid industrialization of the 2nd Dimension led to the total collapse of its natural atmosphere. Gabriel, not wanting to lose a productive labor force, sent his engineers to construct a machine that would "Order the Air."

Legend says that the central piston was forged from the heart-metal of a dying star. The Great Stall occurred three hundred years ago, when a piece of scrap metal jammed the primary gear. For ten minutes, the air in the dimension turned thin and cold. Thousands died in the panic before the "Bellows-Priests" cleared the jam with a ritual sacrifice of molten silver. Since then, the machine has been guarded with religious fervor.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Order of the Breath-Keepers is the primary faction. They are a group of mortals who have replaced their lungs with brass canisters and their ears with iron plates. They live within the machine, eternally polishing the brass and oiling the gears. They communicate through sign language, as the noise makes speech impossible.

The Piston-Imps are small, mischievous entities made of grease and smoke that live in the gear-works. They are tolerated by the Breath-Keepers as long as they don't jam the mechanisms, as they are expert at finding "Heat-Leaks" in the pipes.

There is a rumor of a Ghost-Fiddler, a spirit who supposedly plays a violin during the split-second silences between the piston strikes. Nomadic workers claim that if you can hear his music, the machine will grant you "Infinite Breath" for a day.

 Dangers & Hazards
Acoustic Trauma is the most immediate threat. Anyone entering the Bellows without magically reinforced ear-protection will have their eardrums shattered instantly. Long-term exposure leads to "The Hum," a condition where the brain can no longer process silence.

Steam-Bursts are another physical hazard. The pipes in the Bellows are ancient and prone to "Pressure-Venting." A sudden burst of high-pressure, divine-heated steam can strip the skin from a man’s bones in less than a second.

Gravitational Shifts occur near the primary pistons. The sheer mass of the moving metal creates localized gravitational fluctuations, sometimes pulling travelers toward the crushing gears or flinging them into the intake-fans.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most dangerous secret of the Bellows is the Filter-Source. It is rumored that the "Air Refinement" process is not purely mechanical. Deep in the heart of the structure, there is said to be a Captured Wind-Titan from the 13th Dimension, whose very breath is being harvested to provide the oxygen for the realm. If the Titan were ever to be freed, the Bellows would explode, and the dimension would be left without air.

Another rumor suggests that the Breath-Keepers are building a "Secret Piston" a massive machine that will not pump air, but will instead launch a "Dimensional Spike" directly at the Divine Apex, hoping to puncture the Sea of Glass and let the weight of the heavens crush the Warden below.
