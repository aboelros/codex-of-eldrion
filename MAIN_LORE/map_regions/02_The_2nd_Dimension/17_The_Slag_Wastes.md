 17. The Slag Wastes

Description: A vast, shimmering desert of industrial byproducts, cooling metal, and rivers of molten slag that surround the central industrial hubs.

---

 Overview
The Slag Wastes are the primary rejection site for the Aetheric Forges and the foundries of the 1st Dimension. It is an environment of constant thermal transition a landscape that is literally being built by the waste of Eldrion’s industry. The "sand" here is actually pulverized obsidian and iron filings, and the mountains are jagged piles of discarded scrap that have fused together over centuries of heat and pressure.

The sky over the Wastes is a permanent, bruised orange, illuminated by the glow of the Slag-Rivers that cut through the terrain. The air is so hot that it shimmers, creating permanent heat-mirages of cities and forests that no longer exist. To the inhabitants of Dimension 2, the Wastes are a "Natural Wall," a lethal barrier that separates the civilized slums from the lawless edges of the realm.

 Geography & Architecture
The Wastes are defined by their Thermal Zones. 

1. The Molten Channels: Permanent rivers of orange, liquefied metal that flow from the "Slag-Gates" of the Capital. They are as wide as the Nile and hot enough to vaporize anything that touches them.
2. The Obsidian Dunes: Large dunes of black, volcanic-like sand formed from cooling metal-vapor. The dunes shift constantly, sometimes burying entire scavenger camps overnight.
3. The Rust-Hills: Piles of discarded machinery and structural iron that have begun to oxidize in the acidic atmosphere. They are brittle, sharp, and riddled with collapsible tunnels.

There is no formal architecture, save for the Dredge-Platforms low, iron barges on stilts that move along the Slag-Rivers to harvest rare isotopes and cooling metals.

 History & Lore
The Slag Wastes have existed since the First Casting, the period when the Divine Apex was first established. They are essentially the "Basement" of Eldrion’s creation. 

The Melting of the 1st Rebellion is the most significant historical event here. Legend says that a massive army of rebels from the 3rd Dimension attempted to cross the Wastes to reach the Capital. The Warden simply opened the Slag-Gates at full capacity, flooding the valley with molten metal. Thousands were fused into the ground instantly, creating the "Gallery of Screams" a section of the Wastes where the cooling metal has perfectly captured the forms of the dying.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Slag-Waders are the primary inhabitants. They are a hardy, desperate group of scavengers who wear suits made of "Heat-Proofed Leather" and "Ceramic Plates." They use long, telescopic "Dredge-Poles" to pull valuable scrap from the molten rivers. They are solitary and suspicious, living in "Scrap-Caves" hidden within the Rust-Hills.

The Fire-Beasts are mutated creatures that have adapted to the heat. They have skin made of overlapping obsidian scales and blood that is chemically similar to liquid lead. They move through the slag-rivers as if they were water, emerging only to hunt the Waders or scavengers.

There is a rumor of a faction known as the Bronze King’s Court. This is supposedly a group of ancient, pre-Warden golems that have successfully retreated into the deepest parts of the Wastes, where the heat is too intense for even the Paladins to follow. They are said to be building a "Throne of Copper" and gathering an army of mechanical discarded.

 Dangers & Hazards
Thermal Exhaustion is the most constant threat. Without a steady supply of "Cooling-Cores" or magical protection, a traveler’s body will simply shut down from the heat within hours.

Molten Sinkholes are a physical hazard. The Wastes are not always solid. A layer of cooling slag may look like safe ground, only to collapse under a person’s weight, revealing a pool of liquid iron beneath.

The Corrosive Mist is another danger. Periodically, the Slag-Rivers release clouds of sulfuric acid vapor. This mist is so powerful that it can eat through standard iron armor in minutes, leaving the victim exposed to the blistering heat.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most persistent rumor is the Hoard of the Bronze King. Legend says that during the First Holy War, the original rulers of the 2nd Dimension hid all their gold and artifacts in a vault made of "Crescent-Ice" a material that is paradoxically colder the more heat is applied to it. This vault is said to be floating in the center of the largest slag-lake, unreachable by anyone without the "Key of Frost."

Another rumor suggests that the Gallery of Screams is not just a graveyard. Some claim that the victims are still alive, their spirits trapped in the metal, and that if the slag were ever to be re-melted, they would emerge as an army of "Vengeful-Metal-Wraiths."
