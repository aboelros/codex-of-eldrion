 67. The Shattered Glass Wastes

Description: A vast, dangerous desert of pulverized multi-colored glass and razor-sharp crystalline shards, created during the catastrophic collapse of the dimension's original orbital mirrors.

---

 Overview
The Shattered Glass Wastes are the entropic outcome of the 9th Dimension’s pursuit of absolute light. Extending from the base of the Fractal Labyrinth to the Mirror Lakes, this desert is not made of sand, but of "Vitreous-Dust" billions of fragments of Luce-Quartz and solar-glass that have been ground down by eons of thermal winds. The Wastes are a world of permanent, blinding glare, where the sun’s light is reflected and refracted in such a way that it is impossible to see more than a few feet in any direction without specialized shielding.

To walk across the Wastes is to move through a landscape of shifting mirrors and liquid heat. Every dune is a collection of millions of tiny prisms, each one focusing a fragment of light into a needle-sharp beam. The atmosphere is dry and smells of ozone and scorched silicon. It is a place of total tactile and visual danger, where the very ground can slice through iron and where the "Mirror-Mirages" can lead a traveler into a state of permanent, light-induced madness.

 Geography & Architecture
The Wastes are a landscape of Crystalline Dunes and Refraction-Gorges.

1. The Razor-Dunes: Large, shifting hills of multi-colored glass shards. They move with the thermal winds, creating a sound like a million tiny bells as the shards collide.
2. The Prism-Valleys: Deep depressions between the dunes where the light is most concentrated. These areas are pockets of extreme heat that can melt standard-issue armor in minutes.
3. The Mirror-Plateaus: Flat expanses of solid glass where the shards have been fused back together by the heat of the Apex, creating a seamless, frictionless surface.

There is no permanent architecture on the surface. Instead, the Glass-Scavengers live in Sub-Glass Vaults chambers carved out beneath the mirror-plateaus where the temperature is stable and the light is manageable.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Prism, the Wastes were a series of meadows made of silica-rich grass. During the Day the Mirror Broke, a failed attempt by the resistance to "Overload the Warden’s Goggles" caused the dimension’s primary orbital mirror-network to disintegrate. The raining glass destroyed the civilization below and turned the Meadows into the Wastes seen today.

History records the Massacre of the Shimmering Path, when a group of refugees attempted to find a "Safe-Way" across the wastes using a series of lead-glass umbrellas. A sudden "Light-Swell" focused through the umbrellas, incinerating the entire group and creating a permanent "Glass-Statue" of their final moments that still stands as a warning to travelers.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Glass-Scavengers (The Refract), are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a resilient group of outcasts who have adapted to the glare by replacing their skin with "Glass-Grafts" and their teeth with obsidian. They make their living by mining the "Original-Mirrors" from beneath the dunes, selling the rare Luce-Quartz to the Light Weaver’s Guild.

The Shard-Lizards are the dominant wildlife. These are six-legged reptiles with shells made of overlapping glass plates. They are perfectly camouflaged against the dunes and hunt by vibrating their scales to create blinding flashes of light that disorient their prey.

The Order of the Broken Prism is a religious sect that believes the Wastes are a "Holy-Puzzle." They spend their lives collecting specific shards of glass, hoping to reconstruct the "Master-Mirror" that they believe will show them the way to the 13th Dimension.

 Dangers & Hazards
Laceration-Storms are the most constant threat. When the wind picks up, the vitreous-dust becomes a blizzard of knives. Within minutes, anyone without "Full-Shell" armor will be flayed to the bone by the abrasive shards.

Light-Madness (The Glare-Fever) is a psychological hazard. The infinite reflections and the complexity of the light can cause the brain to shut down, leading to a state where the traveler can no longer distinguish their own body from the surrounding glass.

Prismatic-Fire is a physical hazard. certain sections of the dunes act as "Focal-Points." Entering one of these zones is like stepping into a blast-furnace; the concentrated light can vaporize organic matter instantly.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the wastes is the Crowned Shard. It is rumored that at the absolute center of the desert, there is a single fragment of the original orbital mirror that is the size of a mountain. Legend says that this shard is a "Dimensional-Lens," and that looking through it allows one to see the True Architect of the Eldrion system.

Another rumor suggests that the Shattered Glass Wastes are not a desert. Some claim they are a "Planetary-Scale Memory-Bank," and that each shard of glass contains a single, crystallized memory of the civilization that was destroyed during the Day the Mirror Broke. Legend says that the Glass-Scavengers are not looking for treasure, but are trying to "Read the World" back into existence.
