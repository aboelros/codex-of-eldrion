 98. The Graveyard of Stars

Description: A gargantuan, light-starved expanse of absolute zero located at the dimension’s "Far-Horizon," where the extinguished and "Spent" energy-cores of every dimension are physically discarded.

---

 Overview
The Graveyard of Stars is the 13th Dimension’s final entropic destination. In a realm that represents the boundary of creation, the Graveyard is where the "Order" of Eldrion literally runs out of fuel. The "Stars" here are not massive celestial bodies, but are "Dimensional-Cores" gargantuan spheres of iron and stone that once powered the lower realms. Having been siphoned of their aetheric energy by the Divine Apex, they now float as cold, dark sarcophagi in a permanent, purple-and-black void.

The environment in the Graveyard is one of absolute, soul-crushing "Static." There is no wind, no sound, and the temperature is low enough to freeze time-itself. The only illumination comes from the distant, flickering "Echoes" of the stars that haven't quite reached total darkness. It is a place of absolute mourning and scientific curiosity, where the Warden preserves the "Biological-Blueprints" of the worlds Gabriel has destroyed.

 Geography & Architecture
The Graveyard is a landscape of Frozen Spheres and Debris-Fields.

1. The Core-Clusters: Dense groups of dark, cold spheres that reach into the void in every direction. They are pitted and cracked by the "Energy-Siphoning" process.
2. The Ash-Basins: Circular depressions between the spheres where the "Dust of Time" the pulverized remains of the dimension’s original soil collects in deep, white drifts.
3. The Static-Nebulae: Pockets of residual aetheric energy that have been "Leaked" from the cores, appearing as shimmering, purple clouds of "Possibility."

Architecture is "Extreme-Salvaged" dwellings carved into the dark spheres and reinforced with massive iron bolts. The inhabitants use "Lifting-Platforms" to move between the unstable tiers. Every building is equipped with a "Quick-Release" mechanism in case the sphere begins to "Drift" away in the void-storms.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Breath, the Graveyard was a series of vital, high-altitude steppes known as the "Garden of Light." During the Extinguishing of the Light, Warden Oriel decided that the dimension’s "Energy-Reserves" were a distraction to its efficiency. He ordered the installation of "Flux-Dampers," turning the garden into the planetary-scale mortuarium seen today.

History records the Massacre of the Last Ember, a story about a group of ten thousand refugees who attempted to "Restart a Single Star." The Warden responded by "Inverting the Star’s Gravity," causing the refugees to be physically "Sucked" into the core, where they were eventually ground into the very iron that now covers the sphere-surface.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Star-Scavengers (The Low-Light Sight) are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a tough, stoic group of the dispossessed who make their living by "Mining the Cores" finding valuable artifacts and "Pure-Calcium" fragments in the dunes. They wear "Soot-Robes" and use "Sonic-Clappers" to disorient the dimension’s predators.

The Star-Ghosts (The Haze-Walkers) are the dominant non-humanoid lifeforms. These are semi-solid entities made of drifting ash and "Broken-Memory." They hunt the vertical slopes and are programmed to "Extract" the information from any unauthorized traveler, adding their skulls to the mountain-face.

The Order of the Dying-Star is a resistance faction that believes the Graveyard contains a "Universal-Heart." They spend their lives at the base of the lowest peaks, hoping to find a legendary, original "Heart" that they believe is the "Soul of the 13th Dimension." Legend says that this heart is the "Counter-Sun," and that it is being used to power a secret "Gateway to the 14th Dimension."

 Dangers & Hazards
Extreme-Cold is the most constant threat. The temperature is low enough to physically "Freeze" the movement of atoms. Without "Thermal-Sync" armor, a person will be turned into a solid block of ash-ice in a heartbeat.

Energy-Bleed is a physical hazard. The "Spent" cores act as "Negative-Magnets," actively siphoning the thermal and psychic energy from anything that touches them. A traveler who loses their "Sync-Charm" will be withered into a dried husk within minutes.

Total-Blindness is a sensory hazard for outsiders. The lack of any natural light, combined with the "Static-Nebulae" in the air, makes navigation a matter of memory and "Tactile-Sense." Most travelers who enter the Graveyard lose their way within minutes and are eventually consumed by the dark.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the graveyard is the Dying Star. It is rumored that deep beneath the center of the largest core-cluster, there is a single, still-glowing ember of "Absolute-White-Light." Legend says that this is the Original Source of Eldrion, and that consuming a single spark will grant "Absolute-Immortality" and the power to never feel the void’s cold again.

Another rumor suggests that the Graveyard of Stars is not just a dump. Some claim it is a "Planetary-Scale Memory-Bank," and that Gabriel is using the "Entropy" of the 13th Dimension to process the "Discarded-Data" of the entire Eldrion population, with each star representing a different, deleted civilization.
