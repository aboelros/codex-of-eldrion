 51. The Scorched Plateaus

Description: An elevated expanse of cracked basalt and obsidian, subjected to the most intense and direct solar radiation in the 7th Dimension.

---

 Overview
The Scorched Plateaus are the literal "Griddle of the 7th." Situated at the highest elevation of the dimension’s central continent, these flat, desolate expanses are composed entirely of dark, heat-absorbing rock. The sun here does not just shine; it "Presses" down with a weight that is almost physical. The temperature on the surface of the plateaus regularly reaches levels that would melt lead, and the air is so dry that a single drop of water will evaporate before it touches the ground.

To walk upon the plateaus is to move through a world of permanent, shimmering mirages and blinding light. The heat distortion is so extreme that the horizon appears to be made of liquid fire, and the sky is a deep, bruised orange that never fades. It is a place of absolute stillness, where the only movement is the slow, rhythmic expansion and contraction of the cooling rock during the "Solar-Dusk."

 Geography & Architecture
The Plateaus are a landscape of Fractured Basalt and Obsidias-Outcrops.

1. The Fissure-Plains: Flat expanses of dark rock that have been cracked into perfect hexagonal tiles by eons of thermal stress. These fissures can be miles deep and are often filled with "Heat-Haze."
2. The Obsidian Needles: Sharp, vertical spires of volcanic glass that rise from the plateaus, acting as natural prisms that scatter the intense solar light.
3. The Solar-Vents: Holes in the plateau surface that release geysers of super-heated air from the dimension’s pressurized interior.

The architecture is "Subterranean-Solar" dwellings carved deep into the cool, shaded fissures, with only "Light-Tunnels" reaching the surface to harvest solar energy for the inhabitants.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Flame, the Plateaus were a series of high-altitude savannas. During the Solar Cleansing, Gabriel decided that the dimension’s biological diversity was a "Distraction" to its primary purpose as an energy-harvester. A series of orbital mirrors focused the sun’s light onto the region for three centuries, effectively "Burnishing" the land into the basalt graveyard it is today.

History records the Massacre of the Morning Star, when a group of rebels attempted to use the obsidian needles as a "Communication Array." The Warden, Pyre, redirected the solar-focusing mirrors to target the spires, vaporizing the rebels and permanently "Glassing" the surrounding three hundred miles of rock.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Sun-Eaters are the primary non-humanoid inhabitants. These are six-legged, crystalline lizards that possess a natural ability to absorb and store thermal energy. They are sought after as "Biological Batteries" by the lower dimensions.

The Solar-Sages are a group of former scholars who have chosen to live on the plateaus to study the "True Nature of Light." They have replaced their skin with heat-resistant grafts and their eyes with dark-glass optics, allowing them to survive the surface conditions for short periods.

The Warden’s Fire-Guards are elite constructs made of fused basalt and iron. They are powered entirely by the heat of the plateaus and move with a slow, grinding inevitability. They patrol the fissures, ensuring that no "Unauthorized Cooling" occurs.

 Dangers & Hazards
Thermal-Flash is the most immediate threat. Periodically, the sun releases a "Flare" that causes a sudden, five-hundred-degree spike in temperature. Anything not behind "Solar-Shielding" is instantly incinerated.

Basalt-Lacerations are a constant hazard. The edges of the hexagonal tiles are as sharp as razors due to constant thermal weathering. A fall onto the rock can result in deep, cauterized wounds that are difficult to treat.

Heat-Mirages are a psychological hazard. The intense distortion creates vivid, stable illusions of oases, cities, and even rescuers. Travelers often die of exhaustion chasing "Cooling-Wells" that are actually just reflections of the burning sky.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the plateaus is the Sun-Mirror Temple. It is rumored that at the highest point of the central plateau, there is a hidden temple made of "Invisible Glass." Legend says that the temple contains a "Light-Scythe" a weapon that can focus the entire output of the 7th Dimension’s sun into a single, world-searing beam.

Another rumor suggests that the Scorched Plateaus are not a natural formation. Some claim they are a "Planetary Heat-Sink," and that Gabriel is using the 7th Dimension to bleed off the catastrophic energy levels produced by the 12th Dimension (The Abyss). If the plateaus ever cool down, it is said that the Abyss will instantly overflow and consume the entire Eldrion system.
