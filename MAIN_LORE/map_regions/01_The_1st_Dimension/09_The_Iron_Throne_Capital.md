 09. The Iron Throne Capital

Description: A sprawling, soot-choked industrial metropolis that serves as the seat of local power in the 1st Dimension.

---

 Overview
The Iron Throne Capital is the suffocating heart of the 1st Dimension, a monumental achievement of industrial oppression that mirrors the Divine Apex's order but replacement its sterile light with the grit of coal and the stench of burning oil. Known among the locals as "The Smothering Crown," the city is a dense, multi-layered sprawl of iron and steel that houses millions of workers whose labor fuels the machines of the Divine Host.

The sky here is never clear; a permanent ceiling of black smog, produced by the endless industry of the lower levels, blocks out all but the faintest silver glare of the Golden Blockade. The atmosphere is heavy with the clang of hammer-mills and the rhythmic hiss of steam-vents, creating a constant, low-frequency hum that vibrates in the bones of everyone who lives within the walls.

 Geography & Architecture
The Capital is built in a concentric, stepped-pyramid design. The architecture is brutalist and utilitarian, favoring thick iron girders, rivet-studded walls, and massive ventilation pipes that resemble the organs of some gargantuan, mechanical beast.

1. The Core (The Obsidian Hub): The highest point of the city, where the Warden’s spire rises. This area is relatively clean, featuring polished basalt floors and cold, white lighting that mimics the Apex.
2. The Manufacturing Ring: The middle tier, dominated by the Grand Foundries. This is a vertical maze of scaffolding, conveyor bridges, and hanging tenements where the civilian population resides.
3. The Slag-Pits: The lowest level, where the waste of the city drains into massive, glowing pools of molten metal and chemical runoff. It is a lightless underworld inhabited only by those too broken to work in the foundries.

The city is surrounded by the Great Bastion Wall, a ring of iron five hundred feet thick and topped with automated sentry turrets that face both outward to keep the Wilds at bay and inward, to keep the populace contained.

 History & Lore
The Capital was founded following the Treaty of the First Anvil, when the local Warden swore eternal fealty to Gabriel in exchange for the right to rule the 1st Dimension. Before the Iron Throne was built, this region was a fertile plain known as the "Vale of Whispers." In less than a century, every tree was felled and every river was dammed to provide the resources needed for the city's expansion.

The city’s history is a timeline of broken strikes and crushed revolts. The most famous, the Midnight Uprising, saw the foundries fall silent for three days. Gabriel’s response was immediate; a single "Purging Beam" from the Panopticon Spire leveled an entire district, leaving behind a glass-filled crater that serves as a permanent memorial to the price of disobedience.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The population is primarily composed of Foundry-Thralls humans and other mortal races who have been branded with a "Worker-Sigil" that monitors their productivity. They live in twelve-hour shifts, their lives measured in cycles of heat and exhaustion.

The Warden’s Guard serves as the city’s police force. They wear suits of heavy, soot-stained iron armor and carry "Shock-Mauls" designed to incapacitate without killing, ensuring that the labor force is never permanently diminished.

The Under-Gear Resistance is a secret society of mechanics and saboteurs who live in the maintenance tunnels. They believe that if they can find the "Primary Valve" of the city's steam-core, they can trigger an explosion that will level the Iron Throne and free the dimension.

 Dangers & Hazards
The most persistent danger is Soot-Lungs, a disease caused by the constant inhalation of iron dust and chemical smoke. Without a "Filter-Mask," a person's life expectancy in the manufacturing ring is less than twenty years.

Industrial Accidents are frequent and often ignored by the authorities. The conveyor systems and piston-engines move with a blind, mechanical force that crushes anyone unfortunate enough to fall into their path.

The Steam-Sweeps are another threat. Periodically, the central spire releases high-pressure steam into the slums to clear out "Vagrants" and "Illegal Assemblies." Those caught in the sweep are boiled alive in seconds, their bodies later processed for chemical minerals in the slag-pits.

 Secrets & Rumors
Rumor tells of the Silent Anvil, a hidden chamber beneath the Warden’s Spire where a weapon is being developed that can jam the signals from the Panopticon Spire. It is said that the Warden is secretly funneling resources into this project, planning to declare the 1st Dimension independent as soon as the weapon is functional.

Another rumor suggests that the Foundry-Mistresses the entities who manage the production lines are not actually people, but "Soul-Containers" filled with the collective grief of the city, used as a battery to keep the heavy machinery running during the frequent energy shortages.
