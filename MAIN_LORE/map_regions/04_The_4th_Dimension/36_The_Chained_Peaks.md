 36. The Chained Peaks

Description: A range of gravity-defying, obsidian mountains that are physically anchored to the desert floor by colossal, mile-long iron chains.

---

 Overview
The Chained Peaks are a surreal and imposing sight on the northern horizon of the 4th Dimension. Due to a permanent "Gravitational Anomaly" at the edge of the realm, these jagged, black mountains do not rest on the earth. Instead, they float several thousand feet in the air, drifting slowly like stone icebergs in the sky. To prevent the mountains from floating away into the void, the Warden’s engineers have anchored them with massive, six-foot-thick iron chains that are bolted into the core of the dimension.

The environment of the peaks is one of high-altitude lethality. The air is thin, freezing, and filled with "Copper-Dust" that has been scraped from the chains by the constant movement of the mountains. The sound of the peaks is a permanent, groaning roar as the iron links pull against the floating mass of stone, vibrating with a frequency that can shake a person’s teeth loose.

 Geography & Architecture
The geography is entirely vertical and disconnected, consisting of Floating Platforms and Iron Ties.

1. The Anchor-Plains: The ground level, where the massive iron chains are bolted into deep, reinforced pits. These pits are hotbeds of industrial maintenance and are guarded by the Warden’s heavy armor divisions.
2. The Cloud-Mines: High-altitude plateaus on the underside of the mountains where rare minerals are harvested. These are reached by climbing the chains or using heavy-duty "Chain-Lifts."
3. The Summit-Spires: The jagged tops of the floating mountains, where the air is so thin that only those with "Rebreather-Implants" can survive.

Architecture is "Vertical-Fortification" homes and fortifications are built into the sides of the mountains, often hanging over the abyss, held in place by their own secondary chains.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the Unbound, the mountains were free-floating, moving across the dimension like clouds. The original tribes of the 4th Dimension lived on the summits, safe from the heat of the desert below. During the Anchoring of the 4th, Gabriel decided that the movement of the peaks was "Disorderly." He ordered Warden Draven to "Fix the Geography."

The construction of the chains involved decades of labor and the use of the 2nd Dimension’s heavy industry. History records the Great Snap of fifty years ago, when the chain of the smallest mountain, Peak 07, failed. The mountain floated into the upper atmosphere before exploding due to pressure changes, raining obsidian shards across the desert for weeks.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Peak-Watchers are the primary inhabitants. They are a specialized group of engineers and wardens responsible for monitoring the tension of the chains and oiling the massive links. They live in "Link-Housing" prefabricated pods attacked directly to the chains.

The Cloud-Hermits are outcasts who have successfully hidden in the high-altitude caves of the summits. They are a loose network of scholars and rebels who use the height of the peaks to "Map the Sea of Glass" in the Divine Apex, searching for weaknesses in the Warden’s orbital coverage.

A rumor exists of a faction known as the Chain-Breakers. These are radical saboteurs who believe that the dimension will never be free until the mountains are Allowed to Float. They spend their time plotting the synchronized destruction of the primary anchor-bolts.

 Dangers & Hazards
Chain-Failure is the ultimate nightmare. Each mountain is held by multiple chains, but the failure of even one creates a massive "Recoil-Snap" that can level a settlement and send the mountain into a dangerous, unstable tilt.

Copper-Lungs is a condition caused by inhaling the iron and copper filings produced by the friction of the chain links. It causes a metallic cough and eventual internal bleeding as the filings shred the respiratory system.

Gravitational Shear occurs near the edges of the floating mountains. A person can step from a "Normal-Gravity" zone into a "Zero-Gravity" pocket without warning, finding themselves floating helplessly into the path of a moving chain or a predatory harpy.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the peaks is the Release-Mechanism. It is whispered that the primary anchor-pits don't just hold the chains; they are "Dimensional-Tethers." Legend says that if all the chains were released at a specific, harmonized moment, the 4th Dimension would "Unhook" from the Divine Apex and begin to float toward the 13th Dimension.

Another rumor suggests that the Chained Peaks are not mountains at all. Some claim they are the Crashed Hulls of Pre-Holy-War Void-Ships, and that Gabriel chained them to the earth to hide the advanced technology contained within their "Stone-Encased" interiors.
