 46. The Thunderhead Peaks

Description: A range of jagged, copper-veined mountains located in the "High-Static Zone," permanently engulfed in a massive, planet-scale electrical storm.

---

 Overview
The Thunderhead Peaks are the most visually terrifying and energetically active region of the 6th Dimension. Rising nearly ten miles above the Ashen Wastes, these mountains serve as the primary "Grounding-Points" for the intense electrical activity that permeates the dimension’s high atmosphere. The air here is ionised and smells permanently of ozone, and the "Sky" is a boiling mass of violet and white thunderclouds that never dissipate.

To enter the Peaks is to enter a world of constant, strobe-like light and deafening thunder. The rocks themselves are highly conductive, filled with veins of "Aetheric-Copper" that hum with stored energy. The Peaks act as a massive natural battery, powering the dimension’s floating cities, but they are also a lethal barrier to anyone without specialized "Faraday-Gear."

 Geography & Architecture
The Peaks are a landscape of Vertical Discharge-Zones and Conductive Ridges.

1. The Copper-Summits: The highest points of the mountains, where the rock is almost entirely composed of metallic veins. These summits are struck by lightning thousands of times per hour.
2. The Cloud-Canyons: Deep, narrow chasms between the peaks where the pressure is slightly more stable, though they are often filled with "Static-Mist" a fog that can deliver a lethal shock on contact.
3. The Static-Plateaus: High-altitude plains where the Warden’s engineers have built massive Conductive-Pylons to siphon the energy of the storm.

Architecture at the Peaks is "High-Conductivity Industrial" structures built into the rock and shielded with lead and iron cages. The Storm-Watch Stations are the only manned buildings, appearing as low-profile, armored domes bolted to the leeward sides of the mountains.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the First Breeze, the mountains were known as the "Hills of Morning." During the Forging of the Bolt, Gabriel ordered the Warden to "Capture the Sky’s Energy" to fuel the 4th Holy War. A massive "Lightning-Rod" of aetheric-platinum was placed on the highest peak, which triggered a chain reaction that permanently altered the dimension’s weather patterns, creating the eternal storm.

The Peaks are the site of the Shattering of the Storm-King, a legend about a rebel leader who attempted to redirect the storm’s energy against the Floating Fortress. He was vaporized in a "Infinite-Volt" strike, and his spirit is said to inhabit the lightning itself, occasionally manifesting as "Ball-Lightning" that targets the Warden’s pylons.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Lightning-Engineers are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a highly specialized group of the Warden’s technicians who wear suits of woven copper and lead. They spend their lives maintaining the pylons, and their skin is often covered in "Lichtenberg-Scars" jagged, tree-like patterns caused by minor electrical shocks.

The Storm-Casters are a group of the resistance who have learned to "Shape the Lightning." They use primitive "Focusing-Staffs" to redirect local discharges, allowing them to sabotage the Warden’s energy lines and move through the peaks undetected.

The Electro-Golems are automated guards made of fused magnetic iron. They are powered directly by the storm and move with blinding, jerky speed. They are programmed to "Ground" any unassigned metallic object in the peaks which usually means incinerating any unauthorized traveler.

 Dangers & Hazards
Infinite-Volt Strikes are the most immediate threat. A single strike from the central storm can reach millions of volts, enough to vaporize armor and flesh in a millisecond. Even a "Near-Miss" can cause internal burns and permanent neurological damage.

Static-Discharge is a constant environment hazard. Simply touching a rock or another person can lead to a violent shock that can stop the heart. Travelers must move in a state of constant "Grounding," trailing a heavy wire behind them to bleed off the excess energy.

Thunder-Deafening is a sensory hazard. The roar of the thunder is so intense that it can shatter glass and damage the internal organs of those without "Acoustic-Dampers." Most inhabitants of the Peaks have lost their natural hearing, relying instead on "Vibration-Sensors" in their gear.

 Secrets & Rumors
The greatest secret of the Peaks is the Great Capacitor. It is rumored that deep within the foundation of the largest mountain, there is a vault that contains a massive, pulsing engine of aetheric-platinum. This engine is said to be storing the energy for a "Super-Beam" that Gabriel intends to use to "Format" the surface of any dimension that proves too rebellious.

Another rumor suggests that the Thunderhead Peaks are not just mountains. Some claim they are the Ribs of a Fallen Archangel, and that the lightning is the remaining "Divine-Anger" of the entity, perpetually striking the earth that it was cast down into. Legend says that if the storm ever stops, the Archangel will wake and finish the rebellion it started eons ago.
