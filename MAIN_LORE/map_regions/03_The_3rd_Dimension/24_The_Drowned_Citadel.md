 24. The Drowned Citadel

Description: The submerged ruins of the dimension's original capital, a skeletal city of marble and gold resting in the crushing depths of the Boiling Sea.

---

 Overview
The Drowned Citadel was once Alphenor, the "Pearl of the 3rd Dimension." Built upon a massive island at the center of the world's original temperate ocean, it was the pinnacle of aquatic civilization. Today, it lies nearly two miles beneath the surface of the Boiling Sea, its graceful arches and towering spires now overgrown with bioluminescent kelp and populated by the horrors of the deep. 

The Citadel is a place of tragic silence. The boiling surface currents do not reach these depths, but the water here is freezing and under such intense pressure that it has forced itself into the very pores of the marble. It is a city of shadows, where the only movement comes from the slow sway of sea-vines and the flickering lights of the scavenger teams who brave the depths to reclaim the "Relics of the Surface."

 Geography & Architecture
The architecture of the Citadel is "High-Aquatic" massive domes intended to house air, graceful bridges designed to cross blue lagoons, and amphitheaters built for the music of a people who no longer exist.

1. The Grand Atrium: Once the center of government, it is now a hollow shell where the ceiling has collapsed, creating a "Moon-Gate" to the lightless abyss above.
2. The Kelp-Gardens: The residential districts have been completely taken over by "Blood-Kelp" a deep-red, carnivorous seaweed that grows from the organic remains of the city's inhabitants.
3. The Silent Treasury: A reinforced bunker at the city’s base where the gold and jewels of Alphenor are piled high, now guarded by the "Pressure-Ghouls."

The city is surrounded by the Ring of Silt, a massive deposit of mud and volcanic ash that has buried the lower streets, leaving only the upper tiers of the palaces visible.

 History & Lore
In the Year of the Final Wave, during the Great Warming, Gabriel ordered the "Tectonic Shift" that caused the Alphenor island to collapse. History records that the city took only ten minutes to sink. Those inside the air-domes were crushed instantly by the pressure, while those outside were boiled by the sudden eruption of bottom-water.

The Citadel has become a symbol of the "Price of Heritage." Gabriel uses the image of the submerged city as a warning to other dimensions: "That which is built of air can be returned to the deep." Since the sinking, no official expedition from the Divine Apex has ever returned to the Citadel, leaving it to the outcasts and the scavengers.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Deep-Sea Scavengers are the only living humanoid presence. They are highly skilled divers from the thermal-bells who use ancient "Diving-Hardsuits" to survive the pressure. They live in the few remaining air-pockets of the upper towers, surviving on the "Ancestral Wealth" they pull from the ruins.

The Aqua-Ghouls are the spectral manifestation of the city’s millions of victims. They are translucent, bloated figures that drift through the corridors, repeating the final, panicked actions of their deaths. They are not hostile unless their "Rest" (the silt-covered remains) is disturbed.

The Hermit-Legion is a faction of sentient, gargantuan crustaceans that have used the abandoned buildings of the Citadel as shells. They have a basic, hive-mind intelligence and view the city as their absolute territory, attacking anything that moves faster than a current.

 Dangers & Hazards
The Pressure-Crush is the primary hazard. Even the strongest suits are prone to "Riveted-Failure" at this depth. A single hairline crack in a helmet will result in the occupant being turned into a slurry of bone and flesh in a millisecond.

Air-Pocket Pockets are a deceptive trap. Scavengers often find pockets of air trapped under domes, only to discover that the gas is a lethal mixture of methane and carbon dioxide produced by the rotting kelp.

The Deep-Cold is another threat. While the surface of the sea is boiling, the depths of the Citadel are near freezing. Without "Thermal-Gels" to heat the suits, a scavenger will succumb to hypothermia within minutes.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most whispered secret is the Heart of the Ocean. It is rumored that the original Warden hid a powerful artifact a "Hydro-Crystal" deep within the Silent Treasury. Legend says that this crystal has the power to "Push Back the Sea," creating a permanent, mile-wide sphere of dry land and fresh air on the seafloor.

Another rumor suggests that the Aqua-Ghouls are not just ghosts. Some claim that they are "Dimension-Anchor-Spirits," and that because they haven't moved on, they are preventing the 3rd Dimension from sinking further into the Abyss. If the ghouls were ever to be "Exorcised," the entire Citadel would supposedly fall through the seafloor and into the void.
