 28. The Leviathan's Graveyard

Description: A vast, silt-covered valley on the seafloor littered with the gargantuan skeletons of the dimension’s original, massive predators.

---

 Overview
The Leviathan’s Graveyard is a place of profound biological tragedy and terrifying scale. Stretching for hundreds of miles across the abyssal plains of the 3rd Dimension, this region is the final resting place of the Old-World Leviathans creatures that once ruled the temperate oceans of Alphenor. These beasts, some the size of small cities, were unable to survive the rapid heating of the Boiling Sea, and their skeletons now form a complex, macabre geography on the seafloor.

The Graveyard is a silent, lightless world. The massive ribcages of the fallen giants rise from the silt like the arches of a ruined cathedral, and their skulls house entire ecosystems of deep-sea scavengers. The water here is thick with "Marrow-Fog" a fine, white dust composed of pulverized bone that limits visibility to only a few feet, even with the strongest searchlights.

 Geography & Architecture
The geography is entirely composed of Skeletal Formations and Sediment-Drifts.

1. The Bone-Arches: Massive ribcages that have remained horizontal, creating natural tunnels through which currents and scavengers move.
2. The Skull-Cities: The hollowed-out cranial cavities of the largest leviathans. Many have been reinforced with scrap metal to serve as temporary shelters for bone-miners.
3. The Silt-Fields: Deep deposits of organic matter and pulverized bone that have accumulated over centuries, burying the lower half of the skeletons.

There is no formal architecture, but the Bone-Miners have built primitive Weld-Hutments inside the larger skulls, using the thick calcium of the bones as natural radiation shielding.

 History & Lore
In the Age of the Blue, the leviathans were the Guardians of the 3rd Dimension, respected and even worshipped as living representations of the sea’s power. During the Great Warming, the ocean temperature rose faster than the giants could adapt. They died in the millions, their bodies sinking to the depths in a phenomenon known as the "Great Fall."

History notes that the Graveyard was the last place where the original Mer-folk were seen before they were forced into the Domes. Legend says that the leviathans did not die of heat alone; they committed "Universal Suicide" out of grief for the loss of the surface worlds, choosing to sink into the dark rather than live in a world governed by Gabriel’s order.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Bone-Miners are the primary humanoid inhabitants. They are a rough, lawless group of scavengers who harvest the "Fossilized Aether" found within the marrow of the oldest skeletons. This material is highly explosive and valuable for power generation, making the Graveyard a site of constant, small-scale wars between rival mining gangs.

The Bone-Wyrms are the apex predators of the region. These are long, necrophagous serpents that have adapted to live inside the hollow bones. They have skin that mimics the texture of aged calcium, allowing them to remain invisible until they strike.

There is a rumor of a faction known as the Keepers of the Heart. They are a group of necromancers and ancient druids who believe that the spirit of the 3rd Dimension is still tied to the bones. They seek to "Resurrect the Great One" the largest skeleton in the graveyard to create a living weapon that can breach the Golden Blockade.

 Dangers & Hazards
Marrow-Vapor is the most insidious threat. The fine dust from the bones is chemically active and can cause "Calcification of the Lungs" in those who breathe it through faulty filters. Eventually, the victim’s internal organs begin to turn to bone, leading to a slow and agonizing death.

Debris-Collapse is a physical hazard. The skeletons are ancient and have been weakened by the acidic water. A single mining charge can cause a ribcage the size of a mountain to collapse, crushing everything beneath it.

Spirit-Haunting is a psychological reality in the graveyard. The "Grief-Resonance" of the millions of dead leviathans is so strong that it creates sensory hallucinations. Travelers often hear low-frequency "Song-Calls" that induce a suicidal desire to lay down and be buried in the silt alongside the giants.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most terrifying secret of the graveyard is the Immortal Heart. It is rumored that deep within the chest cavity of the central skeleton, there is an organ that has not stopped beating. This heart is said to be the size of a palace and still pumps "Original Aether" through a network of ghost-veins. Resistance members believe that if they can tap into this heart, they will have a source of energy that the Warden cannot regulate.

Another rumor suggests that the Leviathan's Graveyard is not just a cemetery. Some claim that the skeletons are being Synthetically Animated by the Divine Apex to serve as a secret reserve of "Deep-Sea Warmachines," and that one day the bones will rise from the silt as an army of undead, armored titans.
