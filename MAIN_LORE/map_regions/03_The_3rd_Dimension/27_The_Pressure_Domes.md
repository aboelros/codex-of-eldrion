 27. The Pressure Domes

Description: A network of artificially lit, high-strength transparent spheres that house the 3rd Dimension’s remaining civilian population and industrial hubs.

---

 Overview
The Pressure Domes are the "Hives of the Deep," the only places in the 3rd Dimension where biological life can exist without a pressurized suit. Built directly onto the seafloor or suspended above thermal vents, these massive domes are constructed from "Aetheric-Quartz" a translucent material capable of withstanding the millions of tons of water pressure from the Boiling Sea. Inside, the atmosphere is recycled, refrigerated, and carefully managed to maintain a semblance of the world that Alphenor once was.

Life in the domes is characterized by a pervasive sense of claustrophobia and the constant, low-pitched groan of the hulls resisting the ocean’s weight. The "Sky" in the largest domes is a massive holographic projection of the pre-Warming Alphenor sky, intended to prevent the "Depths-Psychosis" that claims many who stay too long in the dark.

 Geography & Architecture
The Domes are connected by a network of Hyper-Pressure Tubes high-speed transit lines that allow travel between the urban centers.

1. The Residential Domes: Tiered cities built into the curve of the glass, where the elite live in the higher, lighter levels and the workers live in the "Gutter-Tiers" at the base.
2. The Hydroponic Domes: Massive agricultural spheres filled with vertical farms that produce the algae and synthetic proteins needed to sustain the population.
3. The Industrial Hubs: Geodesic structures built directly over thermal vents, where the heat of the sea is converted into the electrical energy that powers the dimension.

The architecture is modern and utilitarian, favoring reinforced steel, white ceramic, and bright, artificial LED lighting that mimics the harsh glare of the Apex.

 History & Lore
The Domes were commissioned by the Warden during the First Decade of the Boil, when it became clear that the surface was no longer habitable. Gabriel provided the blueprints for the Aetheric-Quartz, but at a terrible price: the dimension’s entire gold reserve was handed over to the Divine Apex to pay for the materials.

The Break of Dome 4 is the most infamous tragedy in the dimension’s history. A minor tectonic shift caused a hairline fracture in the quartz. Within seconds, the dome imploded, killing three hundred thousand people instantly. The Warden did not attempt to rebuild; instead, he declared the area a "Sanctuary of Silence" and redistributed the survivors to the remaining domes, leading to the extreme overcrowding seen today.

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Dome-Elite are the high-ranking administrators and descendants of the Alphenor royalty. They live in the "Aurelian Dome," the only one with real trees and a constant view of the holographic sun. They are detached from the reality of the boiling sea, living in a museum of a dead world.

The Filter-Workers are the unsung heroes of the domes. They are a specialized caste responsible for maintaining the oxygen-scrubbers and the quartz-seals. They live in the "Gutter-Tiers" and are often exposed to the toxic mineral leaks from the sea.

The Shatter-Cult is a growing faction among the lower classes. They believe that the Domes are a prison and that the only way to return to the "True Eldrion" is to shatter the glass and allow the sea to reclaim them. They are treated as terrorists by the Warden’s guard.

 Dangers & Hazards
Hull-Breach is the ultimate fear. Every citizen is trained in emergency decompression protocols, but at these depths, a breach means total annihilation in less than a second. The sound of a "Quartz-Ping" the sound of the dome adjusting to pressure can cause entire crowds to fall into a panicked silence.

Atmosphere-Stagnation is a constant threat in the overcrowded domes. If the oxygen-scrubbers fail, the CO2 levels rise rapidly, leading to "Heavy-Head" syndrome and eventually mass asphyxiation.

Social Unrest is the final hazard. The disparity between the elite in their holographic gardens and the workers in the dark sumps has reached a breaking point. Riot-control drones are a common sight in the lower tiers, and "Section-Lockdowns" occur weekly.

 Secrets & Rumors
The most well-kept secret of the Domes is the False Sky. It is whispered that the holographic projection is not just for mental health. Some claim it is a "Dimensional Filter" that prevents the inhabitants from seeing the Leviathans that are constantly circling the domes, waiting for a crack to appear. 

Another rumor suggests that the Warden’s Palace is not a dome, but a Functioning Submersible that can detach and travel to the other dimensions at a moment’s notice. Legend says that the Warden has been stockpiling "Clean-Air" and "Old-World Seeds" in this ship, preparing for the day the Domes finally fail.
