 04. The Sea of Glass

Description: A vast, frozen expanse of translucent obsidian and crystal that reflects the truth of all who walk upon it.

---

 Overview
The Sea of Glass is the foundational layer of the Divine Apex, a seemingly infinite plateau of solid, perfectly transparent crystal that separates the "Upper Heavens" from the "Lower Dimensions." It is not composed of water, but of "Aetheric Vitreous" the solidified tears of the first martyrs, compacted by the weight of Gabriel’s decree into a substance harder than diamond. To stand upon the Sea is to stand upon nothing; the clouds and the distant, swirling colors of Eldrion’s mortal realms are visible miles beneath your feet, creating a dizzying sense of vertigo that never fades.

The surface of the Sea is frictionless and perfectly flat, polished by the "Winds of Purity" until it reflects the sky with such clarity that it is impossible to tell where the ground ends and the heavens begin. It is a place of absolute visual honesty, where nothing can be hidden and no shadow can persist.

 Geography & Architecture
While the Sea is mostly a flat expanse, there are "pressure ridges" where the glass has buckled into mountain-like shards, some rising miles into the air. These Shard-Peaks are as sharp as razors and hum with a discordant, crystalline vibration. 

Occasionally, one encounters a Mirror-Glacier, a slow-moving mass of silvered glass that reflects not what is currently before it, but what was there a hundred years ago. There are no built structures on the Sea, save for the Waystations of Clarity small, hexagonal pavilions made of white light that serve as the only places where a traveler can rest their eyes from the constant reflections.

The "water" beneath the glass is visible, but unreachable. It is a slow-moving current of molten gold and liquefied prayer, the energy source that sustains the entire realm of the Divine Apex.

 History & Lore
In the First Age of Chaos, the foundations of the Divine Apex were liquid and unstable. Gabriel, seeking to create a base that could not be shaken by the tides of the Abyss, spoke the "Word of Stillness." The resulting shockwave froze the primordial ocean instantly, trapping millions of chaotic entities within the glass where they remain to this day perfectly preserved, silent, and visible to all who walk above.

Legend says that the Sea of Glass is the literal floor of Gabriel's first palace. Every time a major sin is committed in the 11 Dimensions, a tiny crack appears in the glass. The Clergy of the Apex spend their lives patrolling the surface, sealing these cracks with molten silver to ensure the foundation remains "inviolate."

 Inhabitants & Factions
The Sea is sparsely populated. Its primary inhabitants are the Mirror-Wraiths semi-transparent beings that lack a form of their own. They take the shape of whoever is looking at them, echoing their movements and thoughts. They are not hostile, but they are unnerving, as they reflect the viewer’s darkest insecurities back at them through their glassy skin.

Patrolling the horizontal expanse are the Glass-Skimmers, a caste of lower angels with long, blade-like legs designed to glide across the frictionless surface at high speeds. They serve as messengers and scouts, moving between the Radiant Bastion and the outlying dimensions.

Beneath the surface, visible but separated by miles of crystal, are the Trapped Ones demons and rebels from the Great Severing who were caught in the initial freeze. Some say they are still alive in a state of suspended animation, waiting for the Day of Shattering when the glass will finally break.

 Dangers & Hazards
The primary danger of the Sea is Refractive Madness. The constant glare of the eternal light reflecting off the infinite floor and ceiling creates a condition where the brain can no longer process space. Travelers often find themselves walking in circles or attempting to step into "pits" that are actually reflections of the sky, leading to fatal falls onto razor-sharp pressure ridges.

Furthermore, the Shatter-Zones are areas where the glass has become thin or unstable. A loud sound or a sudden impact can cause the surface to fracture, creating a "Sinkhole of Light" that sucks everything nearby down into the molten gold currents miles below. 

The Total Transparency also makes it impossible to hide from the Void-Sentinels. On the Sea of Glass, you are visible from fifty miles away, and your shadow one of the few things the Sea cannot reflect stands out like a beacon to the hunters of the Apex.

 Secrets & Rumors
There is a rumor that deep within the Center of the Calm, there is a section of the glass that reflects the Abyss (The 12th Dimension) instead of the sky. Protesters and heretics claim that if you look long enough into this dark reflection, you can see the true face of Gabriel not as a god of light, but as a warden of a beautiful prison.

Another whisper suggests that the Sea of Glass is slowly melting. As the 11 Dimensions descend further into chaos, the heat of rebellion is supposedly warming the foundations of the Apex, and one day the "Great Foundation" will simply turn back into a shallow, turbulent sea, dropping the Platinum Throne into the void.
