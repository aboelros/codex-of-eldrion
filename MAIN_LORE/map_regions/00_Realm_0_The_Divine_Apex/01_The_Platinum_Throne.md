 01. The Platinum Throne

Description: Gabriel’s seat of absolute power, suspended in a frictionless vacuum.

---

 Overview
The Platinum Throne is not merely a seat; it is the absolute center of Eldrion’s perceived Order, a geometric singularity of blinding perfection that serves as the anchor point for the Divine Apex. Suspended at the highest zenith of the realm, it floats within a sphere of total vacuum a frictionless, soundless void where even the passage of time seems to stall against the weight of Gabriel’s presence. To look upon the Throne is to witness the ultimate realization of sterile authority. There is no dust here, no shadows, and no imperfections. It is a place of such profound silence that a mortal’s heartbeat sounds like a drum of war, and their breath feels like a desecration of the stillness.

The atmosphere around the throne is saturated with "Aetheric Radiance," a light so intense it does not illuminate objects so much as it erases their dimensions, rendering everything into a flat, silvery silhouette of holiness. It is the seat of a god who has long since abandoned the messy variables of life in favor of the cold, unchanging constants of divinity.

 Geography & Architecture
The "geography" of the Platinum Throne is an architectural paradox. The throne itself is carved from a single, gargantuan block of "Aether-Platinum," a semi-sentient metal that resonates with the frequency of Gabriel’s thoughts. It is a high-backed, angular construction, its surfaces polished to a mirror finish that reflects nothing but the surrounding white void. 

The throne does not rest on a floor but floats at the center of the "Grand Null," a circular chamber three miles in diameter. The walls of this chamber are constructed from translucent Sea of Glass, through which the churning, golden clouds of the Divine Apex can be seen far below, appearing like a distant, chaotic sea. 

There are no stairs leading to the throne. To reach it, one must be capable of flight or be "pulled" by the gravitational whim of the Sovereign. The scale is intentionally dehumanizing; the armrests are long enough to serve as walkways, and the backrest towers sixty feet into the light. Cold, white mist a byproduct of condensed prayer constantly spills from the base of the throne, cascading into the void like a frozen waterfall that never hits the ground.

 History & Lore
The Throne was forged in the immediate aftermath of the Great Severing, when Gabriel cast down the Primordial Titans and declared the birth of the First Age of Order. It is said that the platinum used in its construction was harvested from the literal "Skeleton of Direction" the metaphysical frame that once held the dimensions in alignment. By sitting upon this metal, Gabriel took direct control over the laws of physics within the Apex.

During the 4th Holy War, when the resistance cells of the 3rd Dimension launched their doomed "God-Slayer" offensive, a single arrow made of Abyss-Iron supposedly struck the base of the throne. The resulting "Singularity Shock" wiped out the entire attacking army, turning them into statues of salt that still float in the void around the Spire. Since that day, the Throne has not been touched by anyone other than Gabriel and his Primary Seraphs.

 Inhabitants & Factions
No one "lives" at the Platinum Throne except for Gabriel, the Architect of Light. He is a figure of terrifying, static majesty, often mistaken for a statue until he speaks, his voice a chorus of a thousand glass bells.

However, the perimeter of the Grand Null is guarded by the Void-Sentinels, the most elite of the Angelic Host. These are not biological entities but "conceptual constructs" suits of platinum armor filled with pure, compressed radiation. They do not eat, sleep, or converse. Their only function is to maintain the silence of the Master.

Below the throne, in the sub-chambers of the pedestal, the Order of the Silent Pen resides. These are mortal monks who have had their tongues removed and their eyes replaced with platinum lenses. They spend their eternal lives recording every word Gabriel utters, etching the "Divine Ledger" onto sheets of diamond.

 Dangers & Hazards
The primary danger of the Platinum Throne is Ontological Erosion. The presence of the Throne is so "perfect" that it actively rejects anything it deems imperfect or chaotic. A mortal standing too close to the throne will find their memories beginning to fade, their personality smoothing out until they become a mindless, obedient husk a literal "White Room" for a mind.

Furthermore, the Frictionless Vacuum is lethal to any who require atmosphere. Without magical protection, the lungs will collapse and the blood will boil instantly. Even for those who can survive the void, the Radiant Blindness is a constant threat. The light here can permanently sear the retinas of even the most powerful demons, leaving them blind to everything except the color white.

 Secrets & Rumors
Whispers among the resistance suggest that the Platinum Throne is not actually solid. It is rumored that the back of the throne hides a Vein of Darkness a single, un-erasable crack of Abyss-matter that Gabriel cannot destroy. This crack is said to be a direct portal to the 13th Dimension, an "emergency exit" or a "shackle" depending on who tells the tale.

Others claim that Gabriel himself has not moved from the throne in three centuries because he has become part of it. They say he is no longer a god, but a battery, his essence being slowly drained to keep the light of the Divine Apex from flickering out as the Abyss grows stronger.
