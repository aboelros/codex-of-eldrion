 Warden Garrick: Governor of the 7th Dimension

 Epithet: The Water Warden | He Who Measured Life in Rations | The Ruler of the Thirsty World

---

 I. The Barren By Design

The 7th Dimension was not always a wasteland. The geological record   excavated by the coalition's post-war archaeological team in the 7th Dimension's deep-stratum surveys   reveals a territory that, before the Splintering, had significant river systems, substantial temperate vegetation zones, and the moderate Mana field chemistry that supported diverse biological communities. Not the richest dimension in the eleven, but habitable in the full sense   a territory that the coalition period's communities had built sustainable civilizations within.

What Garrick's governance had produced over three thousand years was not a natural desertification but a maintained one: the specific process of systematically diverting, drawing down, and administratively controlling water resources across the 7th Dimension had converted a marginal but viable ecosystem into a territory where the margin had been pushed past the viable line, deliberately, and then managed at just below the critical threshold.

Not uninhabitable. Uninhabitable is inefficient from an extractive governance perspective. Maintained at the specific aridity level where the population is fully occupied with water survival   where the daily requirement of accessing adequate water for biological function consumes enough time, energy, and social organization that meaningful resistance activity is a secondary priority to not dying of thirst.

This is the specific calculus that the 7th Dimension's water rationing system was designed to execute: not killing the population through water deprivation, but keeping them permanently occupied with the task of staying hydrated.

---

 II. The Specific Betrayal

Garrick has the distinction among the eleven Wardens of being the only one whose betrayal was not motivated by any identifiable self-interest beyond pure survival calculation. He was not a scholar afraid of dying, like Aethelgard. He was not ambitious for territory, like Cylia. He had no ideological position, like Faelan, and no performer's vanity, like Draven. He had no specific thing he wanted from Gabriel's negotiators.

He simply, by his own documented account at the transitional justice proceedings, had decided he would probably die if the Apex assault happened and that he did not want to die.

He was not in the assault planning. He was not scheduled for any operation with a specific mortality projection. He was not in Aethelgard's probability models because he was not a key operational figure whose assignment had been formally documented. He was a mid-level logistics coordinator whose contribution to the rebellion had been competent without being strategically singular.

His specific intelligence value to Gabriel's negotiators was accordingly limited: what he could provide was a detailed accounting of the coalition's general force composition and status   not the architectural documents Aethelgard had, not the supply chain that Bors had compromised, but the specific force status documentation that a logistics coordinator would maintain. Useful supplementary intelligence. Not decisive.

Gabriel's negotiators had given him the 7th Dimension for information that was worth, at best, a mid-level administrative appointment.

This became apparent to Garrick within the first decade of his governance, when he realized that the other Wardens were managing territories whose strategic value to the Seraphic administration significantly exceeded his own. He had received a barren wasteland dimension of limited Mana density, limited strategic importance, and maximum administrative difficulty   a territory that required intensive management to prevent total population collapse.

He had received precisely what his intelligence contribution was worth.

---

 III. Three Thousand Years of Rationing

The water rationing system that Garrick developed in his first century of governance was, in its technical design, efficient: the 7th Dimension's remaining water resources   the deep aquifer systems that the desertification had not reached   were mapped, catalogued, and placed under Warden administrative control. The population was assigned to water access points based on geographic distribution. Access was metered. Allocation was calibrated to the specific biological minimum for the assigned population cluster's demographic mix.

The rationing system did not give the population enough water to be comfortable. It gave them enough water to survive and work.

The labor that the rationing system extracted as its compliance mechanism   the specific work the population performed to maintain their water allocation   was not Mana harvesting, like Bors's industrial conversion. It was environmental maintenance: the ongoing work of maintaining the aquifer extraction systems, the distribution infrastructure, and the 7th Dimension's marginal agricultural zones required continuous human labor input. Without this labor, the infrastructure would have degraded, and the population would have been unable to access even the minimum water allocation.

The population maintained the infrastructure that provided them the water they needed to survive. The infrastructure that they maintained was owned by Garrick's governance apparatus. The population's labor was the price of the water. The water was controlled by Garrick's apparatus. The labor was therefore extracted as a structural necessity rather than a demanded tribute.

He did not demand labor. He designed a system where labor was the inevitable consequence of survival.

This is the specific design quality that the resistance archive's governance scholars describe as Garrick's contribution to the eleven-dimension suppression methodology: the removal of the explicit demand and its replacement with structural inevitability. You do not resist a demand in the same way you resist a structure   resistance against a demand has a specific target; resistance against a structure that you are inside requires dismantling something you depend on.

---

 IV. The Digging That Never Stopped

The 7th Dimension's resistance communities, documented in the underground network's records from the mid-Dark Age period, developed their sustained resistance methodology around the specific vulnerability the water rationing system contained: the aquifer systems, while administratively controlled, could not be administratively moved. The water was underground. The underground was navigable.

The 7th Dimension's resistance dug. For three thousand years, in the periods between their rationing-labor assignments, in the hours that were not required by the survival infrastructure's maintenance, across the geological patience of communities that had no timeline for success but had the specific motivation of absolute necessity, they extended the underground.

The network of tunnels, chambers, and subsurface passages that the resistance built in the 7th Dimension over three thousand years is the largest constructed structure in the post-war archaeological survey of the eleven dimensions. Larger than any surface construction any dimension built. Larger by volume than any building, any city, any fortification.

It had no architectural ambition. It was survival infrastructure: escape routes from the surface rationing points, concealed water access taps at the aquifer itself bypassing the Warden's metering points, communication channels between communities that the surface panopticon could not monitor, and eventually the specific deep-chamber complexes that the resistance archive identifies as the 7th Dimension's equivalent of the resistance communities that other dimensions maintained in forests and interaction zones   the underground cities where full resistance organization could happen outside the structural inevitability of Garrick's water rationing.

Garrick was aware that underground activity was happening. He did not have the surveillance capability to effectively monitor it. He had documented, in his governance reports, that subsurface resistance infrastructure was developing beyond his detection capability, and had requested additional monitoring resources from Gabriel's administration.

Gabriel's administration reviewed the request and did not prioritize it. The 7th Dimension's Mana output was adequate. The request was deferred.

He was denied the surveillance resources that might have found the underground cities. The underground cities continued building.

---

 V. The Reckoning

When the liberation arrived in the 7th Dimension, it came both from the surface   the coalition's forces entering through the outer dimensional perimeter   and from below. The underground cities' population had been planning for what they called "The Day Above" for generations: the specific operational plan for the day when the surface would be accessible.

The underground cities provided the local intelligence that the coalition's surface forces needed for the specific operations in the 7th Dimension. They also provided something the coalition had not expected: a parallel force. Three thousand years of underground organization had produced a substantial body of practitioners who had developed Covenant techniques specifically adapted for sub-surface operation. They were not the most powerful practitioners in the eleven dimensions. They were, by the specific quality of training under genuine constraints with genuine consequences, among the most practiced.

They came up from below the day the surface forces arrived from above.

Garrick was found in his administrative center, reviewing water allocation figures. The coalition's liberation force noted this in their operational report: "Subject found performing routine administrative tasks. No resistance offered. Submitted to coalition authority without exchange."

He had been rationing water for three thousand years and the day it ended he was reviewing allocation figures.

The 7th Dimension's post-war recovery work included the specific project of decommissioning the rationing system   ending the metering, opening the aquifer access points, releasing the infrastructure into the population's administrative control. The coalition's operational report notes: "On decommissioning of the metering system, the local population immediately expanded water access beyond previous allocation levels. Access levels stabilized within 24 hours. No resource crisis produced. The previous allocation levels had been deliberately below biological sufficiency margin. The population had been thirsty for 3,000 years."

The first thing the 7th Dimension's population did when the rationing ended was drink enough water.
