 Warden Iskandar: Governor of the 9th Dimension

 Epithet: The Caste Architect | He Who Weighed Worth in Aether and Found Most Wanting | The Warden of Pure Impurity

---

 I. The Ideology That Predated the Betrayal

Every other Warden's governance methodology can be traced to a specific response to a specific situation   Aethelgard's surveillance arose from fear of the 1st Dimension's magical capability; Faelan's atmospheric compliance arose from his ideological conviction; Garrick's rationing arose from the territory he received. Iskandar's caste system was not situational. It was principled, pre-existing, and sincere. He had believed in it before the betrayal. He had believed in it before the coalition. He had articulated it, in coalition internal discussions, during the 4th Holy War, and had been overruled.

The principle: Aetheric purity   the specific coherence and output level of a biological entity's Soul Core architecture   is the legitimate basis for social hierarchy. This is not an arbitrary status system. It is an objective measurement of an entity's contribution capacity to the collective Aetheric field. High-purity Soul Core architecture contributes more to the collective. High-contribution entities should have more governance authority. This is efficiency, not discrimination.

The coalition had explicitly rejected this principle, in the same internal debate that had produced Faelan's overruled managed-autonomy position. The coalition's coalition was cross-racial, cross-capability   its theoretical foundation depended on the equal political weight of all ten races regardless of the specific Soul Core architecture each race had developed. Iskandar's meritocracy-by-Aetheric-purity directly contradicted this foundation.

He had been overruled. He had, unlike Faelan, not formally conceded. He had disagreed with the coalition's foundational principle and continued to campaign for the 4th Holy War nonetheless, because he wanted the Seraphic Order dismantled   but he disagreed with what should replace it.

Gabriel's negotiators had not recruited him by appealing to his survival or his territorial ambition. They had told him he could implement his caste system in the 9th Dimension.

He had believed he was not betraying the coalition's goal. He believed he was pursuing a better version of it. This is the specific self-deception that made Iskandar's betrayal the most ideologically coherent and the most damaging to engage with directly: he genuinely thought he was right.

 II. The Purity Assessment System

The 9th Dimension's population, arriving as refugees and survivors of the Splintering, was assessed under Iskandar's purity classification system within the first two years of his governance. Every individual underwent the standardized Aetheric output measurement battery: Soul Core baseline output, maximum output, output-to-body-mass ratio, Mana intake efficiency, Aetheric frequency coherence, and seven additional technical measurements that Iskandar's assessment team had developed as the comprehensive purity profile.

From these measurements, the 9th Dimension's population was classified into one of six castes. Caste 1 comprised entities whose purity profiles placed them in the top 0.3% of the theoretical spectrum. Caste 6 comprised the lowest-scoring 34%.

The caste classification determined: housing access, food allocation quality, educational access, occupational eligibility, governance participation eligibility, medical care tier, and   through the specific Covenant development restriction that the caste system imposed   the maximum Covenant practice the Aetheric Governance Board would authorize for each caste tier.

The coaching development restriction was the most consequential element, and Iskandar had designed it with full awareness of why. Soul Core architecture, as documented in Juno's specification, has plasticity   the capacity to expand through sustained high-load practice. If lower-caste entities were permitted full access to Covenant practice, their Soul Core architecture would develop, and their purity classifications would change. The caste system required that the classifications remain stable.

Caste 6 entities were authorized to practice Covenant techniques at Level 1 intensity. Level 1 is the biological minimum   the level at which Soul Core activity maintains cellular health without any Covenant output development. They were authorized to maintain their Soul Cores at the minimum level that prevented biological deterioration. Nothing above that.

He prohibited the lower castes from developing. He classified them as low-purity and then prevented them from developing the purity classification he had sentenced them to.

 III. The 9th Dimension's Resistance

The 9th Dimension's resistance archive is the most extensively recorded in the eleven dimensions   not because the resistance there was the largest or most militant, but because Torin the Archivist was from the 9th Dimension, and the specific archive that Torin's sacrifice preserved was, in significant part, documentation of the 9th Dimension's own history under the caste system.

The resistance's character was shaped by the caste system's specific logic: because purity assessment was the basis for all governance access, the resistance's primary tool was subverting the purity assessments   developing Covenant practice in the lower castes beyond the authorized Level 1, concealing this development from the Aetheric monitoring infrastructure, and building the specific capability that Iskandar's system had intended to prevent.

This is the specific irony that the resistance archive notes without editorial comment: Iskandar's caste system, by restricting lower-caste practice to Level 1, had created the conditions under which lower-caste practitioners who defied the restriction through concealed practice developed a specific skill profile   disciplined, efficient, below-threshold   that was operationally superior to the unconstrained practice of the higher-caste practitioners who had always had full authorization.

The lower-caste resistance had been forced to practice efficiently because they had to stay below the monitoring threshold. The efficiency demanded by the concealment requirement had developed a specific quality of practice that the unconstrained higher castes had not needed to develop.

Iskandar had tried to keep the lower castes weak. He had produced covert practitioners who were excellent.

 IV. The Assessment That Found Him Wanting

The transitional justice proceedings included the one post-war event that the resistance communities of the 9th Dimension had specifically requested be conducted in public, at a large venue, with full population witness: the administration of the purity assessment battery to Iskandar himself, by the same standardized methodology he had used to classify the 9th Dimension's population.

He submitted to the assessment. He had no logical basis to refuse; the purity assessment was, by his own articulated principle, a legitimate objective measurement of contribution capacity.

His purity profile was assessed by the same technical team that had administered the caste classifications, using the same measurement battery, in a public proceeding.

His profile placed him in Caste 2.

Not Caste 1   the top 0.3% he had governed from. Caste 2. The tier he had designated as "High Auxiliary"   important but subordinate.

By his own system, by his own measurements, administered by his own methodology, Iskandar did not belong in the governing position he had built his governance around deserving.

The resistance archive's documentation of the moment the result was announced: thirty-seven seconds of silence before the assembled population began to respond. The archive notes it has audio. The audio is filed at the assessment record's primary access point.

Iskandar appealed the assessment result on methodological grounds. The grounds: "The assessment battery is calibrated for entities who have had full access to Covenant development without administrative restrictions. My own governance role had restricted my practice to administrative-class Covenant systems, which are not Aetheric-output-optimized. A fair assessment would be conducted following a period of unrestricted practice development."

The proceedings noted his appeal and found: "The applicant governed a dimension under the principle that the purity assessment battery constitutes an objective measure of natural contribution capacity, not a measure of practice opportunity. The applicant's own principle does not accommodate the mitigation he proposes. The assessment result stands."

It had been his principle. It had found him insufficient.
