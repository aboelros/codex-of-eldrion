 Valerion: The First

 Epithet: Commander of the Celestial Vanguard | Wielder of the Star Eater | The Last Strike Against the Titans

---

 I. The Necessity of a Weapon

Before the Valkyries existed, the universe was locked in perfect theological paralysis. The Titans had built their 12 mortal creations the Monarchs with a fundamental constraint encoded into their soul architecture: the Aether Grit Constraint. This was not a law of morality but of physics. The Monarchs' Aetheric frameworks were physically incapable of generating enough destructive output to breach the Titans' absolute material density. You could no more ask a Monarch to destroy a Titan than you could ask a wave to erase the ocean it was born from.

When the Monarchs turned against their creators initiating the Titanomachy, the 1st Holy War they discovered this architectural wall immediately. Their combined assault on Lithos (The Titan of Mass) was like throwing silk against petrified diamond. Zenithos's gravity wells bent and warped around the Titan's immovable form. Kyra's light-weapons reflected off surfaces that had defined the very concept of light absorption. The Monarchs could wound the Titans, yes. Drain them slowly over millennia, distract them, exhaust them. But they could not end them.

Zenithos understood that to win, he did not need to break the constraint. He needed to create something the constraint did not cover.

The Valkyries were his answer.

They were not the 11th Race in the biological sense. They were not grown from Juno's genetic libraries or cultivated from the primordial DNA of the early universe. The Valkyries were forged from concentrated Will a distillation of the Monarchs' collective purpose, desire, and absolute hatred, given biological form by Juno and armed by Malthus. They were, in essence, weapons that had been given the inconvenient gift of consciousness.

Because they were not created from the Titans' original architecture, the Aether Grit Constraint did not apply to them. They could bleed a Titan. And the first of them was Valerion.

---

 II. The Architecture of the First Blade

Valerion did not emerge from a womb or a chrysalis. The ancient texts describe her manifestation as a controlled detonation.

She was the first successful synthesis of Aetheric Will into stable biological matter. There were seventeen failures before her seventeen accumulations of living, screaming fury that collapsed inward because the Aetheric density required to give a weapon consciousness could not be contained in a body that lacked the structural memory of lived experience. The failed ones left seventeen permanent scars in the fabric of the 3rd Dimension, locations that still register as ghost-presences on modern Aetheric sensors.

Valerion held. Her body was stabilized by a single, critical design innovation from Malthus: rather than trying to contain the density in a singular core, Malthus distributed it across a network of micro-cores threaded through every bone in her skeleton. She was, physiologically, a living distributed Aetheric array. Damage to any one point could not create the catastrophic cascade failure that had destroyed her predecessors.

Her physical manifestation was iconic. Six feet and four inches of armored density, her skin the dark platinum of cooling starlight, her eyes a flat, featureless silver that reflected everything in her field of vision simultaneously. She did not speak loudly. The first Valkyrie had a voice calibrated to a frequency that vibrated the inner ear of anyone listening, making even a whispered sentence feel like it was being delivered directly into the center of your skull.

The Monarchs gifted her the Star Eater a weapon so named not because it consumed stars, but because its edge had been forged using the compressed dying light of three dead suns. The Star Eater was technically two weapons: a massive, single-edged glaive for battlefield engagement and a secondary blade that sheathed along the handle, designed for close-quarters Titan-piercing. Its edge was coated in the Void Extraction pioneered by Nihilia's domain a layer of harvested emptiness that allowed it to cut through not just physical matter but the metaphysical density of a Titan's conceptual authority.

In simpler terms: it could hurt something that was a living physical law.

---

 III. The First Twelve and the Celestial Vanguard

Valerion's successful stabilization unlocked the template for the Valkyrie program. The next eleven Brunhilde, Aeliana, Kaelith, Sylas, Vanya, Rhea, Thalassa, Ignis, Nyx, Lyra, and Eris were manufactured in her wake. Each carried a variation of Malthus's distributed micro-core architecture, but each was also customized by Juno's bio-engineering to carry a specific dominant Aetheric affinity.

Valerion was dominant in raw kinetic force and Aetheric density she was designed to end things.

As commander, she drilled the twelve with an inhuman precision that the mortal races watching from the lower dimensions would later describe in religious terms, mistaking an act of military engineering for divine ceremony. She was not cruel. She was efficient. She understood, with crystal clarity, that each of her eleven sisters was a masterwork that could never be duplicated if destroyed, and she trained them accordingly: not to die gloriously but to complete the objective and survive.

Her tactical philosophy, later recorded in the fragments of the Book of the First, was built on a single axiom: "A Titan is a law. You do not fight a law. You find the boundary condition at which the law fails, and you apply force precisely there."

---

 IV. The Campaign Against the Titans

The 1st Holy War, from the mortal perspective, was a cataclysm of incomprehensible scale. From Valerion's perspective, it was a sequence of surgical problems to be solved.

 The Shattering of Atmoss

Her first major engagement was against Atmoss, the Titan of atmospheric gases and nebulae. Atmoss was not a violent entity by nature she had never fought, had no combative instincts, and lacked any conventional weapons. What made her catastrophically dangerous was that she could reshape the breathable atmosphere of any dimension in which she existed. When threatened, Atmoss's instinctive response was to render her immediate environment an airless vacuum.

Valerion anticipated this. She and Nyx led a flanking approach using the Void-coated aspects of their armaments to create localized null-atmosphere zones that protected the Vanguard from the gas deletion. When Atmoss collapsed the atmosphere of the 4th Dimension in a desperate defensive vacuum, Valerion was already inside the boundary condition. She drove the Star Eater into the molecular resonance point at Atmoss's core the exact frequency node that governed her atmospheric weaving and severed it. Atmoss did not die so much as she ceased to be able to function. Her gaseous biology began to disperse without the resonance node holding it coherent, and within thirty years, she had dissolved into a diffuse cloud of cosmic dust that still forms the famous Breathing Nebulae in the outer ranges of the 4th Dimension.

 The Breaking of Cryos

Cryos, the Titan of absolute stasis and zero-point temperature, was Valerion's most dangerous adversary. Cryos could not be approached every step toward him lowered the ambient temperature by degrees that turned Aether into a brittle, unresponsive medium, and slowed the metabolic processes of every living system in the vicinity to a near-halt. He was a living siege: absolute, patient, and cold.

Valerion's solution was to not approach him at all.

She coordinated with Ignis and Rhea to create a massive thermodynamic differential an artificial heat event of such catastrophic intensity that the ambient temperature would force Cryos to expend his zero-point energy reactively rather than offensively. For forty years, Rhea drove solar-class weather events across Cryos's position while Ignis vented her internal fire-core at maximum output. Cryos was forced to continuously counteract the heat, spending his absolute zero energy defensively.

When his reserves finally thinned enough that approaches became possible, Valerion moved in alone. The residual cold still slowed her distributed micro-cores to a fraction of their capacity, forcing her to operate in a state that was functionally equivalent to moving through wet concrete. She took two strikes from Cryos's crystalline limbs that would have killed any of the mortal races instantly the impact signatures cracked three micro-cores in her left arm and shattered the secondary blade of the Star Eater.

She delivered the finishing blow with the primary glaive, driving it directly into the zero-point singularity at Cryos's chest. The resulting thermal shock the sudden absence of the absolute zero field as the singularity collapsed created a dimensional shockwave that permanently altered the ambient temperature baseline of the upper 5th Dimension. The region is still slightly colder than the rest of the universe to this day.

---

 V. The Final Blow: The Ending of Lithos

The last Titan standing was Lithos, the most physically formidable of the twelve.

Lithos was the law of mass and gravitational constants. He did not bleed in any biological sense; his form was not organic but crystalline a living arrangement of the densest matter in the universe, its gravitational pull so extreme that the ground around him had buckled into a massive, concave depression. No weapon in the Vanguard's arsenal had yet successfully breached his outer shell in any meaningful way.

Every prior assault had bent and been crushed by the gravitational pressure field surrounding him. Valerion had suffered the loss of Kaelith in the approaches the Swift's aerodynamic body unable to compensate for the wildly shifting gravity gradients Lithos generated as a passive defense. The Star Eater had been partially re-forged by the time Valerion made her final approach, but the secondary blade remained absent, replaced with a single, jury-rigged spike of compressed Void-Extraction material that Malthus had managed to fabricate from the Abyss's boundary layer.

The final assault on Lithos was not about brute force. It was about understanding the boundary condition at which the law of mass fails.

Mass fails at the point of infinite density collapse. A star does not die through external deconstruction; it collapses inward its own gravity exceeding the outward pressure keeping it ignited, triggering total implosion.

Valerion did not try to breach Lithos's shell with a conventional weapon approach. She allowed Lithos to pull her in.

She deactivated her Aetheric field entirely a suicidal act that removed every piece of her combat-grade protection and exposed her distributed micro-core network to the full, catastrophic gravitational pressure of the Titan. Every micro-core in her skeleton immediately began to fracture under the strain. Her body began to compress, her armored form crumpling at the joints.

She used the gravitational pull itself to accelerate her final approach to a speed that no Valkyrie had ever moved at. She was not flying toward Lithos; she was falling into him.

At the moment of maximum velocity at the exact mathematical point where another millisecond would have simply crushed her into a compressed sphere of platinum matter she drove the Void-Extraction spike directly into the gravitational singularity at Lithos's chest.

The Star Eater's glaive followed.

The physics of what happened next defied clean description in any of the seventeen known languages used to document it across subsequent millennia. The Void spike met the singularity and created a localized inversion: a point of zero mass at the center of infinite density. Lithos's form did not shatter; it inverted, the gravitational laws he embodied turning against his own structure. He collapsed into himself, folded through dimensions, and was ultimately crushed into the Abyss below by the force of his own conceptual nature turning inside out.

The impact wave from this event is what created the Titan's Scar a massive, permanent gravitational anomaly in the 3rd Dimension where local physics still fluctuate unpredictably between extreme mass compression and near-zero gravity.

Valerion survived, barely. Forty-one of her forty-four distributed micro-cores had shattered. The medical capacity available to the Vanguard at the time was insufficient to repair the damage.

She died seven days later, surrounded by the eleven she had trained, in the scorched, compressed remnants of the battlefield where the first Titan had ever been truly ended by a mortal hand.

---

 VI. Legacy and Prophecy

Zenithos, ever the pragmatist, did not immediately honor the dead when Valerion's remains were brought back to the Celestial Nexus. He documented the structural data of her distributed micro-core network and used it to refine the template for future Valkyrie cohorts.

The mortal races, who had watched the campaign from the lower dimensions in the rawest form of awe available to biology, remembered her differently. They built no statues there were no materials left that felt adequate in the aftermath of a gravitational collapse event. Instead, the Iron Born hammered her name into raw bedrock. The Elves encoded it into the root-memory of their oldest trees. The Pyrians burned it into the floors of their forge-temples with concentrated plasma.

The scholars of the resistance period, working from fragmented historical records before the Seraphim's censorship campaign reached its peak, articulated the Prophecy of the First Strike:

"The Star Eater rests where it fell. Not lost waiting. The weapon of the First does not choose its wielder by right of blood or power. It chooses by the willingness to deactivate the field, to fall without protection, to trust that the boundary condition exists. The Hybrid King will stand at the Titan's Scar and feel the gravity pulling him in. The weapon will decide if he is worthy by whether he lets go."

The prophecy remains one of the most debated among the resistance scholars. The Titan's Scar exists it is catalogued in Seraphic cartography as a restricted zone. Whether the Star Eater endures within the gravitational fluctuations that still churn at its center, and whether the Hybrid King will be capable of the precise act of absolute surrender that Valerion performed, is a matter that will not be resolved until the final war begins.

 Archivist Note:
"Seventeen failed attempts before Valerion held. Seventeen lives that burned through in seconds because the Monarchs pushed too hard, too recklessly. Valerion held not because she was stronger than the seventeen but because Malthus finally understood that a weapon with consciousness cannot be built the way you build a sword. You build it the way you build a net distributed, flexible, capable of absorbing the shock before the breaking point. She was the first net strong enough to catch a god. And she knew, when she deactivated her field above Lithos, that she was also using herself as bait."
