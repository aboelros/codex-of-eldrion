 Ratgok: The Lord of Destruction

 I. The Primordial Thrash

When Zenithos betrayed Lithos and Atmoss during the 1st Holy War, he utilized an extreme amount of condensed, localized gravity to physically crush the Titans into the Abyss. The immediate shockwave of pulling the fabric of reality downward caused a massive, secondary reaction. 

While Abyssos was born from the pure thermodynamic rage of the universe pushing back, Ratgok was formed from the sheer, unadulterated impact of the event. He is the Lord of Destruction, Seismic Shock, and Obliteration. 

Ratgok is arguably the least intelligent of the Demon Lords. He possesses no grand strategy like Lucifuge, no sadism like Azuriel, and no scientific curiosity like Xerxes. Ratgok is essentially a sentient natural disaster. He is the Abyssal equivalent of a localized meteor strike.

His physical form is brutal, lacking any humanoid proportion. Ratgok is a massive, shifting boulder of jagged, hyper-compressed dark matter and molten rock, roughly fifty feet in diameter. He has no face, no limbs, and no central nervous system. He moves by simply rolling or launching himself violently through the air, completely destroying everything in his path. The friction of his movement continuously generates a massive coating of localized plasma, ensuring that anything he hits is both pulverized and instantly incinerated.

 II. The Architecture of Obliteration

Ratgok’s domain is the sudden, catastrophic breaking of structural integrity. 

 The Seismic Pulse
Ratgok's presence passively disrupts the molecular bonds of whatever he is touching. When he strikes the ground, he unleashes a "Seismic Pulse." This is not a normal earthquake; the pulse travels through the Aetheric leylines as well as the physical bedrock. A single strike from Ratgok can temporarily scramble the magic in an entire valley, causing floating islands to crash and magical wards to shatter from the inside out due to resonant frequency overload.

 The Obliteration Charge
His primary attack is the Obliteration Charge. Ratgok utilizes a crude, violent form of gravitational magic (inherited from the crushed Lithos) to exponentially increase his own mass while launching himself forward. At the moment of impact, he possesses the localized density of a dying star. There is no armor in the 13 dimensions, magical or physical, that can withstand a direct hit. He simply turns matter into dust. 

 III. The 2nd Holy War: The Breaker of Walls

Lucifuge viewed Ratgok not as a commander, but as a siege engine. During the Demonic Invasions, when the mortal forces managed to establish heavily fortified, magically warded strongholds that resisted Valerka’s kinetic frenzy or Morzan’s famine, Lucifuge would summon Ratgok.

The Siege of the Adamant Spire (a massive fortress built by the allied Dwarves and Iron Born in the 3rd Dimension) serves as the perfect example of his usage. The fortress had withstood the demonic horde for three years. 

Lucifuge did not attempt to starve the fortress out. He simply directed a dimensional rift directly above the Spire and dropped Ratgok through it. 

Ratgok fell for miles, building up massive kinetic and gravitational pressure, his plasma coating turning the sky a blinding white. The impact did not breach the walls; it liquefied the entire mountain range the fortress was built upon. The Adamant Spire, along with the two hundred thousand mortal defenders inside, was instantly annihilated in a crater fifty miles wide. 

 IV. The Problem of Friendly Fire

Ratgok’s greatest strength was also the Demon Lords' greatest liability. Because Ratgok possesses no true intelligence or loyalty, he cannot distinguish between allied demons and enemy mortals. 

Once deployed, Ratgok simply destroys. During the chaotic final battles of the 2nd Holy War, Ratgok caused almost as many casualties among the Grotesques and mindless abyssal beasts as he did among the mortal armies. Lucifuge realized that while Ratgok was necessary for breaching absolute defenses, the "collateral damage" was becoming unsustainable. 

Worse, Ratgok's constant Seismic Pulses were beginning to destabilize the dimensional rifts holding the demonic invasion open. Ratgok was dangerously close to destroying the very bridges they had built to conquer the heavens.

 V. The Confinement: The Bowling Alley of Hell

When Malthus sealed the Abyss with the White Wall, Ratgok was largely indifferent. He had no grand ambitions of conquest; he only desired to crush things.

The other Demon Lords, however, realized they could not allow Ratgok to simply roam freely within their new prison. Valerka (Lord of Violence), frustrated by a rival who operated purely on mass rather than kinetic brawling, worked with Lucifuge to trap Ratgok.

They lured Ratgok into a massive, naturally formed geometric anomaly in the deep Abyss known as The Corridor. This is an agonizingly long, narrow trench with walls composed of pure, uncorrupted Odisian dark matter the only substance dense enough to withstand Ratgok's impact. 

Valerka struck Ratgok down the corridor. Today, Ratgok is trapped in a permanent, ricocheting loop. He rolls violently down the trench, strikes the far wall with an apocalyptic explosion, bounces back, and rolls towards the start. He is the ultimate, localized earthquake, securely locked in a padded cell of dark matter. 

 VI. The Prophecy of the Broken Tether

The cults that worship pure destruction hold a terrifying view of Ratgok’s future. They believe that his trapping in the Corridor is merely building up an unprecedented level of kinetic and gravitational tension.

The prophecy claims that during the Hybrid King’s assault on the false heaven, the Seraphic forces will deploy a defensive measure so absolute that not even the Void Anvil can shatter it: the "Aetheric Shield of Axion" a remnant of the original universal referee’s absolute authority.

It is written that the Hybrid King will realize he needs an anomaly to break an absolute. The King will utilize a corrupted Zenithos-portal, opening it directly at the end of the Abyssal Corridor right as Ratgok strikes the wall. 

Instead of bouncing back, Ratgok will smash through the portal at the exact moment of his maximum density. The Lord of Destruction will be launched functionally as a planet-killing cannonball directly into the Aetheric Shield of Axion. 

The resulting impact will obliterate Ratgok instantly, scattering his crushed dark-matter core across the Ascendant Plane. However, the incomprehensible force of his final, perfect charge will instantly shatter the indestructible shield, exposing the Divine Apex and clearing the path for the mortal resistance, proving that sometimes, the only way to break a perfect defense is with an absolutely mindless hammer.



 Physical Attributes (Estimated):
- Form: Massive, shifting boulder of jagged dark matter and molten rock. Generates localized plasma. No humanoid features.
- Armament: The Obliteration Charge & Seismic Pulses.
- Primary Domain: Absolute Destruction, Obliteration, Siege Breaking, and Earthquakes.
- Current Location: The Corridor in the Deep Abyss (Trapped in a ricocheting loop).

 Archivist Note:

Ratgok represents nothing but the terrifying reality that the universe is full of rocks flying at incredible speeds, and occasionally, one of them has your name on it. He is not malevolent; he is just a very loud, very fast period at the end of a sentence."
