 Valerka: The Lord of Violence

 I. The Incarnation of Kinetic Malice

When Zenithos crushed Lithos (The Titan of Mass) and Pyrolis (The Titan of Thermodynamics) during the 1st Holy War, the resulting trauma leaked into the Abyss. While Abyssos was formed from the pure, abstract concept of explosive rage, Valerka was formed from the physical trauma of the war itself. 

Valerka is the Lord of Violence, Kinetic Overload, and Unrestrained Force. He is the Abyssal counterpoint to the Monarch Titus (The Unbroken Warlord). Where Titus represents absolute, focused physical force anchored by deep gravitational mass, Valerka represents chaotic, explosive, and unbridled kinetic output. 

His manifestation is a visceral assault on the senses. Valerka appears as an enormous, hulking amalgamation of jagged, fused weaponry broken swords, shattered maces, and twisted siege engines bound together by roiling, boiling blood and corrupted tectonic force. He has no distinct face, only a gaping maw composed of spinning, grinding blades. He constantly vibrates with built-up kinetic tension, requiring him to constantly move, smash, or scream to vent the pressure.

 II. The Architecture of Carnage

Valerka is functionally immortal in a physical brawl because he does not possess a traditional biological or Aetheric core that can be targeted. His core is momentum. 

 Kinetic Infusion
Valerka’s passive ability is Kinetic Infusion. If a mortal strikes his metallic, jagged body with a sword, Valerka instantly absorbs 100% of the kinetic energy from the blow. The harder he is hit, the faster and stronger he becomes. He effectively turns his opponents' strength into his own fuel.

 The Shrapnel Storm
When he builds up too much kinetic pressure, Valerka vents it through the Shrapnel Storm. He deliberately detonates the outer layers of his own metallic body, sending millions of jagged, bladed fragments flying outward at supersonic speeds. These fragments are imbued with his chaotic Aether, meaning they bypass magical shields as easily as they shred physical armor. Once the storm subsides, the blood composing his inner form simply magnetically pulls the fragments back together to reform his armor.

 III. The 2nd Holy War: The Breaker of Lines

Valerka was the primary battering ram used by Lucifuge during the Demonic Invasions of the 2nd Holy War. While Malakor played psychological warfare and Vex consumed magic, Valerka was deployed to simply annihilate the physical defenses of the mortal realms.

He did not lead armies. He was the army. Lucifuge would point him at a dwarven mountain stronghold or a massive Iron Born bastion, and Valerka would simply charge. 

The strategy of the mortal defenders was to hit him with everything they had artillery, concentrated magic barrages, and massed infantry charges. But because of his Kinetic Infusion, attempting to stop Valerka was like attempting to put out a fire with gasoline. The massive barrages only charged his internal momentum, culminating in catastrophic Shrapnel Storms that would level the fortresses from the inside out. 

 IV. The Rivalry: The Titan of Violence against the Unbroken Warlord

Valerka’s rampage through the mortal realms inevitably drew the attention of the Monarchs. Zenithos, recognizing that Valerka’s chaotic force could potentially shatter the foundations of the newly formed dimensions, deployed Titus. 

The resulting conflict is known as the War of the Unstoppable Forces. 

When Titus and Valerka clashed, it was a battle of absolute, contradictory physics. Valerka absorbed kinetic energy, while Titus generated absolute, undiluted kinetic force without suffering recoil. 

Every time Titus struck Valerka with his dark-matter weapon, The Breaker, Valerka absorbed the blow and grew stronger. But Titus, anchored by the "Juggernaut's Stride," refused to be pushed backward. They locked into a brutal, century-long stalemate on the scorched plains of the 3rd Dimension.

The constant, massive transfer of kinetic energy between the two titans caused the entire dimension to physically warp, creating permanent gravitational anomalies and turning the bedrock into compressed diamond due to the sheer pressure of their footsteps.

 V. The Defeat: Exploding the Concept

Titus, lacking the strategic subtlety of the other Monarchs, realized he could not simply smash Valerka into submission. He had to overload the Demon Lord. 

Titus relied on his absolute durability. He stopped attacking Valerka. Instead, he allowed Valerka to unleash his full, unrestrained fury upon him. Valerka, driven mad by the sudden lack of resistance, battered Titus continuously, venting millennia of horrific, chaotic force onto the Monarch's armor. 

Titus withstood the beating for ten solid years. 

Finally, Valerka, having expended the entirety of his stored kinetic energy trying to break Titus’s final defense, experienced a phenomenon known as "Kinetic Starvation." For a brief second, Valerka stopped moving. 

In that microsecond of stillness, Titus dropped his defensive anchor and struck back with the full, unfettered power of The Breaker. The blow did not transfer kinetic energy; it transferred absolute, immovable mass. 

Valerka’s bladed body, devoid of the internal kinetic tension needed to absorb the blow, shattered. His form violently disassembled, his constituent weapons scattering across the dimension, and his chaotic consciousness was forcefully yanked back down the Abyssal rifts by the sheer gravitational pull of the strike.

 VI. The Current Era: The Rebuilding Beast

Today, Valerka is trapped within the Abyss, sealed behind the White Wall orchestrated by Malthus.

He is no longer the massive, hulking juggernaut of the 2nd Holy War. Because his physical components were scattered across the upper dimensions, he was forced to rebuild his body using the degraded, rusted scrap of fallen demons and the petrified bone of the lower Abyss. 

He is currently a fraction of his former size, though still incredibly lethal. He spends his eternity fighting the other lesser Demon Lords, constantly trying to absorb enough kinetic energy to regain his former mass. He despises Lucifuge, blaming the Lord of Fate for allowing him to face Titus alone. 

 VII. The Prophecy of the Last Charge

The most violent of the surviving mortal warlords revere a twisted aspect of Valerka, knowing that pure violence is sometimes the only answer to absolute tyranny. They believe in the Prophecy of the Last Charge.

The prophecy states that the Hybrid King will not be able to defeat the Seraphic Vanguard (led by Titus) through strategy alone. The Vanguard's armor is too thick, their discipline too absolute. The King will require a weapon of pure, unadulterated chaos.

It is written that the Hybrid King will temporarily breach the White Wall and challenge Valerka in the depths of the Abyss. The King will not fight him; he will offer him a Covenant of Vengeance. 

The King will open a localized, unidirectional rift directly from the Abyss into the heart of Titus’s forge on the Ascendant Plane. Valerka, sensing the presence of the Monarch who shattered his original form, will accept the Covenant. 

Valerka will not fight alongside the Hybrid King. He will simply charge through the rift, crashing into the Ascendant Plane like a meteor of rust and bone. The resulting explosion of pure violence will shatter the Seraphic defensive lines, creating the necessary chaos for the Hybrid King's final assault, and finally granting Valerka the rematch he has spent millennia preparing for.



 Physical Attributes (Estimated):
- Form: Hulking amalgamation of rusted weaponry, bone, and boiling blood. No face, maw of grinding blades.
- Armament: Subconscious Kinetic Infusion & The Shrapnel Storm.
- Primary Domain: Pure Violence, Kinetic Overload, and Unrestrained Force.
- Current Location: The Middle Rings of the Abyss (Rebuilding/Brawling).
