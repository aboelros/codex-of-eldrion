 Xerxes: The Lord of Mutilation

 I. The Corruption of Biology

Juno (The Weaver of Blood) engineered the 10 Primordial Races to be structurally perfect, weaving their Aetheric potential seamlessly into their physical DNA. However, when Zenithos attacked Juno with the God Blight during the latter stages of the Ascendancy to punish her, the raw, rotting genetic material from her laboratories bled downward, trickling into the Abyss. 

Xerxes was born from this specific corruption. He is the Abyssal mirror to Juno. Where Juno used biology to create life, Xerxes uses it as a canvas for agony. He is the Lord of Mutilation, Surgical Horror, and Biological Redesign.

His manifestation is a calculated nightmare. Unlike the chaotic forms of Valerka or Abyssos, Xerxes appears highly intentional. He resembles an impossibly tall, slender, multi-armed surgeon clad in an apron of stitched, tanned mortal skin over a framework of exposed, perfectly polished steel and bone. His "face" is a smooth, featureless dome of glass, behind which sloshes a luminescent, putrid-yellow fluid. His six hands end not in fingers, but in a dazzling, terrifying array of surgical instruments: scalpels, bone-saws, spreaders, and Aether-extracting syringes, all vibrating with dark magic.

 II. The Architecture of Modification

Xerxes does not kill his victims immediately. To kill is to end the experiment. His domain revolves around the horrific alteration of form to maximize suffering and subjugation.

 Flesh-Smithing
Xerxes possesses the ability to instantly manipulate the biological structure of any living entity. By plunging his scalpels into a victim, he can rewrite their DNA in real-time. He can fuse the victim’s joints together, invert their ribcages, or wire their pain receptors directly into their ocular nerves, ensuring that the simple act of "seeing" causes unbearable agony. 

 The Chimeric Fusions
His most devastating technique is Chimeric Fusion. Xerxes views the different races as mere spare parts. During the Demonic Invasions, he would often capture entirely different species an Elven mage, a Giant warrior, and a Pyrian noble and physically sew their bodies and Soul Cores together while keeping them completely conscious. The resulting creatures, known as "Abominations," were driven mad by the contradictory Aetheric signals of their fused souls, lashing out violently against their former allies.

 III. The 2nd Holy War: The Surgeon of the Battlefield

Lucifuge utilized Xerxes not as a frontline shock troop, but as a terror weapon. Xerxes was deployed behind the front lines, targeting the medical encampments and refugee caravans of the 10 Primordial Races.

When Xerxes descended upon an encampment, he worked with blinding, silent speed. He did not emit a roar; the only sound was the high-speed whirring of his bone-saws and the wet tearing of flesh. He would transform hundreds of wounded soldiers and terrified civilians into structurally impossible, screaming Abominations within minutes. He would then point these horrors back toward the mortal front lines.

The psychological impact of Xerxes's work was the primary reason several mortal kingdoms surrendered during the 2nd Holy War. Warlords calculated that an honorable death at the hands of Valerka was infinitely preferable to being captured alive by the Surgeon. 

 IV. The rivalry with Silas

Xerxes's reign of surgical terror eventually drew the attention of Silas, the Sentinel of Resonance, the greatest bio-engineer of the mortal races (and the eventual architect behind the Hybrid King’s genetic success). 

Silas recognized that Xerxes was using a corrupted version of Juno’s original genetic code. The two engaged in a bizarre, horrifying proxy war of biology. 

When Xerxes unleashed a new strain of Abominations capable of exhaling corrosive acid, Silas engineered a genetic counter-virus that he retro-actively spliced into the Vanguard’s bloodline, neutralizing the acid upon contact. When Silas designed the Aegis Mirrors (which eventually defeated Kyra), Xerxes retaliated by engineering demons with faceted, infinitely absorbent retinas capable of swallowing the redirected light.

Their conflict climaxed during the Siege of the 7th Dimension. Silas did not attempt to fight Xerxes directly. Instead, Silas lured Xerxes into a specialized, sterile bio-chamber. 

Silas triggered an Aetheric Autoclave a massive pulse of absolute, uncorrupted, high-frequency Light (utilizing shards of Luminaria). The autoclave did not burn Xerxes's physical form; it sterilized his concept. It forcibly standardized the chaotic, mutating DNA within Xerxes's yellow fluid, temporarily paralyzing his ability to Flesh-Smith. 

While Xerxes was paralyzed, the Vanguard forced him backward into the Abyssal rifts before the White Wall was sealed.

 V. The Confinement: The Flesh Pits

Trapped within the Abyss, Xerxes found himself starved for fresh mortal canvas. 

He established his domain in the Flesh Pits a massive, subterranean amphitheater carved into the bedrock of hell. Because he cannot harvest fresh mortals, Xerxes is forced to experiment on the lesser demons and the Grotesques originally created by Knull. 

He constantly dissects and rebuilds the demonic horde, trying to create the "Perfect Predator" capable of breaching the White Wall structurally. His domain is arguably the most horrific location in the Abyss a place of endless, echoing screams where dimensions of bone are shattered and re-knit thousands of times a day in the pursuit of twisted perfection. 

He views Knull not as a rival, but as a clumsy amateur who merely glued souls together, whereas he, Xerxes, intertwines their very strands of existence. 

 VI. The Prophecy of the Perfect Vessel

The darkest texts of the Bio-Synths (mortal scientists who worship the corrupted Weaver, Juno) speak of the Prophecy of the Perfect Vessel.

They believe that Xerxes’s millennia of experimentation in the Flesh Pits are not entirely pointless. The prophecy states that Xerxes will eventually succeed in engineering a single, flawless, biological vessel out of pure, compressed Demon-flesh a body lacking a soul but possessing physical parameters that rival Titus the Unbroken.

It is written that during the Hybrid King’s descent into the Abyss, Xerxes will not attack the King. Xerxes will offer him the Vessel. 

The prophecy claims that the Hybrid King, knowing that his mortal (albeit enhanced) body cannot physically withstand the absolute zero vacuum of Malthus’s upper-level defenses, will accept the Covenant. 

The King will allow Xerxes to surgically extract his dual-natured Soul Core and implant it into the Perfect Demonic Vessel. For one glorious, horrifying moment, Xerxes will achieve his masterpiece: he will have successfully operated on a god. 

However, the prophecy concludes by stating that immediately after the surgery is complete, the sheer, unadulterated power of the Hybrid King’s core operating within the perfect vessel will generate a backlash of pure biological supremacy. The resulting shockwave will permanently sterilize Xerxes, fusing his surgical instruments into solid blocks of dead steel and leaving the Lord of Mutilation incapable of ever cutting flesh again.



 Physical Attributes (Estimated):
- Form: Slender humanoid draped in stitched skin. Glass dome for a head filled with putrid yellow fluid. Six arms ending in whirring, rusted surgical instruments.
- Armament: Subconscious Flesh-Smithing, Aetheric Scalpels.
- Primary Domain: Mutilation, Biological Horror, Eugenics, and Chimeric Fusion.
- Current Location: The Flesh Pits (Experimenting on Grotesques).

 Archivist Note:
"Most demons want to kill you. Xerxes considers death to be a waste of good material. He looks at a living, breathing person and sees nothing but a puzzle that was assembled incorrectly. The true horror of Xerxes is not the pain he inflicts, but the cold, clinical silence with which he works. He will turn you into a monster, and he won't even have the decency to hate you while he does it."
