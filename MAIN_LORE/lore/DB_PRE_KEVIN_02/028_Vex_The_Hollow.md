 Vex: The Hollow

 I. The Phenomenon of Aetheric Starvation

The Abyss, despite its churning chaos, possesses a twisted ecosystem. At the top of the food chain sit the Demon Lords, gorging on the corrupted Aether of the fallen Titans to power their immense conceptual domains. Below them are the Grotesques, feeding on the despair of trapped mortal souls. However, there exists a horrific sub-strata unique to the demonic realm: the phenomena of absolute starvation.

Vex is not a true entity in the traditional sense. It has no Ego, no soul, and no true intelligence. Vex is a localized vacuum of Aetheric need, birthed from the sheer conceptual absence left behind when Nihilia collapsed into the Abyssal Maw. 

As a sentient "Hunger," Vex constantly attempts to fill a void that structurally cannot be filled. It lacks the capacity to process Mana or Aether into sustenance; it merely absorbs and destroys it. Vex manifests as an entity composed of jagged, shifting glass-like material that acts as a prismatic void, reflecting the terrified visages of its victims while absorbing any light, heat, or magic that strikes its surface.

 II. The Architecture of the Void Parasite

Vex does not conquer; it infests. It is a parasite that latches onto sources of Aether and drains them dry, leaving behind brittle, colorless husks.

 The Null-Vortex
While Abyssos utilized the Null-Flame to burn concepts, Vex utilizes the Null-Vortex to drain sheer volume. When Vex targets a magical conduit such as an Empathic Leyline or the Soul Core of an unfortunate Demon it initiates a forced syphon. The syphon ignores magical resistance because it is not an attack; it is the physical law of a high-pressure system emptying into a vacuum.

 The Glass Infection
Vex does not have traditional physical attacks. When its glass-like limbs strike a target, the edges do not cut; they infect. The crystalline void material actively rewrites the target’s Aetheric biology, turning their flesh and muscle into the same hungry, brittle glass. This infection rapidly spreads until the victim is completely crystallized, at which point Vex shatters them, consuming the remaining latent energy trapped inside the shards. 

 III. The 2nd Holy War: The Inadvertent Vanguard

During the catastrophic demonic invasions of the 2nd Holy War, the Demon Lords initially attempted to command Vex, viewing it as a powerful weapon against the Monarchs' magic. They were wrong. Vex cannot be commanded because a vacuum does not follow orders. 

When the Abyssal rifts opened, Vex surged forward not to conquer the heavens, but to feed on the massive concentration of Aether within the mortal realms. It was an inadvertent vanguard. 

Vex descended upon the heavily magically-fortified Elven enclaves of the 4th Dimension. The Elves, accustomed to fighting elemental threats, unleashed devastating barrages of thermal and kinetic magic. Vex simply absorbed the barrage. The magic fueled its Null-Vortex, allowing the entity to expand exponentially in size and reach. 

Vex did not engage in combat. It simply moved slowly through the Elven cities, its crystalline infection turning entire populations into frozen, glass statues in mid-stride. It was a silent, terrifying apocalypse of raw consumption that threatened to consume the entire dimension simply by existing within it.

 IV. The Sacrifice of the Crystal Guard 

The Monarchs, currently distracted by Abyssos and the true Demon Lords, ignored the threat of Vex, viewing the destruction of the Elven cities as an acceptable loss. 

The defense against Vex fell to a specialized order of mortal knights known as the Crystal Guard, formed by the remaining free Elves and allied Iron Born. They realized that you cannot kill a vacuum by throwing energy into it.

The Crystal Guard utilized a suicidal tactic. They intentionally severed their own Soul Cores from the ambient Mana of the environment, a process known as "The Severance," which caused immediate cellular death within hours. By severing their cores, they successfully turned their own bodies into Aetheric blanks.

When the Crystal Guard charged Vex, the entity attempted to syphon them, but found nothing to drain. The Glass Infection failed to take hold because the Guard possessed no internal magic to rewrite. The Guard managed to physically isolate Vex, trapping the entity within a massive containment field constructed not of magic, but of raw, untreated lead and petrified wood materials lacking any Aetheric resonance.

Vex, cut off from any source of Magic, began to rapidly shrink as its internal vacuum collapsed inward. 

 V. The Ascendancy: The Warden's Pet

The Crystal Guard succeeded in containing Vex, but they all perished from the fatal effects of their self-inflicted Severance. Vex remained trapped in the containment field, reduced to the size of a small, jagged crystal. 

Following the Apex Rebellion and the splintering of the dimensions, Zenithos became aware of the contained entity. Zenithos, ever pragmatic, saw utility in the parasite. 

He did not destroy Vex. He offered it to Jael, the brutal Warden of the 10th Dimension.

 The Hound of the 10th
Jael is the Warden who utilizes extreme time manipulation and localized gravity distortion to crush his subjects. However, Jael is paranoid about magic-users within his domain developing Covenants capable of resisting his distorted physics. 

Jael took the contained Vex and subjected it to the same corruptive eugenics that Juno used to twist the mortal races. He engineered a collar of pure Odisian dark matter to keep Vex permanently starved but firmly under his control. 

Today, Vex is known as the Hound of the 10th. It is no longer a massive apocalypse, but a localized terror. 

When Jael detects a rogue mage or a pocket of resistance within his dimension, he unleashes Vex. The entity moves with terrifying speed through Jael's time-distorted landscapes. It falls upon the magic-users, its Null-Vortex instantly draining their Soul Cores and turning them to brittle glass. 

 VI. The Prophecy of the Shattered Mirror

Vex is essentially an attack dog for a tyrant, but the Archivists believe it is also a ticking time bomb. 

The prophecy regarding Vex is short and terrifying: "The mirror cannot hold the void forever."

The scholars theorize that the dark matter collar Jael uses to control Vex is slowly degrading under the constant strain of the entity's Null-Vortex. Furthermore, every time Vex consumes a rogue mage, a microscopic fraction of their Aether is trapped within Vex's crystalline structure, slowly building up internal pressure. 

It is written that during the final push by the Hybrid King, Jael will become desperate and unleash Vex against the King's Vanguard. 

The Hybrid King, utilizing the resonance techniques of his ancestors, will not attack Vex with magic. He will strike the dark matter collar precisely on its degraded fault line with a burst of pure acoustic force. 

When the collar breaks, Vex will instantly attempt to rapidly expand to consume the environmental Mana. But the sudden influx of chaotic magic will violently interact with the millions of trapped magical shards already inside Vex. The resulting explosion will not just kill the entity; it will trigger a chain reaction of total Aetheric detonation that will glass the entirety of the 10th Dimension, taking Warden Jael with it. 



 Physical Attributes (Estimated):
- Form: A jagged, shifting mass of crystalline void-glass that reflects terror and absorbs energy.
- Armament: Null-Vortex Syphon & The Glass Infection.
- Primary Domain: Aetheric Starvation, Magical Consumption, and Void Parasitism.
- Current Location: The 10th Dimension (Enslaved by Warden Jael).

 Archivist Note:
"We fear the Demon Lords because they are powerful and cruel. We should fear Vex because it is nothing. It does not hate us; it does not even recognize we are alive. It only recognizes that we are fuel. Jael thinks he has tamed a beast, but you cannot put a leash on a black hole. When the collar breaks, there will be no one left to sweep up the glass."
