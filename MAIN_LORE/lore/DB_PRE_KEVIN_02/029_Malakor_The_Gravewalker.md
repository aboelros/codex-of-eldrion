 Malakor: The Gravewalker

 I. The Necrotic Origin

The creation of the Soul Core by Juno and Aetherion was intended to be a one-way biological battery. When the physical body (the vessel) sustained critical damage, the Soul Core tether would snap, and the Aether within the core would safely dissolve into the environmental Mana pool, rendering the body inert. 

However, during the catastrophic influx of Titan Aether into the Abyss following the 1st Holy War, this fundamental biological law was corrupted. The lingering, shattered "Will to Exist" of Aetherion saturated the deepest trenches of hell. 

Malakor was born from this specific corruption. He is not a mutated mortal soul like Knull, nor an embodiment of rage like Abyssos. Malakor is the Lord of Necromancy and False Resurrection. He is composed entirely of the residual Aether of billions of severed soul tethers that drifted into the Abyss instead of dissolving cleanly. 

He manifested as a skeletal, regal entity draped in rotting, ornate burial shrouds. His skull was fused with jagged, obsidian stalactites resembling a crown, and he radiated a cold, green Aetheric flame the sickly, inverted light of false life. He carried a massive, rusted scythe known as the Tether-Sever, capable of cutting strings that had already been cut, further splintering the soul.

 II. The Architecture of Necromancy

Malakor 'invented' Necromancy. Before him, the dead stayed dead. His power was derived from forcing biological Aether engines to run in reverse. 

 The False Tether
Malakor’s primary ability was the creation of the "False Tether." When a biological entity died, he could plunge his corrupted Aether into the cooling corpse and forcefully re-ignite the physical remnants of the Soul Core. He could not bring back the soul (the ego, memories, and identity); he only brought back the engine. The resulting creature was an undead thrall a mindless, rotting puppet powered by the agonizing friction of inverted Aether.

 The Bone-Smithing
He also possessed an inherent mastery over calcium and bone density. He could utilize the latent Lithic (gravity/mass) resonance remaining in the bones of the dead to construct massive, organic siege engines, towers, and armor entirely out of fused skeletons. He viewed graveyards not as monuments to the past, but as arsenals waiting to be activated.

 III. The 2nd Holy War: The General of the Dead

During the 2nd Holy War, when the Demon Lords breached the upper dimensions, Malakor commanded the most terrifying and psychologically devastating armies. 

The other Demon Lords required Cults and Void-Callers to summon their minions, or they had to pull feral demons from the Abyss. Malakor simply raised his armies on site. As the Monarchs' mortal loyalists fell in battle, Malakor immediately wove his False Tethers, turning the fallen soldiers against their living comrades within seconds of their deaths.

The psychological impact on the 10 Primordial Races was profound. To fight Malakor was to literally fight your own fathers, brothers, and sons, their rotting mouths slack, their eyes glowing with cold green fire. 

The Elves, specifically, were horrified by his magic, as their culture deeply revered the cycle of life and the natural return of Aether to the environment. Malakor explicitly targeted Elven cemeteries in the 4th Dimension, ripping thousands of years of peaceful ancestors from their rest to form his elite vanguard. 

 IV. The Pact with the Traitor

Malakor’s most significant impact on the history of the universe occurred not through conquest, but through a corrupted Covenant. 

During the later stages of the 4th Holy War (The Apex Rebellion), as the Hybrid King’s forces seemed poised to breach the Divine Apex, a mortal commander in the Hybrid King's army grew envious. This commander, later known only as Warden Kael, realized that even if they won, he would be secondary to the King. 

Malakor, sensing the profound ambition and fear of death within Kael, reached out through a minor dimensional tear. He offered Kael a devastating Covenant. 

In exchange for sabotaging the rebellion’s defenses and ensuring the Hybrid King's capture, Malakor offered to teach Kael the secrets of the False Tether. 

Kael accepted. He betrayed his own troops, dropping the Aetheric shields protecting the Vanguard's flank, which allowed Zenithos and Titus to decimate the rebellion. 

Following the splintering of the dimensions, Zenithos rewarded the traitor by making him the Warden of the 11th Dimension. However, Kael had kept his end of the Abyssal bargain. The knowledge of Necromancy was firmly planted in the physical realm.

 V. The Ascendancy: The Silent Betrayal

Malakor’s brilliant tactical move backfired spectacularly.

He had intended to use Kael as a puppet to conquer the 11th Dimension from the inside out once the Abyssal rifts were reopened. However, he underestimated mortal ambition augmented by divine power. Kael, now wielding the absolute authority of a Zenithos-appointed Warden and the knowledge of Necromancy, did not serve Malakor. He usurped him.

Warden Kael utilized his new power to forcefully drain the latent necrotic Aether from the Abyssal breaches lingering in his dimension. He essentially stole Malakor's battery source. 

When the "White Wall" finally permanently sealed the Abyss, Malakor was trapped below, cut off from the physical graves he loved to harvest, while Kael ruled the most chaotic, death-ravaged dimension above, using Malakor’s stolen magic to build an empire of undeath.

 VI. The Current Era: The Chained Gravewalker

Today, Malakor is a humiliated, furious entity trapped in the lower rungs of the Abyss.

He no longer has vast armies, because the souls falling into the Abyss are aggressively consumed by Knull or dissolved by the Corrosive Sea before Malakor can reliably raise them. He is forced to scavenge the most degraded, pulverized bones at the bottom of the pit just to maintain a small, pathetic retinue of feral bone-golems. 

His hatred for Warden Kael completely eclipses his hatred for Zenithos or the Seraphim. He views Kael as a plagiarist and a thief. Malakor's green necrotic flame flickers weakly now, barely capable of sustaining his own manifestation. 

 VII. The Prophecy of the Last True Death

The death-cults that still secretly operate in the 11th Dimension under Kael's brutal rule believe that the Warden's empire of undeath is built on a stolen hourglass, and the sand is running out.

The prophecy claims that the False Tethers powering Warden Kael’s undead armies are fundamentally flawed because they lack Malakor’s true "author's signature." It is written that the Hybrid King will eventually breach both the 11th Dimension and the Abyss simultaneously through an act of supreme dimensional resonance. 

The King will locate the weakened Malakor and form a temporary, iron-clad Covenant. The King will offer the Gravewalker three minutes of absolute, unfettered access to the 11th Dimension. 

In those three minutes, Malakor will surge upward. He will use his mastery to violently rip the False Tethers out of every single undead thrall in Warden Kael’s army simultaneously, recalling the stolen magic back into himself. Kael's empire will turn to dust in an instant.

The prophecy states that Malakor will then strike down the traitorous Warden with the Tether-Sever. However, because the Covenant with the Hybrid King is strictly time-bound, Malakor will be instantly dragged back into the Abyss the moment the three minutes expire. 

He will return to the dark, having finally executed the thief, leaving the 11th Dimension swept clean and ready for true life to bloom once again from the ash.



 Physical Attributes (Estimated):
- Form: Skeletal, regal humanoid. Fused obsidian crown. Emits cold, green necrotic flame.
- Armament: The Tether-Sever (Rusted, corrupted scythe).
- Primary Domain: Necromancy, False Resurrection, Bone-Smithing, and Undeath.
- Current Location: The Lower Scavenging Grounds of the Abyss (Humiliated/Weakened).

 Archivist Note:
"There is a dignity in letting the dead rest. Malakor offended the fundamental cycle of the universe by forcing engines to run when their fuel was spent. But the universe has a chaotic sense of humor. The God of Undeath was robbed by a mortal grave-robber. Now Kael wears Malakor’s stolen crown, and the lord of the graveyard is reduced to picking through the trash."
