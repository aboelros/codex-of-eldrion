 Classification: Pack-Hunting Gravitational Predators | Status: Dispersed / Adapted | Origin: Peripheral Dimensional Zones

---

 I. Gravitational Tides and the Hunt

While Gravitational Aether Arts (see: SYS_02/17_Gravitational_Aether_Arts.md) manipulate mass through intense metaphysical calculation, the universe itself naturally generates vast, shifting gravitational fields. High-mass celestial bodies moons, drifting planetoids, and specifically the massive debris fields of the shattered dimensions create immense gravitational "tides" that pull and distort the localized Aetheric fields.

The Lunar Wolves are the apex predators that evolved specifically to hunt within these gravitational tides.

They are massive, lupine entities, typically matching the size of a heavy terrestrial cavalry mount. However, their defining feature is their complete lack of biological kinetic friction. A Lunar Wolf does not run by pushing its paws against the ground. It runs by continuously exploiting microscopic localized gravity wells, "falling" forward along the gravitational tide rather than exerting muscular effort.

Because they move entirely via a frictionless slide along the gravitational spectrum, a hunting pack of Lunar Wolves is perfectly, terrifyingly silent. They do not generate footfalls. They do not displace air. They are shadows that ride the pull of the moon.

---

 II. The Physiology of the Pack

The Lunar Wolves do not possess a standard cellular structure. They are biological manifestations of condensed gravitational pressure wrapped around a specialized, highly predatory Soul Core. 

Their "fur" is a bristling layer of microscopic spatial distortions. If a practitioner attempts to strike a Lunar Wolf with a physical blade, the blade will abruptly curve around the Wolf's body, pulled off trajectory by the entity's personal gravity shield. A direct kinetic strike requires immense, overwhelming force simply to bypass the localized spatial curve covering the beast.

They hunt in synchronized packs numbering anywhere from six to fifty members. Their hunting mechanism relies on isolating prey and systematically layering their individual gravity wells on top of one another. By circling a target, the pack creates an intense, localized singularity. The prey is not bitten or mauled; the prey is simply crushed to death by the sudden, overwhelming weight of the localized gravity well the pack has generated around them.

The Lunar Wolves feed on the highly concentrated Aether released when the prey's Soul Core ruptures under the pressure.

---

 III. The Ancestral Progenitors and the Lycan Lineage

The history of the 13 Dimensions notes several distinct instances where human or localized populations successfully adapted the physics of a Primordial Beast into their own biological architecture (e.g., the Iron Born utilizing the Dying Star material of the Dragons).

The Lycans (frequently mischaracterized in modern folklore as "werewolves") are the direct evolutionary consequence of forced biological integration with the Lunar Wolves. 

During the chaos of the 1st Holy War, isolated tribes living on the dimensional peripheries were subjected to intense gravitational bombardment by the Monarchs. To survive the crushing pressure, these tribes utilized extreme Tier 3 existential Covenants (see: SYS_02/11_The_Mechanics_of_Covenants.md), binding their own biology to the localized Lunar Wolf packs to adopt their gravitational resilience.

The resulting lineage the Lycans did not become monsters. They became highly elite, gravity-resistant infantry capable of navigating environments that would physically crush standard humans. However, the Covenant permanently bound their internal Aetheric rhythms to the shifting gravitational tides of the celestial bodies, enforcing a biological cycle reliant on lunar positioning.

---

 IV. The Dark Age Dispersal

Following the Splintering of the Dimensions and the establishment of the Seraphic Dampeners, the vast, inter-dimensional gravitational tides the Lunar Wolves relied on for migration were severely disrupted. 

The Seraphic Dampeners create massive localized "flat" spots in the gravitational field, effectively building invisible walls that the Wolves cannot frictionlessly slide through. 

Consequently, the great packs were broken up and dispersed across the isolated dimensions. They are no longer the apex predators of the cosmic tides. In the Dark Age, they act as highly territorial, fiercely defensive scavengers, hoarding the small pockets of remaining stable gravitational energy deep within the highland forests or shattered archipelagos.

The Wardens occasionally utilize specialized "Wolf-Kill" units. Because the Wolves hunt by sensing gravity, the Seraphic hunting units utilize anti-gravity Relics to mask their approach, attempting to isolate and exterminate the packs to harvest their highly condensed Soul Cores for the Mana engines. 

The mortal resistance (specifically the surviving Lycan operatives) frequently attempts to run interference on these hunts, recognizing that the Lunar Wolves are one of the few remaining natural defenses against Seraphic patrols. A Seraph operating a dampener engine disrupts the gravity field; if a Lunar Wolf senses that artificial disruption, its instinct is to immediately hunt the source to eliminate the anomaly.

 Archivist Note: The Silence in the Trees
The Highland scouts teach a very specific survival protocol regarding the deep woods of the 6th Dimension. If you are walking through the timber, and you suddenly feel an inexplicable lightness in your step if your pack suddenly feels weightless, and the leaves falling from the trees are dropping sideways instead of straight down do not run. You cannot outrun a creature that moves by falling. Stand perfectly still, compress your Aetheric output to absolute zero, and pray that the pack determines your gravitational signature is too insignificant to be worth crushing. The Wolves are starving. A starving predator does not care if you belong to the resistance or the Seraphim. It only cares about the weight of your soul.
