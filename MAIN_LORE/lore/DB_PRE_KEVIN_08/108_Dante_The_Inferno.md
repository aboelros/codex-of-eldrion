 I. What the Harvesting Facilities Did

The Mana Harvest infrastructure   Ophion's system for draining the lower dimensions of life energy to feed the ascended Gods   operated through physical installations distributed across all eleven dimensions: Harvesting Facilities, each one a Seraphic-administered station where the extraction process ran continuously.

The Harvesting Facility in the 3rd Dimension's western archipelago zone was, by the extraction yield records that post-war intelligence recovered, the single highest-output facility in the eleven dimensions. The specific geological Mana concentration in the 3rd Dimension's deep aquifer systems   the systems that Cylia's pressure governance had access to   produced a yield rate at the western archipelago installation approximately 3.4 times the standard dimensional average.

The facility's external appearance, as documented in the resistance's reconnaissance records: a complex of enclosed structures built into the elevated coastal cliffs of the archipelago's central island, presenting to the surrounding ocean as a series of sealed processing units connected by enclosed transit infrastructure. To the casual observer from the water   and most observers in the 3rd Dimension's aquatic environment were at the water level   the facility was industrial architecture of a type the Seraphic administration used across many functional contexts.

Inside: extraction arrays, operating continuously, drawing Mana from the geological substrate of the archipelago through a network of deep-bore installations that ran from the facility's basement levels down into the specific aquifer concentrations below.

The deep-bore installations were the facility's technical core. The extraction arrays were the processing mechanism. The facility's output reached Ophion's collection infrastructure through the dimensional transfer system that the Seraphic engineering team had built in the facility's uppermost level.

Dante had been inside the facility. Three times, as a maintenance worker in the cover identity the 3rd Dimension's resistance network had built for him over two years.

He had memorized the deep-bore installation architecture.

 II. The Pyrian Combustion Condition

The Pyrian Soul Core architecture   Ignis Prime's legacy, the race that had learned to sustain their flames without consuming the world around them   had a specific biological characteristic that the standard racial taxonomy documented as both the race's greatest asset and its most carefully managed constraint: the Pyrian Soul Core, unlike every other racial variant, had no internal ceiling on Aetheric output in the thermal domain.

Other racial Soul Core variants had architectural limits that prevented output above a certain threshold. The limit was Juno's design choice: operational ceilings that prevented practitioners from exceeding sustainable levels without the ceiling-approach expansion that required sustained high-load practice. The ceiling existed because exceeding it produced permanent architectural damage.

The Pyrian thermal output architecture had a ceiling. The ceiling was not a hard limit on thermal generation   it was a threshold beyond which the biological architecture required active suppression to prevent runaway generation. The Pyrian practice tradition had developed the specific Covenant technique for managing the thermal output boundary: a suppression system integrated into daily practice, maintained as automatically as breathing, that continuously regulated the thermal generation below the runaway threshold.

The threshold, once crossed deliberately, produced a specific event that the Pyrian tradition called the Furnace State: runaway thermal generation that the biological architecture could not contain, producing exponentially increasing thermal output until the biological architecture itself was consumed.

It was not death in the conventional sense. It was conversion: all biological energy, all Soul Core energy, all Aetheric capacity   converted to thermal output in a single sustained release.

The Furnace State produced, in a sufficiently developed Pyrian practitioner, a thermal event of specific and enormous magnitude. Dante was sufficiently developed.

 III. The Calculation

The resistance's strategic analysis of the western archipelago facility had identified it as a priority target for two years before Dante's operation. The extraction yield of 3.4 times the dimensional average meant the facility contributed disproportionately to Ophion's collection total. Destroying it would reduce the Gods' Mana supply at a rate that other dimensions' facilities could not compensate for on a short timeline.

The facility's resilience to conventional resistance sabotage methods was the obstacle. The Seraphic engineering team had built the facility with specific anti-sabotage redundancy into every critical system. The deep-bore installations had triple-redundant thermal protection   a design choice that made the Pyrian-tradition thermal weapons the most obvious sabotage approach specifically the most defended against.

Dante's analysis, which he presented to the resistance's strategic council, was specific: conventional thermal weapons at the facility's perimeter would not reach the deep-bore core. The facility's thermal protection would absorb conventional external thermal attacks. What the thermal protection was calibrated to defend against was external thermal input.

It was not calibrated to defend against internal thermal generation.

A practitioner inside the facility, in the specific location of the deep-bore installation core, in the Furnace State.

The council's response: silence, of the specific kind that occurs when a plan is presented that is clearly effective and clearly involves the proposal's author not returning.

Dante was aware of this. He presented it as a practical question: the Furnace State would destroy the facility. No other available approach would. His specific genetic profile   assessed by the council's medical team as the highest thermal output potential among the resistance's Pyrian practitioners   made him the specific individual whose Furnace State would be sufficient.

He was thirty-one years old. He had been practicing at the ceiling since he was fourteen.

 IV. The Last Three Days

Dante spent the three days before the operation doing things that the archive's biographical research has documented from the accounts of the people he spent them with.

Day one: he visited his younger sister, who was living in the 3rd Dimension's coastal network's safe community. The account of the visit is from the sister's own testimony, given to the archive's biographical team fifteen years after liberation: "He came for the whole day. We cooked. He was better at cooking than me. He always had been. He didn't say anything about what was happening. We just cooked all day. I found out afterward. He made three dishes and we ate all of them. I had always thought there would be more days like that."

Day two: he wrote twelve letters. The archive has eleven of them   one was lost in the transit network. The eleven letters are addressed to specific individuals in the resistance's network and contain the specific personal content that a person writes when they know they will not be present for the responses.

Day three: he prepared.

The preparation was technical: the deep-bore installation core's specific location required him to reach it in his maintenance worker cover identity, pass through the facility's standard internal monitoring checkpoints, reach the core, and then execute the Furnace State from inside the most thermally protected zone of the most protected facility in the 3rd Dimension.

He arrived at the facility at 06:00. He reached the deep-bore installation core at 06:47. The Seraphic facility operations log   recovered by post-war intelligence   records an abnormal thermal generation signature detected at 06:51.

The log ends at 06:52.

The facility was not destroyed cleanly   the thermal event at the deep-bore core produced sequential failures in the extraction arrays, the transfer infrastructure, and the surface facilities over approximately three minutes, with the geological substrate's Mana concentration itself igniting at the aquifer level under the thermal input's specific intensity.

The western archipelago facility ceased operations permanently. The archipelago's geological substrate in the facility zone required a post-war environmental restoration project to address the thermal alteration of the aquifer formations.
