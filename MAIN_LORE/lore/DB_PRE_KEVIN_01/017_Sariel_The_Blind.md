 Sariel THE BLIND: The God of Regret 

 I. The Observer of the Heavens

Among the 12 Original Monarchs, there were tyrants like Zenithos, administrators like Ophion, and architects of illusion like Vytas. But the Titans understood that a universe cannot be governed solely by control and manipulation. It required an entity capable of emotional resonance, a being designed to observe the pain and joy of the mortals and ensure that the rigid laws of physics did not crush the fragile biological life they supported. 

Sariel was crafted from a massive infusion of Luminaria’s pure light and Aion’s deep, historical empathy. He was the Monarch of Observation and Resonance. He was not given a domain of destruction or creation, but rather the domain of understanding.

Sariel’s physical form was breathtaking. He appeared as a towering, androgynous figure possessing six wings composed entirely of cascading, kaleidoscopic light. His eyes, before they were taken from him, were said to hold the reflection of every star in the 13 dimensions. He was the gentlest of the Monarchs, often spending his time walking invisibly among the mortals, experiencing their brief, brilliant lives.

He did not interfere. His mandate was observation. When a civilization rose, he watched with quiet pride; when it fell, he stood in the ruins and wept light. 

 II. The Architecture of Empathy

Sariel’s function was not passive; his observation influenced the very fabric of reality. 

 The Resonance of the Soul Core
Sariel is responsible for the deep, emotional connectivity of the Aether. Before Sariel, a Soul Core was merely a biological battery designed by Aetherion. Sariel was the Monarch who caused the Aether to react to emotion. He tied the strength of a mortal's magic to their passion, their grief, and their hope. He made it so that a mother protecting her child could temporarily generate a shield of Aether far beyond her physical capacity. 

 The Empathic Leylines
He also wove the thin, fragile Empathic Leylines that connected the minds of the 10 Primordial Races. These were not the massive power-conduits of Manisiria, but subtle threads of shared experience. Because of Sariel, art could transcend language, and music could bridge the gap between a Giant and an Elf. He was the god of shared humanity.

 III. The 1st Holy War: The Silent Accomplice

The tragedy of Sariel is the tragedy of inaction. When Zenithos began planning the treason against the Titans, he knew that Sariel could not be convinced with promises of power or eternal life. Sariel did not desire rule; he desired peace. 

Zenithos used Vytas to weave a complex web of lies regarding the Titans' intentions. Vytas projected false futures into Sariel's mind, showing him a universe where the rigid laws of the Titans eventually crushed all mortal life into absolute, motionless stasis. Zenithos argued that the rebellion was not an act of pride, but an act of salvation for the 10 Races. 

Caught between his loyalty to his creators and his profound love for the mortals, Sariel hesitated. He possessed the power to expose Zenithos’s treason to the Titans to shine the absolute light of truth on the conspiracy. But the fear of the false future Vytas had shown him stayed his tongue. 

When the 1st Holy War erupted and the Titans were pulled down, Sariel did not strike a single blow. He stood on the Ascendant Plane and watched. His silence was the ultimate betrayal. He allowed the creators of the universe to be tortured and sealed in the Abyss because he was too afraid to act.

 IV. The Burden of the Truth: The First Rift

Following the war, as Zenithos established the Divine Apex and Ophion began the brutal Grand Siphoning, the scales fell from Sariel’s eyes. The false futures woven by Vytas shattered against the horrific reality of the Monarchs’ reign. 

Sariel watched the 10 Primordial Races being drained of their vitality. He heard the screams of the mortals echoing up the Syphon Leylines. The Empathic Leylines he had created became conductors of pure, unfiltered agony, flooding his mind with the collective suffering of a trillion souls across the 13 dimensions. 

He was the first Monarch to experience Cognitive Dissonance. The other Monarchs rationalized their cruelty or ignored it entirely, their egos shielding them from guilt. But Sariel, designed to feel exactly what the mortals felt, cracked under the weight of his own complicity. 

He confronted Zenithos in the Grand Hall of the Divine Apex. It was the first rift in the divine ranks. Sariel demanded an end to the Siphoning and called for the release of the Titans. He threatened to use his resonance to sever the Monarchs' connection to the mortal Aether, starving them out.

 V. The Blinding of the God

Zenithos, who had just secured total control over the universe, would not tolerate a dissenting voice, especially not one capable of inducing mass empathy. But Zenithos also knew that he could not simply kill Sariel; the Monarchs' combined power was still required to keep the Titans sealed. 

Instead, Zenithos ordered Kyra the Radiant and Malchus the Binder to punish the God of Observation. 

Kyra, utilizing the stolen, corrupted light of Luminaria, focused a beam of absolute, burning radiation directly into Sariel’s eyes. She didn't just burn away his physical retinas; she seared the concept of "Sight" from his Aetheric code. 

Malchus then bound Sariel's brilliant, six-winged form in chains forged from the heavy, suffocating metal of the Abyss. They dragged the blinded, screaming God of Regret into the deepest, darkest dungeon of the Divine Apex a place where the Empathic Leylines could not reach. 

 VI. The Ascendancy and the Tears of Sariel

When Zenithos splintered the dimensions and retreated after the 4th Holy War, he left Sariel chained in the dark. 

Today, Sariel exists as the ultimate prisoner of conscience. He is completely blind, unable to see the universe he loved. He is cut off from the Empathic Leylines, leaving him in absolute, agonizing isolation. He is the Monarch of Regret, drowning in the memory of his own cowardice.

However, even a blinded god can weep. 

Because Sariel was formed from the pure light of Luminaria, his tears are not water. They are drops of condensed, liquid Aether, infused with his profound sorrow and his desperate desire for redemption. These tears occasionally slip through the dimensional cracks of the Ascendant Plane, falling like golden rain onto the 11 segregated dimensions below. 

To mortals, this phenomenon is known as the "Weeping of the Heavens." 

When a Tear of Sariel strikes the earth, it creates a localized Empathic Bloom. For a brief moment, the Seraphic conditioning in the area fails. The Will Severance is broken. Mortals in the vicinity suddenly remember what it feels like to love, to grieve, and to hope without the cold, deadening filter of the Seraphim. 

These Tears are the most highly prized artifacts for the crushed resistance. A single Tear, applied to the brow of a magically conditioned Seraphic slave, can instantly restore their free will. Gabriel’s highest priority is the eradication of these Blooms, deploying entire legions of Inquisitors to glass the earth wherever a Tear falls.

 VII. The Prophecy of the Opened Eyes

There is no glorious battle recorded for Sariel in the ancient prophecies, no grand confrontation with the Hybrid King. The scholars of the resistance believe that Sariel’s role is one of passive salvation. 

The prophecy simply states that when the Hybrid King finally breaches the Ascendant Plane, he will not find a tyrant on the first throne, but a weeping prisoner in the dark. 

It is written that the Hybrid King will take the shattered remnants of his own trauma and press them against Sariel’s blinded eyes. This act of ultimate, shared mortal empathy will reverse the burning inflicted by Kyra thousands of years ago. 

When Sariel opens his eyes again, his gaze will not be one of gentle observation, but of apocalyptic judgment. The sheer, concentrated light of 50,000 years of regret will instantly vaporize the Divine Apex, burning away the false heaven and finally releasing the universe from the grip of the usurpers.
