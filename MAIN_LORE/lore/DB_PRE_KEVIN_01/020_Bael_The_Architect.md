 Bael: The Architect of Segregation

 I. The Cartographer of the Cosmos

If Lithos built the foundation of the universe and Aion set the clock in motion, Bael was the Monarch tasked with drawing the borders. Derived from a complex mixture of Lithos’s mass manipulation and Atmoss’s fluid dynamics, Bael was designated the Monarch of Geography and Dimensional Structure.

Before the betrayal, the 13 Dimensions were not heavily segregated. They overlaid one another, existing as frequencies on the same spatial canvas. A mortal standing in a specific valley in the 1st Dimension might be able to reach out and touch a tree existing in the 5th, provided their Aetheric resonance was attuned correctly. 

Bael’s original purpose was to map these overlapping realities and ensure that the chaotic geographies of the outer dimensions (like the dense, crushing depths of the 10th) did not physically intrude upon the fragile ecosystems of the inner dimensions. 

His manifestation was imposing, though entirely lacking the lethal intent of Zenithos or the vanity of Kyra. Bael appeared as an enormous, multi-armed figure carved from pure white marble and dark obsidian. He carried no weapons, only glowing, floating astrolabes and constantly shifting geometric compasses. He possessed a deeply organized, meticulous mind, obsessed with categorizing and separating the universe into neat, manageable sectors.

 II. The Mastery of Continental Drift

Bael did not just draw maps; he physically altered the landscape of reality. 

 Tectonic Will
He possessed the ability to manipulate the deep subterranean forces of any planetary body. He could raise mountain ranges overnight to serve as natural barriers between warring mortal factions, or he could sink entire landmasses into the sea to quench uncontrollable magical fires started by the Pyrians. He viewed the planets not as living ecosystems, but as clay to be molded into the most efficient structures possible.

 Frequency Shielding
His most vital contribution to the early universe was the implementation of Frequency Shields. These were massive, invisible geographical barriers that prevented the raw, unrefined Mana of the outer dimensions from poisoning the inner ones. Bael was the gatekeeper of the dimensional thresholds, deciding exactly which species were permitted to cross the boundaries.

 III. The 1st Holy War: The Redrawing of the Map

Bael was a crucial component of Zenithos’s rebellion, though he was motivated by order rather than ego. Bael had grown frustrated with the messy, overlapping nature of the Titan’s design. He believed that the 10 Primordial Races were fundamentally incompatible and that leaving them to interact organically only led to chaos and conflict. 

When Zenithos proposed the rebellion, Bael agreed on the condition that he would be allowed to fundamentally redesign the layout of the universe once the Titans were gone. 

During the 1st Holy War, Bael weaponized geography against the Titans’ mortal defenders. 

As the Valkyries and the loyalist mortal armies attempted to march on the Zenithos's forces, Bael simply changed the map. He turned solid plains into bottomless chasms, flipped mountain ranges upside down to crush entire legions, and redirected massive aquifers to drown the staging grounds of the Iron Born. He did not fight; he merely ensured that the enemy could never reach the battlefield.

 IV. The Ascendancy: The Architect of Segregation

Following the imprisonment of the Titans, Bael was given true creative control. Zenithos commanded him to build the Divine Apex the false heaven but Bael’s masterpiece was his brutal redesign of the physical universe below.

To make the mortals easier to harvest (a process managed by Ophion), Bael enacted the Great Segregation. 

He used his mastery over dimensional structure to forcibly untangle the 13 dimensions. He pulled them apart, stripping away the natural thresholds and replacing them with absolute, impassable voids. He isolated the Primordial Races, separating the Pyrians from the Glacians, the Elves from the Giants. 

Bael argued that he was preventing war by keeping the incompatible races apart, but in reality, he was destroying their ability to evolve. By isolating them, he halted the natural cross-pollination of culture and magical theory. The 10 Races began to stagnate, locked into their respective, isolated dimensions, easily managed and harvested by the Seraphim.

 V. The 4th Holy War: The Breaking of the Borders

Bael’s absolute geography remained unchallenged until the rise of the Hybrid King. The anomaly of the King’s soul allowed him to perceive the universe not as Bael had redesigned it, but as it fundamentally was.

The Hybrid King understood that Bael’s voids were not empty space, but artificial constructs. Using the resonance techniques developed by Silas and the probability mathematics of the defected Kaelen, the Hybrid King managed to create temporary, localized breaches in Bael’s segregated dimensions. 

This allowed the 10 Races to finally reunite. The resulting coalition army was a terrifying combination of diverse magical disciplines that Bael had spent thousands of years trying to keep apart. 

During the siege of the Divine Apex, Bael attempted to use his Tectonic Will to crush the coalition beneath the floating islands of the Ascendant Plane. However, the Hybrid King, supported by the Vanguard warlord Garrick, directly opposed Bael's spatial manipulation. While Garrick anchored the landmasses with raw gravitational force, the Hybrid King shattered Bael's astrolabes using concentrated acoustic resonance.

Stripped of his tools and his geographic control, Bael was overwhelmed. He was not a warrior; in hand-to-hand combat, he was quickly dismantled by the unified fury of the Iron Born and the Pyrians.

 VI. The Punishment: The Shattering of the 11th

Bael survived the brutal beating, but his failure enraged Zenithos. Because Bael could not keep the races separated, the Divine Apex had nearly fallen. 

When Zenithos negotiated the betrayal of the "Nameless Traitor" and executed the Hybrid King, he decided that Bael’s method of segregation was insufficient. Zenithos took matters into his own hands and enacted the Dimensional Splintering, violently breaking the universe into the 11 dimensions known today and appointing the Wardens to rule them.

Zenithos blamed Bael for the near-loss of the kingdom. As punishment, Zenithos stripped Bael of his grand title and his ability to manipulate the upper planes. Bael was thrown down into the heavily damaged, magically poisoned realm that would become the 11th Dimension.

 VII. The Current Era: The Mad Warden

To add insult to profound injury, Zenithos appointed Kael, a traitorous, necromancy-wielding commander from the crushed rebellion, as the Warden of the 11th Dimension, placing the former god of geography under the rule of a mortal traitor.

Today, Bael is a broken, mad entity. He exists as a massive, immobile mountain of weeping marble located at the center of the 11th Dimension's wasteland. He has lost all connection to the Ascendant Plane and the other Monarchs.

Warden Kael exploits the paralyzed god mercilessly. Kael uses the residual tectonic power of Bael’s petrified form to constantly churn the earth of the 11th Dimension, unearthing ancient, fossilized Mana for his undead armies to mine. 

Bael, who once desired ultimate order and neat, categorized borders, is now trapped in the most chaotic, death-ravaged dimension in existence. He screams continuously in the language of grinding tectonic plates, a constant, low-frequency earthquake that drives the inhabitants of the 11th Dimension slowly insane. He is the cartographer of the cosmos, reduced to a tool for a grave-robber.

 VIII. The Prophecy of the Final Map

The deeply hidden, almost entirely forgotten texts of the ancient Cartographers' Guild speak of the Restoration of the Canvas.

The prophecy claims that the 11 segregated dimensions are inherently unstable because they were broken by Zenithos's gravity, not separated by Bael's careful geography. It is written that the Hybrid King must eventually travel to the 11th Dimension and shatter the petrified marble shell of Bael.

By freeing the mad god from Warden Kael's control and absorbing the trauma of Bael's fall, the Hybrid King will gain the original, uncorrupted blueprints of the 13 Dimensions. Using these blueprints, the King will not just overthrow the Divine Apex; he will physically sew the universe back together, erasing the artificial borders and restoring the Eternal World as it was originally drawn.
