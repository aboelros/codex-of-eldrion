 I. The Formation

The Void Tear of the 8th Dimension is the archive's most significant documented example of a permanent, large-scale reality fracture produced by cumulative Ley-line structural failure in the post-Splintering era.

Following the Splintering of Dimensions (see: SYS_05/58_The_Splintering_of_Dimensions.md), the catastrophic snapping of millions of Ley-lines left numerous localized zones across the 13 Dimensions with severely compromised reality architecture. The Mana Weavers' ongoing triage operations have stabilized the majority of these damaged zones, either through natural energetic settling or through active Weaver repair work.

A small number of zones were too severely damaged for passive stabilization and Weaver repair was prevented by Seraphic access restrictions. In these locations, the reality fracture continued to expand slowly over centuries until it reached a stable self-sustaining size.

The Void Tear is the largest of these unresolved fractures. Its current diameter is approximately three kilometers at its widest point. It fluctuates between two and four kilometers on a cycle the Oracle monitoring teams have mapped with approximately 80% predictive accuracy.

---

 II. The Behavior

The Void Tear's most visually dramatic property random ingestion and ejection of geological mass is the product of the fracture's interaction with the boundary between dimensional space and the void-space between dimensions.

When the Tear's boundary phase aligns with the external void-space, matter within the three-dimensional zone of the Tear is physically transferred into the void-space channel. When the phase reverses, matter that has been in void-space is returned. The timing of these phase reversals is partially predictable but not fully what the Oracle observation teams can predict is the cycle pattern; what they cannot predict is the specific material that will be on the void-space side of the exchange at the moment of return.

The practical consequence: mountains, geological formations, and occasionally entire landscape features in the Tear's zone vanish and reappear regularly. What reappears is not necessarily what vanished the void-space channel is not a specific holding location but an undefined space. Material that enters the Tear can return immediately or centuries later, from any point in the Tear's history of operation.

Archaeological teams operating under Oracle supervision in the regions surrounding the Tear have recovered geological material that analysis places as originating from the pre-Splintering era matter that entered the Tear centuries ago and returned recently. They have also theorized, based on mass conservation calculations, that material currently in the void-space from the Tear's early operation will continue returning over the next estimated 400 years.

---

 III. The No-Fly Zone

The 8th Dimension's Ancient Oracle network established a mandatory monitoring and exclusion perimeter around the Void Tear in the early Dark Age, which has been maintained continuously since. Gabriel's Panopticon flagged the Tear's location in years 2-4 of the Dark Age and subsequently designated it a No-Data Zone due to its complete Aetheric sensing unreliability.

The Seraphim do not patrol the exclusion zone. They maintain the designation and leave the Oracle monitoring operations in place because the alternative is having Seraphic assets in a zone where the fundamental physics of dimensional spatial integrity are actively failing.

The exclusion zone's Seraphic gap has been used by the 8th Dimension's resistance network as a supply drop relay materials placed in the zone's stable outer buffer transfer to Oracles who maintain positioning within the buffer and pass them onward. It is the slowest and most risk-laden supply method in the Railroad's toolkit. It is also entirely invisible to the Panopticon.

 Archivist Note: The Returned
The Oracle monitoring teams maintain a registry of every piece of geological material that has returned from the Tear along with any archaeological content it contains. The registry includes, in Year 2,847, a fragment of stone wall that returned bearing carved text in pre-Dark Age Elven notation. The text was analyzed by the Vocation's linguistic division. It reads: 'The pass will hold until morning. Tell the children we are coming.' The wall fragment's geological composition places its origin in the 7th Dimension. No 7th Dimension resistance operation from any era matching the description has been located in the archive. The fragment is dated by composition to approximately Year 400 of the Dark Age. The archive records the text. The archive cannot tell you whose wall it was or whether the children were told.
