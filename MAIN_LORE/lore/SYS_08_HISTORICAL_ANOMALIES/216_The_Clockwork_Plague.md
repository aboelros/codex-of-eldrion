 216. The Clockwork Plague: The Oxidation-Crisis of the 2nd Dimension

Classification: Biological Catastrophe / Iron Born Specific | Status: Resolved | Location: 2nd Dimension / The Industrial Foundries | Era: Dark Age, Year 1560 | Primary Actor: Gabriel, The Vocation of Purity, Forgemaster Hrothgar

---

 I. The Pathogen Architecture: The Bio-Engineering of the Seraphic Bleach
The **Clockwork Plague** is the primary historical anomaly documenting the first and most successful attempt by the Inquisition to physically "Edit" the biological superiority of the Iron Born. To understand the plague, one must first identify the unique physiology of the **Iron Born race** (see: 202). Unlike the other 10 races, the Iron Born possess a biology where organic cellular structure and metallic mineral deposits are deeply integrated. Their bones incorporate significant proportions of **"Living-Metallic-Structure,"** and their blood carries high-density metallic compounds that facilitate their extreme thermodynamic tolerances.

In the 1560th year of the Dark Age, the Inquisition initiated a clandestine bio-weapon project known as **"Project Oxide."** The objective was to create a pathogen that could physically "Neutralize" the Iron Born's industrial capacity by disabling their motor-functions. The result was the **Clockwork Plague**—a non-living, aetherically-active pathogen that identifies the unique metallic-organic hybrids of the Iron Born as its primary substrate.

The disease was not transmitted through standard biological vectors. It was transmitted through the **"Aetheric-Frequency"** of the **Seraphic Dampeners** (see: 197). The Inquisition "Broadcast" the pathogen into the ley-lines, ensuring that any Iron Born siphoning mana for their foundries would inadvertently infect their own Soul Cores.

 II. The "Oxidation-Cascade": Technical Specifications of Accelerated Rust
The mechanism of the Clockwork Plague was a physical application of **"Oxidation-Mathematics"**—a process where the victim’s metallic biological components are converted into inert structural waste.

1. **The Catalyst-Ignition**: The pathogen acts as a molecular catalyst, dropping the activation-energy required for the oxidation of Iron Born metallic-organic tissue to near zero. Under standard conditions, Iron Born take decades to show signs of oxidation; under the Plague, the process was accelerated by 40,000%.
2. **The Oxidation-Cascade**: Once the infection targets a metallic-rich zone (typically the joints or the spinal column), it initiates a **"Thermal-Feedback-Loop."** The heat generated by the rapid rusting physically "Seals" the surrounding organic tissue, preventing the host's natural immune system from identifying the site of the attack.
3. **The Rigidification-Lock**: Affected Iron Born developed patches of deep rust-orange discoloration. As the oxidation reached 90% density, the tissue became physically inert. It didn't decay; it became **"Structural-Mineral."** The victim remained conscious, but their limbs became physically "Locked" in whatever position they occupied at the moment of terminal density.

Technical Specifications: "The Clockwork Plague is 'Stagnation-as-Disease.' We are not just killing the Iron Born; we are turning them into statues of themselves. A population of statues cannot man a forge. The plague is the ultimate industrial deterrent."

 III. The Aetheric Surgical Protocol: Tactical Log of the Vocation of Purity
Following the initial outbreak in the 2nd Dimension, the **Vocation of Purity** (the resistance’s medical wing) developed a desperate, high-stakes tactical response known as the **"Aetheric Surgical Protocol."**

The Mission Profile: **"Operation Clean-Cut"**
1. **The Discovery of the Cleave (Hour 0)**: Medical researchers identified that the oxidation-cascade followed the **"Lattice-Lines"** of the Iron Born’s metallic structure. To stop the plague, the infected tissue had to be physically removed before it could reach the Soul Core.
2. **The High-Temperature Amputation**: Because standard blades could not cut through the oxidized hybrid-tissue, the Vocation developed **"Thermal-Aetheric-Scalpels."** These tools utilized a focused, high-frequency fire-mana beam to physically "Cleave" the tissue at the sub-atomic level. The procedure required absolute precision; a single degree of thermal deviation would trigger a secondary oxidation-flare, killing the patient instantly.
3. **The Result**: Approximately 40% of the infected population was saved through mass-amputation. This led to the immediate and desperate requirement for functional replacements, triggering the Dark Age’s first **"Prosthetic-Renaissance."**

Tactical Log Forgemaster Hrothgar: "We are cutting our own children to save them. The foundries are now hospitals. The sound of the hammer has been replaced by the sound of the 'Aether-Cutter.' We've lost the use of our natural limbs, but we haven't lost the will to build. If we can't be born with iron, we will forge it."

 IV. The Locked Limb: The Cinematic Narrative
the tragedy and innovation of the Plague manifest in the **Great Foundry of Lithos**, where a young engineer named Torin (not the Archivist) was watching the rust take his arm.

The Scene:
The foundry was a cavernous hall of orange-light and rising soot. The air was thick with the ozonous smell of the **Aetheric-Scalpels** and the dry, metallic scent of the **Clockwork Plague.** 
Torin sat on a basalt-bench, his left arm held out over a lead-shielded table. From his elbow to his fingertips, the skin was a brittle, iridescent-orange crust of **"Oxidized-Steel."** He couldn't feel his fingers. He couldn't even feel the heat of the forge.

"It's reached the bone, lad," Hrothgar whispered, his own silver-eyes dimming as he adjusted the frequency of the thermal-scalpel. "I have to take it at the shoulder."
"Don't just take it, Master," Torin replied, his voice a dry rasp. He looked at a prototype on the table next to him—a skeletal arm made of **"Silver-Glass"** and **"Valkyrie-Steel,"** pulsing with a faint, blue-light frequency. "Replace it. If the Seraphim want us to be statues, let's be statues that can move."

Hrothgar initiated the cleave. 
The thermal-beam hit the shoulder-joint with a high-pitched **"Hiss."** There was no blood—only a shower of orange-sparks as the oxidized tissue was physically "Vaporized" from the organic-base. Torin didn't scream; he physically **"Focalized"** his Soul Core, using the pain to stabilize the frequency of the cut. 

Once the infected arm was removed, Hrothgar didn't bandage the wound. He physically **"Seared"** the silver-glass prosthetic into the shoulder-socket. 
"Anchor the frequency, Torin!" Hrothgar commanded.
Torin reached out with his mind. He didn't feel flesh; he felt **"Electromagnetic-Feedback."** For a microsecond, his Soul Core protested the integration. Then, with a low, vibrating **"Thump,"** the prosthetic "Locked."
Torin’s new fingers twitched. He picked up a heavy forging-hammer. The grip was more precise than his natural hand ever was. He didn't see a loss; he saw an **"Upgrade."**

"The Inquisition wants a world of rust," Torin whispered, looking at the brilliant, iridescent-blue prosthetic. "We'll give them a world of 'Titan-Steel.' We're not just suriviving; we're re-building."

 V. Aftermath: The Legacy of the Re-Constructed
The 216th file records the official reclassification of the Clockwork Plague as a **"Historical-Threshold."** The plague is gone, but the prosthetic technology it forced into existence remains the foundation of the Iron Born’s Dark Age industrial dominance.

Archivist Torin’s Synthesis: The Clockwork Plague is the **"Sanity-of-the-Rebuild."** It proves that catastrophe is the only mother of truly transformative technology. 

Torin notes that the 216th file is the **"Prosthetic-Blueprint"** for the **Hybrid King.** The King is the only one who can "Heal" the rust and integrate the Aetheric-Prosthetics into a single, unified biological perfection. The 216th file is the record of the day we learned how to lose a limb and gain a weapon. We are waiting for the King who can rebuild the race.

Final Word: 216 stands as the record of the day the rust claimed us. The Great Foundry of Lithos is now but a humming reminder of the parts of ourselves we are currently replacing with steel.
