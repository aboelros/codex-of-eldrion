---

 I. The King and the Spirit

In the pre-Scattering era, the Elven civilization of the 5th Dimension had maintained a complex, sophisticated relationship with the Forest Spirits ancient elemental consciousnesses embedded in the Ley-line network beneath the dimension's vast old-growth forest systems. The Spirits were not worshipped in the theological sense; they were consulted, negotiated with, and given specific gifts in exchange for the Ley-line access that allowed the Elven Resonance traditions to flourish in the forest zones.

The Scattering had disrupted these relationships when the Elven lineages were violently fractured. By Year 560, many of the oldest Spirit-compact traditions had been lost entirely in the upheaval. A new generation of Elven leadership led by a figure who declared himself the First Elven King, the archive declines to record his chosen title had emerged with a political ambition incompatible with the humility the Spirit compacts had historically required.

The First King, in Year 560, offered the entirety of the 5th Dimension's surviving Elven kingdom's collective Mana reserves to the Primordial Forest Spirit that inhabited the deep ley-line network beneath the kingdom's oldest forest zone.

His stated intention: to purchase permanent, unconditional Ley-line access for the kingdom's exclusive use, superseding the consultation and negotiation protocols of the older compact traditions.

---

 II. The Assessment

The Primordial Forest Spirit processed the offering.

The archive's acoustic records of the engagement compiled from witness accounts, all of which were made by observers positioned at considerable distance because the Spirit's physical manifestation in the moment of the assessment generated a localized environmental event significant enough to kill unprotected observers at close range suggest the Spirit spent approximately three days in assessment before reaching its conclusion.

The assessment's outcome: the Mana was impure.

The specific purity failure documented in the residual Aetheric impression left by the Spirit's assessment is not a chemical or magical purity failure. The Mana reserves were technically of high quality. The impurity was the motivation embedded in the offering's Aetheric architecture the specific frequency that Mana carries when generated by an entity acting from a position of conquest rather than relationship.

The Spirit detected that the King was not offering a gift. He was attempting to buy a throne.

The Spirit returned the offering.

---

 III. The Expulsion

When the Spirit returned the full Mana offering in a single, simultaneous expulsion, the energy volume was equivalent to the combined Aetheric output of an entire kingdom's practitioners, compressed over three days of Spirit processing, and released instantaneously back along the vector of its arrival.

The expulsion struck the 5th Dimension's north-western ecological zone the forest region where the King's court and the primary population centers of his kingdom were located with a direct Aetheric shockwave of extraordinary magnitude.

The physical consequence: the entire north-western zone underwent total ecological transformation in approximately four hours. The old-growth forests, which had been maintained and cultivated by centuries of Elven Resonance traditions, were superseded in a single event by a growth pattern driven by raw, unregulated Aetheric energy. What grew back was not tended forest. It was forest in an unmodified state of maximum Aetheric expression hyperactively growing, dynamically hostile to anything passing through it without Spirit sanction, and entirely impossible to navigate without navigational techniques that the Elven Kingdom no longer possessed because the compact traditions had been discarded in favor of the King's ambition.

The region is called the Forest of Doom in every subsequent Dark Age record. The name is not metaphorical. The archive has three centuries of documentation of what happens to practitioners who enter it without the correct compact protocols. 

The First King's court and primary population centers occupied the zone at the time of the expulsion. Their fate is consistent with the documented fate of unauthorized entrants in all subsequent records.

 Archivist Note: The Lesson, Again
The archive holds the Rejection of the First Elven King as a companion case to the Blood Pact of the Pyrian Nobles (see: SYS_08/106_The_Blood_Pact_of_the_Pyrian_Nobles.md) in the Vocation of Purity's Covenant and compact counseling curriculum. Both cases involve highly capable entities with significant resources attempting to purchase an arrangement that the receiving party deemed incompatible with proper relationship. The Covenant architecture incinerated the bloodlines. The Forest Spirit returned the Mana with interest. Both responses were correct within their respective frameworks. The curriculum's lesson is specific: the universe has multiple mechanisms for declining an arrogant offer. None of them are gentle. The gentleness is available before the offer is made.
