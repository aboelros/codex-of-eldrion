---

 I. The Arrogance

The Pyrian noble lineages of the 4th Dimension occupied an unusual social position in the Dark Age's resistance ecology. They were wealthy by Dark Age standards their bloodlines controlled significant thermal Relic stockpiles and operated several of the surviving large-scale underground forges. They funded resistance activities generously but participated in them minimally. They viewed themselves as the patrons of the cause rather than its practitioners.

In Year 1,810, five Lords of the Pyrian noble caste Calder, Venn, Orathis, Sorel, and Braun entered a private negotiation that they did not disclose to the Vocation of Purity's Covenant counseling service, the resistance network's academic council, or any external party. They designed and executed it themselves in a disused alchemy chamber in Calder's estate complex.

Their stated objective, reconstructed from the surviving estate records: the unification of five high-tier Pyrian Soul Cores into a single, sustained combined output sufficient to generate a thermal field equivalent in intensity to a minor stellar body. Their practical goal: to become, collectively, a false god. Not to serve the resistance, but to use the force of a combined stellar-scale output to place themselves above both the Seraphic Order and the mortal resistance, establishing a third, sovereign power in the 13 Dimensions with themselves at its apex.

They were not students of the Vocation. They were not trained in advanced Covenant architecture. They were wealthy men with very powerful cores and a very specific idea of what they deserved.

---

 II. The Covenant Architecture's Response

Standard Covenant physics (see: SYS_02/11_The_Mechanics_of_Covenants.md) require an exchange that is proportional to what is requested. The Covenant architecture is not sentient, but it processes the specific request against the universal physical Ledger to identify the appropriate exchange cost.

The five Lords requested the effective power of a god. The Covenant architecture identified the exchange cost precisely.

To become a god requires an existence justification equivalent to a godlike entity. A godlike entity that exists solely to serve its own sovereignty provides no functional value to the universal physics framework. An entity that provides no functional value to the universal physics framework has no legitimate basis for the resource expenditure required to create it.

The Covenant architecture processed the exchange and returned the only response that was thermodynamically coherent: the request was denied at the cost of the request itself.

The five Lords' Soul Cores were incinerated from the inside out in the moment of the Covenant's execution. They were not killed by the Covenant failing; they were killed by the Covenant acknowledging their request, determining it was founded on arrogance incompatible with the universal Ledger's requirements, and charging them for having asked.

The incineration was total. Their physical forms were consumed. The estate's alchemy chamber was sealed by the resulting thermal event.

---

 III. The Bloodline Consequence

The specific horror of the Blood Pact outcome was not the deaths of the five Lords five arrogant wealthy practitioners making an extraordinarily poor decision was not, in itself, an unusual Dark Age event.

The horror was the Covenant architecture's collateral extension of the charge.

Standard Covenant physics focus the exchange on the individuals who enter the Covenant. The Blood Pact's architecture was designed by its executors to bind across their collective bloodlines they wanted their descendant heirs to inherit the combined power they were attempting to create. The Covenant architecture, processing a request that included explicit bloodline extension terms, extended the denial accordingly.

Every living member of all five Pyrian noble bloodlines extending across all branches and degrees of relation was consumed by the same incineration event simultaneously.

The archive's calculation of the total affected individuals, across the five bloodlines' full extended family structures, places the figure at approximately 2,400 people. They all died on the same night, in every location any member of those bloodlines occupied across the 4th Dimension, simultaneously.

The Pyrian noble caste ceased to exist as a social category. The Covenant denied five men the right to claim sovereignty over the universe and charged the entire family tree for the attempt.

 Archivist Note: The Night of the Burning Houses
The 4th Dimension calls Year 1,810 the Night of the Burning Houses 2,400 buildings across the dimension lit from the inside simultaneously as the bloodline members were consumed. The Vocation of Purity uses the Blood Pact as the primary case study in the introductory Covenant counseling module. Not because the physics is complex it is extremely simple but because the five Lords were not idiots. They were highly intelligent, well-resourced practitioners who understood Covenant basics and executed this anyway because they believed their particular desire was exceptional enough to warrant an exceptional response from universal physics. The Covenant architecture disagreed. The universe is the final word on what is exceptional.
