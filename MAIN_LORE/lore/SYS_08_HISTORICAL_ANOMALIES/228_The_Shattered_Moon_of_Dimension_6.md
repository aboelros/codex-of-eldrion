 103_The_Shattered_Moon_of_Dimension_6

 Classification: Astronomical Catastrophe / Sustained Environmental Hazard | Status: Permanent   Ongoing | Location: 6th Dimension | Era: Dark Age, Year 1,670

---

 I. The Battle

In Year 1,670, two practitioners who had developed Shikigami binding techniques of extraordinary power engaged in a combat event above the 6th Dimension's largest inhabited continent. Both were operating at the upper ceiling of the Tier 3 binding framework, each commanding a Shikigami of sufficient individual Aetheric mass that their simultaneous full-output engagement produced a sustained energy exchange comparable to the localized outputs from the 4th Holy War's senior commanders.

The cause of the combat is documented in the archive's records as a factional dispute between competing underground academic traditions whose philosophical disagreement had escalated over approximately fifteen years of increasingly hostile academic competition until it culminated in direct kinetic engagement. The archive considers the specific philosophical disagreement that initiated the combat both the most petty inciting event in its collection and, given the consequences, among the most consequential.

The 6th Dimension's primary moon was, at the time of the engagement, in its closest orbital approach to the planetary surface a standard astronomical event that occurs on an approximately eighteen-month cycle.

The Shikigami engagement produced a gravity-shear event as a byproduct of two separately-generated intense localized gravity fields interacting at proximity without equilibrium resolution. The shear vector, pointing upward into the low orbital zone rather than laterally across the terrain, struck the moon directly.

The moon fractured along its primary internal stress axes. It did not break into two pieces; it broke into hundreds of thousands of fragments of varying size, which redistributed into a stable ring orbit around the 6th Dimension's planet.

---

 II. The Three Generations

The orbital redistribution of the moon fragments was, from a physics perspective, clean. The fragments found stable orbital positions within approximately forty years of the fracture event. The ring is stable and will remain stable indefinitely.

The transition period between fracture and orbital stability lasted three generations of continuous meteorite fall.

The fragments in unstable transitional orbits impacted the planet's surface over the forty-year stabilization period. Larger fragments produced impacts equivalent to geological events; smaller fragments produced sustained, high-intensity meteorite rain across the equatorial and sub-equatorial zones of the northern continent the most densely populated region of the 6th Dimension.

The archive's casualty estimate for the three generations of meteorite fall is 22 million deaths from direct impacts, secondary geological events, and the sustained agricultural disruption of a continent experiencing continuous high-altitude debris precipitation.

The two practitioners who initiated the combat were killed by one of the combat's own rebounding Shikigami strikes within the first twenty minutes of the engagement. They did not survive to witness what they had started.

---

 III. The Ring

The ring of former moon fragments is now a permanent feature of the 6th Dimension's night sky. It is visible as a wide, luminous band across the equatorial sky zone during peak visibility hours. 

Local artistic and cultural traditions in the 6th Dimension that developed after the stabilization period uniformly incorporate the ring as a central visual motif. The resistance cells in the 6th Dimension use it in their authentication signaling a specific angular reference to a particular segment of the ring serves as a directional coordinate system for Walker transit that does not require Aetheric transmission.

The archive notes that an astronomical catastrophe produced by the specific vanity of two practitioners is now the primary navigational landmark of the 6th Dimension's resistance network.

---

 Archivist Note: The Philosophical Dispute
The archive has made a deliberate decision not to document the specific philosophical positions held by the two practitioners whose combat caused the Shattered Moon event. The positions are in the archive's records. They have been reviewed. They are, to the academic community's consensus assessment, neither interesting enough nor important enough to have been worth twenty-two million lives and three generations of meteorite fall. The archive does not record them here because doing so would imply a proportionality between the philosophical stakes the practitioners assigned to their disagreement and the consequences that disagreement produced. There is no proportionality. The positions are retained internally in case anyone ever needs to understand exactly what not to consider worth fighting about.
