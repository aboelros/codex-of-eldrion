 105_The_Iron_Born_Civil_War

 Classification: Factional Conflict / Resource War | Status: Concluded (Permanently Divided) | Location: 2nd Dimension Primarily | Era: Dark Age, Years 1,200–1,400

---

 I. The Object

Titan Lithos, Sculptor of Mass, is in stasis in the foundational bedrock beneath the 13 Dimensions. In the millennia since his enforced silence, his biology which is composed of material that has no equivalent in standard geological chemistry has been undergoing extremely slow, passive biological processes even at zero Aetheric output.

One of these processes is localized cellular crystallization. Lithos's biological substrate, at the microscopic boundary between his stasis field and the surrounding bedrock, periodically produces small fragments of a material that the archive's chemical analysis framework classifies as "undefined: composite of Aetheric-saturated non-standard mineral and biological byproduct." Colloquially: Lithos's blood, which has slowly petrified over millennia.

In Year 1,198, tunneling operations for a resistance supply convoy route in the 2nd Dimension's deep geological zone broke through into a small cavern containing a single fist-sized nodule of this material. The nodule's properties were immediately identified as extraordinary by the Vanguard engineering team: it was simultaneously the densest and lightest material any of them had encountered, it conducted Aetheric frequencies without any measurable loss, and it appeared to self-repair microscopic damage to its structure over a period of days.

The team's commanding engineer made an immediate decision to transport it to the Iron Born foundry councils for study.

---

 II. The Fracture

The Iron Born foundry councils' analysis of the nodule took approximately four months. Their conclusions, presented at the Year 1,200 General Council, were unanimous on the scientific properties and completely, irreconcilably divided on the question of application.

Three distinct positions emerged and hardened into factional identity within weeks of the presentation.

The Preservation faction held that the nodule was a sacred biological remnant of one of the universe's architects and should be secured intact, studied only by methods that preserved it in its original state, and treated as irreplaceable because it was.

The Weaponization faction held that the material's Aetheric conductivity and structural properties, if the nodule could be broken down and incorporated into a Relic manufacturing process, would produce offensive capabilities that could change the military balance of the Dark Age. Their argument: a sacred object is only sacred if the people who recognize its sanctity survive to keep recognizing it.

The Trade faction held that the nodule's properties were sufficiently unique that other resistance networks would offer extraordinary resources for it, and that the 2nd Dimension's networks would benefit more from the trade value than from any direct application.

The factions did not resolve through debate. They resolved through violence.

---

 III. The Two Hundred Years

The Iron Born Civil War lasted two hundred years and split the Iron Born lineage into three factions that remain formally separate to the present day. The Preservation, Weaponization, and Trade factions established distinct territorial zones in the 2nd Dimension and prosecuted a sustained, intermittent, low-intensity conflict for control of the nodule while simultaneously being required to operate resistance activities against the Seraphic Order.

The nodule itself was secured in a contested neutral vault for most of the conflict's duration, guarded by a rotating joint custody team from all three factions who collectively agreed on exactly one thing: no single faction should hold it until the war ended.

The war ended, technically, in Year 1,400 with a truce document that resolved the custody question by not resolving it. The nodule remains in the joint custody vault. The three factions remain separate. They cooperate on operations against the Seraphic Order with a level of professional functional competence that does not require them to like each other, which is fortunate because they do not.

---

 Archivist Note: The Vault
The joint custody vault's location is one of the most closely guarded secrets in the 2nd Dimension's resistance networks. All three factions know where it is. None of them have breached it since the truce. The archive has been told the location by all three factions independently, under the condition that it is not shared. The archive holds the location under that condition. What the archive can document is the following general observation about the Iron Born Civil War: the three factions spent two hundred years fighting each other over a piece of a sleeping Titan's blood. Two hundred years later, all three factions are still operating, still guarding the nodule jointly, and still conducting resistance operations against the Seraphim. The war was about what the object was worth. The truce proved that the question was less important than the question of what the factions were worth to each other.
