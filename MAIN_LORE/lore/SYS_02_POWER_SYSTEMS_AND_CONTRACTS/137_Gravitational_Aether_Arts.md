 I. Gravitational Mechanics and the Aetheric Field

Gravity in the 13 Dimensions is not solely a product of mass bending space-time. It is a dual-function system dictated originally by Lithos (Sculptor of Mass). In this universe, gravity is the physical symptom of the ambient Mana field interacting with biological and mineral mass. 

Because gravity is intimately tied to the Mana field, it can be manipulated by a practitioner willing to expend the appropriate amount of internal Aether to temporarily overwrite the localized physical laws.

Gravitational Aether Arts do not involve "lifting" things with invisible magical hands. The discipline is strictly mathematical. A practitioner manipulates the specific gravitational constant acting upon their own body, or on an object directly within their Aetheric aura. They alter the value of g. 

If a practitioner reduces the gravitational constant acting on their own mass to near zero, they do not fly in the aerodynamic sense (like Elaraâ€™s cavalry); they simply fall upward, or drift, repelled by the planet's mass. If they invert the constant, they can fall horizontally. 

At the foundational level, this provides extreme mobility. Highland scouts use low-level gravitational suppression to leap across vast chasms or arrest fatal falls. However, as the application of the art shifts from mobility to offensive output, the physics become dramatically more lethal.

They become a human railgun projectile.

Gravitational Aether Arts reached their zenith when paired with the engineering prowess of the Iron Born (specifically Ornn The Smith and Thorne The Breaker). 

Because the human body is an inherently squishy and inefficient projectile, Ornn forged specialized Relic armors designed specifically for Gravitational practitioners.  Archivist Note: The Mathematics of the Fall
There is no magic in the Gravitational Arts that pardons you from math. This is what the Highland instructors tried to beat into their students. The Soul Core can rewrite the rules of gravity, but it cannot rewrite the consequence of speed. The tragedy of the Vanguard practitioners is that they were mathematicians who chose to express their proofs by throwing themselves at walls. They were the most physically destructive forces in the mortal army, but their art required an absolute, cold-blooded acceptance of physics. You do not survive a gravitational drop by hoping you are strong enough. You survive it because your calculations are flawless. When Garrick displaced Abdiel four hundred meters, he wasn't just hitting him; he was solving a physics equation where the remainder was a four-minute gap in the Seraphic line.
