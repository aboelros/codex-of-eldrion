 I. The Misconception of Familiar Spirits

In the early folklore of the 10 Races, specifically prior to the codification of Aetheric science by the academies, Shikigami were widely misunderstood as external entities nature spirits, dimensional ghosts, or lesser beasts that a practitioner had somehow tamed and bound to their will. 

Modern archival science, drawing from the detailed biological dissections and Soul Core analyses preserved in the 10th Dimension's underground networks, categorically refutes this. 

A Shikigami is not a separate biological entity. It is a severed fragment of the practitioner's own Soul Core, pushed outside the physical boundary of the body, and given semi-permanent mass by utilizing a localized vortex of the surrounding atmospheric Mana field. 

To summon a Shikigami is not to call upon an ally. It is to externalize a piece of one's own consciousness and weaponize one's own internal architecture. The structural framework (the "skeleton") is pure internal Aether; the physical form (the "flesh") is condensed external Mana. 

When a Shikigami is destroyed in combat, the practitioner does not lose a pet. They suffer direct, catastrophic trauma to their own Soul Core.

---

 II. The Psychological Catalyst: The 1st Holy War

The origin of Shikigami manifestations is not rooted in martial innovation. It is rooted in mass psychological trauma. 

The archive traces the first documented occurrences of Shikigami manifestations to the immediate aftermath of the 1st Holy War. This conflict, triggered by the Monarchs' shift from overseers to tyrants, was the first time the mortal races experienced cosmic-scale violence. The psychological devastation was unprecedented. Entire populations were subjected to casualty scales and environmental destruction that the mortal mind was simply not evolved to process.

In practitioners with high-tier Aetheric sensitivity, this immense, unprocessed trauma caused the Soul Core to fracture. The human (and Elven, and Giant) mind's natural defense mechanism against overwhelming psychological pain is dissociation the partitioning of the traumatized consciousness. 

In non-practitioners, this results in severe psychological disorder. In high-tier Aetheric practitioners, this dissociative partition became physically manifest. The fractured piece of the Soul Core, overloaded with grief, terror, or survival instinct, pushed itself out of the practitioner's body. Drawing wildly on the ambient Mana of the chaotic post-war environment, it built itself a body.

The first Shikigami were not combat tools. They were physical manifestations of PTSD. They took forms dictated entirely by the practitioner's subconscious: protective beasts, imposing walls, or grotesque nightmare-shapes reflecting the horrors the practitioner had witnessed.

 III. Autonomous vs. Puppet Manifestations

As the centuries progressed through the 2nd and 3rd Holy Wars, the phenomenon of Shikigami manifestation was slowly understood, categorized, and eventually weaponized. The academies developed methodologies to induce the Soul Core fracture in a controlled manner, turning a trauma-response into a tactical capability. 

However, all Shikigami fall into one of two distinct categories, dictated by the nature of the fracture that created them:

1. Puppet Manifestations (The Controlled Fracture):
This is the standard taught in pre-Dark Age academies. The practitioner deliberately excises a small, emotionally neutral piece of their Soul Core. This piece has no independent consciousness. It is a drone, controlled directly by the practitioner's active will. 
Advantages: Perfectly obedient, requires less concentrated Aether to sustain, and the psychological backlash of its destruction is minimal, reading more like a severe concussion than a loss of self.
Limitations: It can only act when the practitioner is actively directing it, dividing the practitioner's attention in combat.

2. Autonomous Manifestations (The Traumatic Fracture):
These are the original expressions of the art, resulting from genuine emotional or psychological trauma tearing the Soul Core. The excised piece is massive and carries a distinct segment of the practitioner's subconscious mind. 
Advantages: The Shikigami has its own operational will. It can defend the practitioner without being directed, react faster than conscious thought, and possesses an Aetheric output that frequently exceeds the practitioner's conscious baseline, because it accesses the uninhibited survival instincts of the subconscious.
Limitations: They are fiercely difficult to control. An Autonomous Shikigami acts on the emotion that birthed it. A Shikigami born of rage will not easily execute a tactical retreat. Furthermore, the destruction of an Autonomous Shikigami results in the violent reintegration of the trauma into the practitioner's mind, often resulting in permanent catatonia or death.

 IV. The Seraphic Governance Perspective

Following the establishment of the Dark Age, the Seraphic Order placed an immediate, universal ban on Shikigami manifestation, classifying it as a Class-1 Heresy akin to Tier-3 Covenant binding. 

The reasoning in the Seraphic administrative doctrine revolves around the concept of "Mana Hoarding." Because a Shikigami’s physical form is constructed from highly condensed external Mana, an army utilizing Shikigami effectively drains the local atmosphere of the energy the Seraphim require for the Mana Harvests. 

However, the archive's psychological research teams propose a secondary, more philosophical reason for the ban. The Seraphim, through the Will Severance procedure, are beings from whom all intense emotion, trauma, and subconscious conflict have been surgically removed. A Shikigami is the literal manifestation of everything a Seraph has been designed to lack. 

An Autonomous Shikigami is a physical monument to mortal grief, rage, and messy, uncontrollable psychological survival. To a Seraph, fighting a Shikigami is not just a tactical engagement; it is confronting the physical manifestation of the empathic architecture they were forced to abandon. 

In the current Dark Age, Shikigami manifestations are exceptionally rare. The Seraphic Dampeners make the massive Mana condensation required for "fleshing" the Aetheric skeleton almost impossible to achieve. Those who still practice the art in the underground resistance networks do so only with Puppet Manifestations, and only in subterranean or highly shielded environments. 

The Autonomous Shikigami the grand, terrifying monsters built from a practitioner's broken heart have not been seen in mass since the catastrophic battles of the 4th Holy War.

 Archivist Note: The Reflection of the Host
A Shikigami cannot lie. Words can deceive, and actions can be faked, but the physical form of a Shikigami is drawn directly from the architecture of the practitioner's subconscious. If a community leader projects a Shikigami that resembles a starving, preying predator, the community has learned to question the leader's actual motives. We are a people forced into hiding, living in the Dark Age with our identities stripped from us. The Shikigami remains the one aspect of a practitioner that cannot hide what it truly is. To look at a practitioner's Autonomous Shikigami is to look directly at the shape of their deepest wound.
