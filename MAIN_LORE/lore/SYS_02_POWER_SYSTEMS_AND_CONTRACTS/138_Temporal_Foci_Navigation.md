Time, as it flows through the 13 Dimensions, is not a frictionless process. Much like an object moving through water, the present moment leaves a turbulent wake behind it (the past) and pushes a pressure ripple out ahead of it (the future).

A practitioner of Temporal Foci Navigation attunes their Soul Core to vibrate at a frequency slightly out of phase with the localized flow of time. Rather than generating a destructive shockwave, this attunement allows the practitioner's sensory input to read the pressure variations in the probability ripple.

In a combat scenario, a Temporal practitioner does not "see" a sword swinging five seconds before it happens. They perceive the overwhelming Aetheric probability that a sword is about to be swung along a specific vector.

 IV. The Hazard of Chronological Drift

The reason Temporal Foci Navigation is classified as highly lethal and essentially extinct in the Dark Age is due to the inherent metaphysical hazard known as Chronological Drift.

To read the probability ripple, the Soul Core must constantly exist slightly out of phase with the present moment. The deeper the practitioner looks into the future (trying to read ripples twelve seconds, or two minutes ahead, rather than two seconds), the further out of phase the Soul Core must vibrate.

The human mind was not biologically designed to process sensory data from the present and from multiple probability futures simultaneously. The cognitive load is staggering.

If a practitioner loses focus, or if they attempt to read too far ahead during a chaotic Aetheric engagement, the Soul Core can "slip" off the exact frequency of the present. The psychological term for this is Chronological Drift. 

The practitioner physically remains in the present, but their consciousness is dragged permanently into a branching probability future. 

The archive's medical texts document devastating accounts of Temporal practitioners suffering Drift during the 2nd and 3rd Holy Wars. To an outside observer, the practitioner suddenly stops moving, staring blankly ahead. Inside the practitioner's mind, they are living out a hyper-realistic, continuous timeline of a future that never actually occurred in reality. 

They become trapped in the ripple. Because they cannot process the sensory data of the actual present, they cannot defend themselves, feed themselves, or participate in reality. They are functionally catatonic corpses lost in futures that do not exist.

Sylvia The Seer was the last known Grandmaster capable of maintaining constant Temporal sight without suffering Drift, a feat she managed through sheer, unbroken psychological willpower and a highly developed Empathic Regulatory valve that perfectly insulated her present-consciousness from the noise of the ripples. When the Seraphim killed her during the Purge, the discipline died with her.
