 I. The Mathematical Limit of the Seraphim

Following the catastrophic Splintering of Dimensions (see: SYS_05/58_The_Splintering_of_Dimensions.md), Gabriel faced a severe logistical crisis regarding the occupation of the 13 Dimensions. 

Gabriel had fundamentally solved the problem of empathy by creating the Seraphim, but he had failed to solve the problem of mass. The mass Genesis event had created approximately three thousand Seraphim. The 4th Holy War, particularly the Siege of the Divine Apex and the Fall of Abdiel, had severely depleted those numbers. 

Even with the dimensions splintered and isolated, attempting to actively police the entirety of the 11 lower dimensions simultaneously with an occupying force of less than two thousand emotionless troops was mathematically impossible. If Gabriel spread his forces too thin, the surviving localized resistance cells could effectively ambush and whittle the Seraphim down via attrition. 

Gabriel required a vast, self-sustaining bureaucratic and military apparatus in the lower dimensions to enforce the Mana Harvests, track Aetheric practitioners, and maintain the Dampener engines. Because he did not have enough Seraphim to perform these tasks, he proposed a horrifyingly pragmatic solution to Zenithos: outsource the oppression.

---

 II. The Architecture of the Proxy

To fully subjugate the mortal races, Gabriel and Zenithos decided to utilize the mortals' own inherent capacity for ambition, greed, and terror against them. 

They initiated a highly selective recruitment process, pulling candidates entirely from the upper echelons of the shattered mortal coalition. Specifically, Gabriel targeted the lieutenant commanders of the various lineages who had survived the siege but had witnessed the horrific executions of Theron, Garrick, and Silas. 

Gabriel offered these individuals a terrifying binary choice: burn in the agonizing torture chambers of Tartarus, or accept the position of a localized, absolute sovereign proxy.

The 11 mortals who ultimately accepted the offer were designated as The Wardens (see: DB_PRE_KEVIN_07). 

Zenithos did not simply hand them a badge. Each Warden was subjected to a highly restrictive Tier 1 Covenant. They were granted near-absolute authority over their designated localized dimension, massive influxes of refined Aether to massively boost their standard biology into near-immortality, and command over localized squadrons of minor Shikigami. In exchange, they were biologically bound to uphold the Seraphic Orthodox laws, prevent unauthorized Aetheric practice, and most importantly, deliver the required quotas for the Mana Harvests directly to the Divine Apex.

---

 III. The Psychology of the Appointees

The appointment of the Wardens was the cruelest psychological tactic executed by the Divine Apex.

Zenithos knew that a resistance movement thrives when it has a clear, unassailable, alien evil to fight against like an emotionless Seraph. By installing mortals as the primary enforcers of the tyranny, Zenithos intentionally muddied the ideological waters. 

When a Highland mining village is violently purged for hiding a Pyrian child, it is not Gabriel descending from the sky to execute them. It is Warden Aethelgard a man who originally fought alongside the Highland Giants during the Abyssal Incursion who gives the order. 

The Wardens enforce the laws significantly more creatively than the Seraphim. Because the Wardens still possess their Empathic Regulators, they understand mortal fear, sociology, and pain. They use this understanding to dismantle resistance cells from the inside out, utilizing blackmail, hostage tactics, and psychological warfare that a Seraph simply lacks the programming to conceptualize. 

The Wardens inherently despise the Seraphim they serve, but they are terrified of Gabriel and biologically bound by their Covenants to obey. They take this immense, trapped frustration and violently redirect it downward upon the populations they govern.

---

 IV. The Division of the Realm

The 11 Wardens were assigned specifically to Dimensions 1 through 11. (The 12th Dimension is the Divine Apex; the 13th is the outer periphery bordering the Abyss).

Each Warden was tasked with maintaining the "Peace" a euphemism for ensuring that the generalized population remained weak, isolated, and sufficiently drained of Aether to prevent any organized uprising. 

The establishment of the Wardens completely shifted the tactical reality of the 13 Dimensions. The Gods were no longer the front-line enemy. The enemy was now the neighbor who sold out the rebellion for a crown, a long lifespan, and the protection of the Gods. This system guaranteed that for the next several millennia, the mortal races would focus their hatred almost entirely on each other.
