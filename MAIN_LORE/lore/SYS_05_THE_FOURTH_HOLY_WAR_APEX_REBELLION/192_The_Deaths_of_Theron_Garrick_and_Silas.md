 57_The_Deaths_of_Theron_Garrick_and_Silas

 Classification: Commander Eliminations / Punitive Torture | Status: Deceased | Primary Actor: Zenithos

---

 I. The Collapse of the Line

The extraction of the Hybrid King by Gabriel's first-generation Seraphim (see: SYS_05/55_The_Treachery_of_the_Nameless_One.md) resulted in the immediate, catastrophic failure of the mortal coalition’s Aetheric synergies. 

Trapped deep within the 12th Dimension, the supreme commanders of the rebellion Theron (Tactician of Entropy), Garrick (Vanguard of Mass), and Silas (Sentinel of Resonance) rapidly realized that a coordinated retreat was mathematically impossible. Gabriel had sealed the 12th Gate behind them and began flooding the courtyard with absolute-zero thermal strikes.

The commanders did not attempt to negotiate or surrender. Silas was completely deaf and bleeding heavily from the Acoustic Breach. Garrick’s internal skeletal structure was violently failing due to prolonged gravitational overload. Theron’s biology was necrotic from extended exposure to his own entropy fields. 

Under Theron's final tactical directive, the three commanders intentionally collapsed their remaining defensive perimeters, clustering their surviving elite squads into a final, suicidal rear-guard action. Their explicit goal was to buy enough time for the lower echelons of the mortal army to scramble down the dimensional transit gates before Gabriel could initialize an orbital bombardment. 

They held the courtyard for three hours against forty thousand Seraphim. They were not defeated by tactics; they simply ran out of Aether.

---

 II. The Capture and the Sentence

Zenithos demanded that the three commanders be taken alive. Gabriel lost two thousand Seraphim attempting to safely subdue Garrick alone, finally resorting to massive localized atmospheric suffocation to render the Vanguard unconscious. 

When the three commanders were brought before Zenithos in the central throne room, they were physically broken. But Zenithos recognized that simply executing them would quickly elevate them to martyrdom. If the mortal races remembered them as heroes who died cleanly in battle, the rebellion’s ideology would survive the defeat. 

Zenithos intended to turn their deaths into a cosmological warning. Because Theron, Garrick, and Silas had audacity to weaponize the fundamental laws of the universe (Entropy, Gravity, and Resonance) against the Gods, Zenithos explicitly decided to use those exact same laws to execute them.

---

 III. The Punitive Executions

 The Execution of Garrick (The Reversal of Mass)
Garrick had spent fourteen years crushing Seraphim using hyper-dense gravitational momentum. Zenithos utilized Gabriel’s architecture to construct a completely localized, inverted Gravity Engine around Garrick. 

Rather than crushing him, Zenithos slowly, molecule by molecule, inverted the localized gravity acting on Garrick’s body. The Vanguard commander was subjected to an impossible outward pressure. For three days, Garrick felt his own bones desperately trying to tear themselves out of his skin, forced to endure the agonizing, microscopic dispersal of his own density. His execution concluded when his Aetheric anchor finally failed, causing his body to violently violently explode outward in a zero-gravity vacuum.

 The Execution of Silas (The Endless Echo)
Silas had utilized the physics of sound to shatter the absolute defenses of the Apex. Zenithos, offended by the corruption of the celestial frequency, ordered Silas locked inside an Aetherically sealed, flawless resonant chamber. 

Because Silas was already physically deaf, Zenithos could not rely on standard Acoustic torture. Instead, the Monarch initiated a telepathic Resonance wave (see: SYS_02/16_Acoustic_Resonance_Magic.md), directly vibrating Silas’s internal spinal fluid and brain matter. Zenithos harvested the dying screams of the Elven choirs slaughtered in the courtyard and looped them directly into Silas’s consciousness at a hyper-accelerated frequency. Silas’s internal organs vibrated into a liquefied state within minutes, an execution of absolute, silent, localized horror.

 The Execution of Theron (The Absolute Order)
Theron had weaponized chaos and algorithmic blindness against Gabriel’s perfect logic. Zenithos viewed Theron as the most intellectually dangerous of the three. 

To execute the Tactician of Entropy, Zenithos utilized absolute Stasis. Theron was stripped of all Aetheric output and locked into a perfectly ordered, rigid temporal loop field. Zenithos forced Theron’s ruined, necrotic body into a state of flawless equilibrium so rigidly ordered that Theron could not move his lungs to breathe or his heart to beat, yet his consciousness remained fully active. Theron was suffocated purely by the violent imposition of perfect structural logic, denied the very disorder that had kept him alive.

---

 IV. The Aftermath

The screams and telemetry data of the executions were broadcasted continuously across Gabriel’s newly stabilized Panopticon network, flooding the lower dimensions. Zenithos ensured that every retreating mortal soldier knew exactly what happened to the men who led them into heaven.

The deaths of the commanders finalized the defeat of the Apex Rebellion. The mortal lines broke entirely, fleeing into the deep peripheries of the lower dimensions to hide from the incoming Seraphic Crusade. The leaders were dead, the King was a battery, and the Gods had firmly retaken the universe.

 Archivist Note: The Broken Trinity
Zenithos thought he was being poetic. He thought executing them with their own specialties was a display of ultimate theological dominance. But you have to listen closely to the retrieved telemetry records to understand the reality of what happened in the throne room. Zenithos tortured them for days, intending to break their spirits before their bodies. But none of the three commanding officers begged. Garrick tried to throw a punch even while his own density was unraveling. Silas spat blood on the floor of the resonant chamber. Theron stared Gabriel directly in the eyes until he suffocated. Zenithos wanted to prove that the Gods were invincible. All he proved was that the mortal commanders were infinitely more courageous than the beings who killed them.
